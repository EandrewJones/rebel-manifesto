Montreal- The Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, today welcomed the international civil aviation community, gathered in Montreal for the opening of the 38th session of the Assembly of the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO), which includes representatives of 191 Member Countries this year. "The ICAO Square is in Montreal. His future is here. I am proud of the fight we have led to stay in the metropolis, where it represents the heart of our aerospace industry. With more than 200 companies, 42,000 jobs and a turnover of $ 12.1 billion, Montreal is undoubtedly the world capital of civil aviation, "said Pauline Marois. 
Last spring, the Quebec government and the City of Montreal, in collaboration with the federal government, had made great efforts to keep in the metropolit the head office of ICAO. The presence of ICAO in Montreal generates more than 1000 direct and indirect jobs, and the economic benefits of $ 101 million a year. It is the largest international organization in Quebec.
