Montréal- On the occasion of the launch of the activities surrounding the 50th anniversary of the Cinémathèque Québécoise, the Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, inaugurated the permanent exhibition and illusions, the magic of special effects, financed from $ 500,000 by the department. 
"With this contribution, the Government of Quebec participates tangibly at the 50th anniversary of the Cinémathèque Québécoise to highlight the invaluable contribution of this institution to the preservation and dissemination of our audiovisual heritage since its foundation. By exploring the fascinating world of image magicians, the new permanent exhibition highlights Quebec's know-how in creating special effects, which will fill visitor of all ages, "said Minister Kotto. 
Exposse and illusions, the magic of special effects, which will open the public as of April 19, focuses on the creation of audiovisual illusions, by tricking and special effects, in the fields of cinema, television and television. new media. It is addressed to all audiences but particularly challenges young people, naturally worn towards inventive technologies. It proposes a participatory and playful approach that integrates interactive devices for experimenting with certain effects. 
Founded on April 18, 1963, the Cinémathèque Québécoise is a mandate to collect, to conserve and disseminate the national cinematographic and television heritage as well as any related material, such as scenarios, photos, press files and posters. The organization is in particular the manager of the legal deposit and the media library Guy-L. "I wish to greet those who, by their fervor, contributed to the success and reputation of the Cinémathèque, which, after 50 years of activities, and despite the difficulties, its essential mission of safeguarding and memory", has Added Minister Kotto.
