Saarland- The Deputy Prime Minister, Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food and Minister responsible for the Abitibi-Témiscamingue Region, Mr. François Gendron, and the member for Rouyn-Noranda-Témiscamingue, Mr. Gilles Chapadeau, are pleased to announce today the creation of the Abitibi-Témiscamingue and Northern Quebec Land Drainage Support Program. The purpose of this program, with a total value of $ 3 million, is to support agricultural enterprises in the regions referred to in the realization of drainage work to improve crop yields and to enhance land with potential. recovery. 
The drainage of agricultural land will have precise beneficial results: 
"The Abitibi-Témiscamingue Party candidates team had taken this commitment to the election campaign and had become a priority as Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food. Abitibi-Témiscamingue has a large land pool that has an interesting potential for the development of our agriculture. Currently, 24% of land is drained in Abitibi-Témiscamingue, compared to 50% throughout Quebec. The region is late and, with this measure, we will get closer to the Quebec average, "said Gendron. 
The new program will be in effect for the next three years. Agricultural enterprises will be able to receive up to $ 50,000 for drainage work and financial assistance that they can take advantage will cover up to 50% of eligible expenses related to the measures they have adopted. 
"The Abitibi-Témiscamingue is a still young region whose agricultural potential deserves to be used. The bioalimentary industry already plays a considerable role in the regional economy. In 2011, some 8,500 direct jobs were attached to it, which equates to 12% of all jobs in the region, "concluded Mr. Chapadeau. 
For more information on the Abitibi-Témiscamingue and Northern Quebec Land Drainage Support Program, companies are invited to communicate with the Department's Regional Directorate.
