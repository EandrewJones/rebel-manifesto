Quebec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, and the Minister of Employment and Social Solidarity, Minister of Labor, Minister responsible for the Status of Women and Minister responsible for the Capitale-Nationale Region, Agnès Maltese announce a $ 201,700 agreement agreement for the restoration of Augustine-Plamondon House, located in Saint-Raymond de Portneuf. 
Cited under the Cultural Heritage Act, since August 1, 2011, the Augustine-Plamondon house was built between 1907 and 1912, by the explorer and surveyor Joseph Bureau, a grandfather of Augustine Plamondon, who was The first music teacher of the famous parchier Luc Plamondon. The latter acquired the house in 2010 and donated the Plamondon Foundation to support the realization of the house restoration and setting up house, which will then become a place of creation, cultural dissemination and training. For artists. 
"In the heart of the municipality and history of Saint-Raymond, the Augustine-Plamondon house saw Luc Plamondon, one of Quebec's most famous parchments and the entire Francophonie. The agreement of principle announced today will eventually allow this house to perpetuate its vocation of place of creation, dissemination and training. It will become a cultural crossroads for the entire region, "said Minister Kotto. 
"The Portneuf region is rich in culture and artistic life. The preservation of its heritage is of national importance. It is all Quebec that will benefit from Luc Plamondon plans to deposit his personal archives, "continued Mrs Maltais. 
Let us mention that this project is supported in components of the Québec Cultural Heritage Fund. The municipality of Saint-Raymond has also committed financially in the project, and the Foundation has also set up a vast popular funding campaign to complete the financial assembly necessary for the realization of the project.
