Quebec- "Today, the Parti Québécois complies with another commitment with the adoption unanimously at the National Assembly of Law No. 13, which will allow nearly 450,000 young people to vote in their educational institution. This is another integrity measure in addition to the new Political Party Financing Act, the fixed-date election bill and the bill that abolished premiums for deputies that resign in progress. mandate. The participation of young voters is essential to the democratic health of Quebec, for today and for the coming years. As Democrats, we have the responsibility to act so that Quebeckers from all generations participate in the electoral process, "said the minister responsible for democratic institutions and citizen participation, Bernard Drainville. 
The adoption of Bill 13 will allow students to vote in vocational training centers as well as in college educational and university education institutions. "Students will be able to vote on the spot, in their training or teaching institution, for a period of four days at the end of the election period. They will be able to vote at a distance so that their vote is counted in the riding of their home. Thus, students who have to leave their region to study will be able to continue to vote without having to move on polling day, "said Bernard Drainville. 
"By passing Bill 13, we send a message to young Quebeckers:" Your ideas, your aspirations and your dreams are important to us. Make yourself heard and be part of your future. We are getting closer to the polls of you; get closer to your democracy. " , "Added the minister.
