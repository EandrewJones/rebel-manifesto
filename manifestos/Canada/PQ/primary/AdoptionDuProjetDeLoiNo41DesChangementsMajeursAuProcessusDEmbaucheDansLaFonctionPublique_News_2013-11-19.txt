Québec- Minister responsible for the Government Administration and President of the Treasury Board, Mr. Stéphane Bédard, is proud to announce the adoption unanimously by the National Assembly, law No. 41, An Act to amend the Public Service Act primarily in terms of job staffing. 
"In the current context where the public service faces many challenges in human resource management, this law introduces major changes to the hiring process. By modernizing and adapting our ways to make the new realities of the labor market, we will be able to increase our competitiveness as an employer and strengthen our internal expertise, "said Minister Bédard. 
While maintaining the principles of equal access to citizens, fairness and competence, the new staffing process will qualify candidates faster. In order to simplify access to public service jobs, it will no longer be necessary for the citizen to register for multiple competitions. Thus, for similar jobs, it will qualify once and its name will be entered in a bank that will meet the various labor needs of ministries and agencies. Changes will also increase the loyalty of students and trainees. 
The implementation of these changes requires the adoption of a regulation and guidelines specifying the rules and modalities for the application of the new staffing process. The announced changes will be effective in the spring of 2014. They will also be accompanied by other measures, in particular to allow candidates to apply online. 
"I am convinced that we will now have all the tools in hand to promote the attraction of the succession and ensure that we have the staff in place to guarantee public services of the highest quality, while providing sound management of resources. Resources of the state, "concluded Minister Bédard.
