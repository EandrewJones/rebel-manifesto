Quebec- The Minister of the Family, Ms. Nicole Léger, and the Minister of Health and Social Services and Minister responsible for the Estrie Region, Mr. Réjean Hébert, previously unveiled the projects that were selected following the recommendations of the regional advisory committees. For the Estrie region, these are 8 projects, for a total of 13 places. These places will be available very quickly, either from this month. 
This announcement follows the outfit, last May, a targeted project call for early childhood centers (CPE) and subsidized daycares to complete existing groups of children, while respecting The ratios provided for in the Educational Care Services Regulations in Quebec. 
The targeted project call is part of the General Project Call launched last February by the Minister of the Family for the creation of 15,000 additional reduced contribution places in order to complete the network of educational custody services at the childhood. Of these 15,000 places, a maximum of 1,000 places were reserved for this targeted project call. Last spring, following the proposal of national associations representing CEPs du Québec, Minister Nicole Léger launched this targeted call to respond quickly to the needs of parents. 
"Better meet the needs of child care families is our priority. By allowing the rapid creation of new places at $ 7, this targeted project call for complementing groups of children in CPEs and subsidized daycares paid off. And this approach is beneficial for many families across Quebec, "said Minister Nicole Léger. 
"It's a great news for the families of Estrie! With the allocation of these 13 new subsidized places, families in the region will benefit more accessible and high-quality child care, "said Minister Réjean Hébert.
