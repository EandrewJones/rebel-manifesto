Quebec- The hon. Member for Saint-Jérôme and Leader of the Official Opposition, Pierre Karl Péladeau, and the member for Hochelaga-Maisonneuve and spokesman for the Quebecois on international relations, Carole Poirier, want to express their sincere condolences And their compassion to the families and relatives of the victims of terrorist attacks perpetrated at the Splendid Hotel and Cappucino Café in Ouagadougou, during which six Quebecers lost their lives. 
"Our thoughts are with the families of the 23 victims from 18 different countries, of which 6 are Quebec. I also wish a speedy recovery to the many injured attacks. On behalf of the Parti Québécois, I also offer my condolences to the families of the victims, as well as my solidarity in Turkey, following the attack in a tourist district of Istanbul on January 12th. Quebec must continue to combat these attacks against our values of freedom and democracy. I strongly condemn these barbaric and ignoble gestures, "said Pierre Karl Péladeau. 
"These attacks are expression of insane hatred, before which no one can remain indifferent. Like many of our fellow citizens, we are shocked as a result of these abject violence that threatens our democracies. I also express my condolences to the family of the Lavallois Tahar Amer-Ouali businessman, the first Quebecer victim of the armed Islamic State abroad, who died at a terrorist attack in Jakarta, in Indonesia, last Thursday, "added Carole Poirier.
