Québec- Following the publication, by the Quebec Transportation Commission, a notice reporting the modifications requested by Orléans Express Inc.. (KEOLIS Canada) As for some of its schedules and frequencies, as well as in connection with the removal of several services, the members of the Parti Québécois sent the Commission an application for public hearings. 
"Considering the importance of the modifications requested by AutoCarirs Orléans Express Inc., the holding of public hearings in the affected areas appears to be essential in order to enable citizens and elected officials to speak on the consequences of a reduction in services and To reflect on the different alternatives that can be considered, "said the member for Vachon and the official opposition spokesman for transport, transport and maritime strategy, Martine Ouellet. 
The decrease in services offered by Autocars Orleans Express Inc. generate several direct consequences, such as a reduction in the mobility of seniors and accessibility to the Rimouski Hospital, a limitation of the parcel delivery service, the loss of about forty jobs, and could even threaten Tourism development in Gaspésie. In addition, this reduction would mainly affect students and seniors, particularly low-income those. 
"With almost non-existent railway service and overpriced air service, the bus becomes the sole and unique means of collective transportation for a large part of the inhabitants of Gaspésie and Bas-Saint-Laurent. A reduction in the service could have significant consequences for our economy and the mobility of our families and our workers, "held remind the member for Gaspé, Gaétan Lelièvre, on behalf of his colleagues in Rimouski, Harold Lebel, Matane- Matapédia, Pascal Bérubé, and Bonaventure, Sylvain Roy.
