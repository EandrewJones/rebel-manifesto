Québec- On 15 May last, a decree authorizing the transfer to the Government of Canada of the usufruct of certain lands so that they are administered in trust for the benefit of the Kahnawake Reserve was adopted by the Council of Ministers. This decree follows a commitment made by the Government of Quebec in 2008 in the context of the layout of the last section of Highway 30. 
Under the responsibility of the Ministry of Transport, the lands covered by the decree are in the motorway hold between the Kahnawake Reserve and Highway 30 in the municipalities of Sainte-Catherine, Saint-Constant and Saint-Isidore, at the Exception of a portion along the Saint-Bernard Road in Châteauguay and a plot near the city of Sainte-Catherine. 
The land area transferred to the Canadian government for the benefit of the Community is estimated at 2.18 km² (539.3 acres). 
On May 31, the government announced the creation of an interdepartmental committee to support economic development in the Corridor of Highway 30. The mandate of this committee is to develop a strategic vision to mobilize all actors for Concerted and effective actions. 
Today, it also unveils the implementation of a conciliation committee, as the RMC de Roussillon claimed and the municipalities concerned. This committee, which will bring together the Ministry of Transport, the Ministry of Municipal Affairs, Regions and the Occupancy of the Territory, the Ministry of Public Safety and the Aboriginal Affairs Secretariat, will make it possible to deploy cooperation mechanisms between the parties and will ensure a harmonious development of the region. 
"The municipalities told us about their desire to see such a committee being created, and we want to guarantee them the full and complete collaboration of the government. The Conciliation Committee will be a meeting place, exchange and assessment of implications for all communities, "concluded Minister Gaudreault.
