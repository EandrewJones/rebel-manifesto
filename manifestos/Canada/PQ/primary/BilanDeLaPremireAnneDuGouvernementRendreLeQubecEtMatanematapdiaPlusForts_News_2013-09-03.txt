At MCI, September 3, 2013 - Pauline Marois became the first woman elected Prime Minister of Quebec on September 4, 2012. The first year of mandate of his government is marked by listening, courage and determination. Today, the Quebec government has again a way to act so that Quebeckers find their pride. Priorities are clear: the economy and job creation, solidarity and help with those who are in need, and the pride of living together. Thus, the Parti Québécois government will take all actions to make Quebec stronger. 
It is clear to the minister and deputy of Matane-Matapédia, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, that his riding is better because Quebec is better. For a year, more than fifty advertisements have been made by the new government in the district of Matana-Matapédia. 
"The extension of the Mont-Joli Regional Airport Track, investments made at the Métis Gardens and the Jean-Cossette Showroom, the creation of new childcare places, the realization of several drinking water projects and treatment of wastewater, the realization of a multifunctional gateway on the Matane River, the opening of a new factory innovated, the renewal of the job creation program in the forest at Bas-Saint-Laurent and the attribution From the 800 MW of wind energy are some of the most important achievements of our government that contributed strongly to the economic development of the district of Matane-Matapédia in the last year, "Bérubé recalled. 
"These investments are major for all the municipalities concerned. Most of these communities have been attending financial assistance for more than 10 years, and in some cases for more than 25 years. The previous government had completely set aside the people of Matane-Matapédia. Our government has remedied this situation by restoring integrity, balance and equity. This wind of freshness is very promising for the future of our region, "concluded Pascal Bérubé.
