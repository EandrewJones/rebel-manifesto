Québec - today announcing the details of the commitment of employment services and to "escape anyone", the Minister of Employment and Social Solidarity, Minister of Labor and Minister responsible for the Status of Women Ms. Agnès Maltais said that special attention will be paid to young providers of last resort financial assistance for the benefit period to be a transition to employment. 
In the department's commitment, youth social assistance applicants under 25 will be accompanied proactively. Employment-Québec will quickly and systematically intervene with them. They will be met from their first request for financial assistance and regular follow-up will be undertaken to support them. 
In 2012, each month, about 2,500 young people under the age of 25 applied for financial assistance to the social assistance program. Of these, two out of three grew up in a family on a social assistance program. This proportion reaches three out of four young people for providers under the age of 21. This is an important loss in human capital, especially since 700,000 jobs will be available in Quebec by 2016. 
"Our goal is to break the cycle of poverty and that is why young people will be met. Begin your adult life with social assistance is not a desirable course. I have too much confidence in our youth so as not to tempt everything so that a passage to financial assistance is only a brief course of course. We have to accompany them to resume their lives in hand, "explained Minister Maltese. 
This new way to intervene with young providers updates the assistance provided to them in order to make it coherent with the new realities of the labor market that have continued to evolve since the beginning of the application of the law. Income Security in 1989.
