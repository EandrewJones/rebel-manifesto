Quebec- The Minister of Finance and the Economy, Mr. Nicolas Marceau, today tabled a balanced budget for 2013-2014. "Quebec has finally a balanced budget after four years of deficits," he proudly declared. 
"To achieve this objective, however, are necessary, however, since we inherited an important budget impasse for the years 2013-2014 and 2014-2015. Let go of things would have been unworthy of a responsible government. We therefore ask the necessary gestures to reach the balance in 2013-14 and maintain it afterwards, "said the minister. 
"In recent weeks, the government has updated its financial framework and has positioned actions to resort $ 1.6 billion for 2012-2013. We asked departments and agencies to find solutions and initiate the necessary checks to meet the targets of expenses established last March. In the same vein, this budget provides that program spending growth will be limited to 1.8% for 2013-2014 and 2.4% for 2014-2015. However, we ensure the funding of major state missions. The health budget will continue to grow at a rate of 4.8% per year. The Education Budget will increase by 1.8% and higher education, 2.0% for 2013-14, "the minister announced. 
He pointed out that the entire government apparatus is involved in achieving a balanced budget, including agencies and special funds. "We will also require significant efforts to Crown corporations. The Society of Alcohols du Québec, Loto-Québec and Hydro-Québec will be involved. For example, Hydro-Québec will have to make gestures to improve its efficiency. It will therefore reduce its short-term operating expenses by eliminating 2,000 attrition items. This improvement in the performance of the Crown corporation will not affect consumer services. The additional efforts of Crown corporations will total $ 290 million in 2013-2014, "said the minister. 
"In addition, the Auditor General of Quebec will now be able to carry out a resource optimization verification in government businesses and their subsidiaries. It can thus ensure that Crown corporations more rigorously manage the resources that belong to all Quebeckers, "he added. 
The minister also announced gestures to reduce tax expenditures. These actions are to suspend two measures announced in the last two budgets with respect to experienced workers, and to include in the calculation of taxable income all refundable tax credits for businesses. These consistency will increase government revenue of $ 68 million in 2013-14 and $ 115 million in 2014-15. 
Finally, he indicated that the intensification of the fight against tax evasion will recover $ 80 million in 2013-14, bringing the target at $ 3,866 million. 
The minister has announced that the level of total capital investments will be set up at a maximum of $ 9.5 billion a year on average for the next five years. This decision will result in savings of $ 200 million in depreciation and interest reduction in 2014-15 and even more later. 
"We will ask banks and other financial institutions to continue and increase their contribution to fiscal balance efforts. The contribution of financial institutions, which was to end in 2014, will be increased and extended until March 31, 2019. Income from $ 80 million in 2013-2014 and $ 211 million in 2014-15 will be generated ", said the minister. 
In addition, the specific tax on the tobacco will be increased by 2 ¢ per cigarette. This increase will represent 50 ¢ per package or $ 4 per cartridge. The specific tax on alcoholic beverages will also be increased. The rise for a bottle of beer will be 3 ¢, that for a bottle of wine, 17 ¢, and that for a bottle of spirits, 26 ¢. These two increases will report respectively $ 130 million and $ 100 million a year from 2013-2014. 
"The government's efforts to balance the finances of the Quebec nation have been hampered by unilateral decisions made by the federal government for a few years. In fact, we are attending a decline of ten years, when all Quebec was mobilized to eliminate the fiscal imbalance. Decisions of 2008, with respect to the Equalization Program, and 2011, with regard to the Canadian Transfer of Health, have had and will continue to have adverse consequences for Quebec's public finances, " denounced the minister. 
He asked the federal government, on the one hand, to return to his decision to impose ceilings with the Equalization Program and, on the other hand, to gradually increase to 25%, within ten years of the year. Funding for health expenditure. The Minister also deplored the federal government's refusal to respond to a long-standing claim of Quebec, to reduce the Canadian social transfer transfer, at least, at its 1994-1995 level, taking into account. 'inflation. He noted that federal disengagement in this area is a shortfall of $ 800 million a year. 
"One of the roads that the government must follow is that of administering our public finances with integrity and a great sense of responsibility to maintain our ability to act collectively. The government's good financial health creates a climate conducive to economic development. It promotes business confidence, encourages their development and ensures the population the quality public services to which it aspires. Everyone benefits from good management of public finances, all of us and future generations, "concluded the minister.
