Quebec- The Chief Opposition Whip, Harold Lebel, accompanied by the member for Vachon and spokesperson for the Parti Québécois on transport, maritime strategy and transport electrification, Martine Ouellet, tabled today a petition signed by more than 10,000 people who request that "the National Assembly of Quebec is resolutely opposed to the passage by train, on its territory, of petroleum products dedicated to export, in particular because of the high risks for the ecosystems and public safety ".

Citizens are concerned about the Project of the Company Terminals, which provides for routing to the new oil sands from oil sands, by train, for export purposes. "This petition is the demonstration that Quebeckers are mobilizing massively to say no oil sands dedicated to export.
