Quebec- In the context of the sharp rise in e-commerce, which increases the competition from our businesses, the leader of the official opposition, Pierre Karl Péladeau, arrested Prime Minister Philippe Couillard so that his government act in the interest of the Quebec economy. 
"Our companies compete with external companies that make more and more important sales in Quebec without ensuring tax deduction. Unfair competition threatens thousands of jobs in our businesses and the government, which has imposed austerity for two years, accepts in addition to losing hundreds of millions in tax revenues without doing anything. The Liberal government has not created a single job for a year. It can not afford to leave unfair competition Cause the closure of stores, as it has already happened in several places across Quebec, "said Pierre Karl Péladeau. 
The leader of the Parti Québécois recalls that Peter Simons, credible and recognized businessman, said that in addition to disadvantage job creators in Quebec, "[O] n is made to cut [in] the education of Our children, in the financing of our parents' health care. 
In 4 years, nearly 3000 shops closed in Quebec. Customs controls are virtually non-existent, and the federal government does nothing to remedy the situation. In addition, the banking sector does not respect our laws, and Ottawa closes your eyes. 
"Since the Prime Minister does not seem to know any solution, we will offer him one, emitted in particular by the OECD. Virtually all purchases made on the Internet involve payment intermediaries to complete the transactions. Why not require visa, MasterCard and Paypal that they levy taxes on transactions from Quebec?
