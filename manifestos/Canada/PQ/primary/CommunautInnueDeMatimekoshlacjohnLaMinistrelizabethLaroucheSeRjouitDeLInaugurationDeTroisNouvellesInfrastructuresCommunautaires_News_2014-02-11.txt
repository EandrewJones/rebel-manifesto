Québec - Minister Delegate to Aboriginal Affairs welcomes the inauguration of the arena, community room and the hotel, which takes place today in the Innue Community of Matimekosh-Lac-John. The support of the Government of Quebec for these three large projects, which have created a fifteen jobs, amounts to $ 1,715,000. 
Ms. Larouche, who could not be present at the inauguration, addressed the community and guests through the band leader, Mr. Réal McKenzie: "Our government is proud to have contributed to the achievement. of these structuring projects that will have significant spin-offs for your community, both socially, educational and recreational and tourism. I hope you will take full advantage of it, and this, in large numbers! Did she signify them. 
The new hotel, which has thirty rooms and a restaurant that will also serve as a conference room, will meet the growing needs of mining companies, contractors and consultants. 
The community hall is a grouping project for community members, facilitating the deployment of activities that can gather nearly 200 people, such as receptions, cultural activities and weddings. 
The repair of the arena is, for its part, a first phase aimed at building a sports complex that can accommodate a new pool. 
The Aboriginal Affairs Secretariat has invested $ 500,000 for the Arena, $ 500,000 for the Community Room and $ 715,000 for Innu Hotel Management Inc. These amounts come from Aboriginal initiative funds I and II. The purpose of these funds are to support projects that may have significant impact on the development of Aboriginal circles in Quebec.
