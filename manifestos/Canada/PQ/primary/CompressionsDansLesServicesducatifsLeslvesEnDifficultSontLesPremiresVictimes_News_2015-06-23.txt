Quebec- The member of Pointe-Aux-Trembles and official opposition spokesperson for primary and secondary education and school perseverance, Nicole Léger, is very worried about the consequences of the liberal government's blind cuts in services students; Indeed, some parents could withdraw their children from the school, lack of adequate services to accompany them and support them in their career. 
"The situation that is reported to us this morning in the media is absolute sadness. However, it is obvious: all students, including those experiencing learning difficulties, must have access to the services they need to ensure their success. We can not afford, as a society, to sacrifice an entire generation on behalf of the achievement of the budget balance; The damage would be too big or even irreparable, and that would not increase good for the future, "said Nicole Léger. 
"The government can repeat that it makes the education of our young people one of its priorities, its gestures demonstrate the opposite; For 2015-2016, we are waiting for cuts in the order of $ 200 million in primary and secondary schools, we are talking about increasing the number of students per class without taking into account the fact that some difficulties, and the number Professionals - like speech therapists, psychologists or orthopedagogues, to name only those - will be considerably reduced. For some families, so it's a real journey from the fighter who looks forward, "continued the spokesperson. 
At the last parliamentary session, the official head of the official opposition, Pierre Karl Péladeau, as well as the member for Pointe-aux-Trembles illustrated their concerns about this by giving them a concrete name and face, which made Move the government a few times. "If I have to bring each case, one to one, to the minister to make him move, I will do it. Because what counts, at the end of the way, is that our young people are equipped to succeed, "concluded Nicole Léger.
