Quebec- "Your work, your exceptional determination and dedication have opened new horizons. Your positive influence reflects Quebeckers and Quebeckers, and exceeded our borders. You make us extremely proud. May your names and achievements stay forever in our collective memory and inspire future generations. It is in these terms that the Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, paid tribute to the recipients of the National Order of Quebec. 
This year, 33 Quebec personalities were welcomed or promoted within the National Order of Quebec: two major officers (respectively officers since 1985 and 2002), three officials and nine officers, as well as nine knitters and ten knights. 
The insignia of the National Order of Quebec were given today to 31 recipients, three of the new members could not appear at the ceremony. The singer Isabelle Boulay, named last year at the rank of Knojah, also received his badge. 
About the National Order of Quebec  Founded in 1984, the National Order of Quebec is the most prestigious recognition awarded by the Quebec state. Members of the Order are eminent personalities that have contributed to the building of a Creative, innovative and solidarity Quebec society. The order of 873 members, ie 821 Quebec personalities and 52 foreigners appointed to the ranks of great officer, officer or knight. 
The biographical notes of recipients can be found on the National Order website: www.ord-national.gouv.qc.ca.
