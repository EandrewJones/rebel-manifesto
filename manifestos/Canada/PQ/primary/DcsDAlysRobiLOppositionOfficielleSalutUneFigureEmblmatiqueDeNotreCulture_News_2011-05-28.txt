Quebec - On behalf of the Parliamentary Wing of the Parti Québécois, the Leader of the Official Opposition, Pauline Marois, and the critic of culture and communications and deputy of Drummond, Yves-François Blanchet, offer their most sincere Condolences to relatives and friends of Madame Alys Robi, as well as a whole generation of Quebeckers who have identified itself to this great lady, an iconic figure of our national culture. 
"Ms. Robi is a great Quebec star who has lived, for the better and for the worst, under the fires of the ramp and the media, until in his personal life. This energetic lady has shown our culture on the international scene at a time when few Quebeckers were illustrating outside Quebec, "said Ms. Marois. 
Mr. Blanchet, for his part, held to emphasize the importance of the career and life of Ms. Robi on the evolution of the collective identity of Quebeckers during the heart of the twentieth century: "Madame Robi was a singer. with absolutely irresistible charm and talent. She not only knew a career that made it one of the first superstars of home, but his life, his dramas, his courage and his commitments for mental health have been a model without whom a lot of our act affirmation. And pride, many of this emancipation of women in Quebec, would not have known the same magnitude. " 
"First and foremost, Quebec has just lost a sincere and charismatic woman who will often, in spite of her, incarnate a rapidly evolving society, because in his life as in his songs, she has been, and will remain, a deeply touching person, "concluded the member for Drummond.
