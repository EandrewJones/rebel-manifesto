Quebec - The hon. Member for Saint-Jérôme and Leader of the Official Opposition, Pierre Karl Péladeau, as well as the spokesman for the Parti Québécois in the field of culture and communications, Véronique Hivon, have offered their most sincere condolences to the relatives of Ms. Rita Lafontaine, died yesterday in Montreal. 
"Today, Quebec is in mourning a cultural ambassador among the most loved public. Through television, cinema and theater, this generous actress has made us live innumerable emotions. On behalf of the elected officials and the big family of the Parti Québécois, I offer all my sympathy to the spouse, family members and the many friends of Madame Rita Lafontaine, "said the official opposition. 
"It's unqualified that she was investing in the roles entrusted to her, creating any pieces of characters who have deeply touched Quebecers. On stage or on the screen, she transmitted her passion and, over the years, several awards came to testify to her immense talent. Rita Lafontaine leaves in the mourning of the generations of comedians for whom she has been an inspiration and a model, "added Véronique Hivon. 
In addition to playing in about fifteen parts of Michel Tremblay, who she was a great accomplice, Rita Lafontaine interpreted several highlights in plays, movies and flagship emissions, especially in Cormoran, the return, The great seduction and grandpa. 
"We will remember his simplicity for a long time, his humor, of his sweetness. Thank you very much, Madame Lafontaine, "concluded Pierre Karl Péladeau.
