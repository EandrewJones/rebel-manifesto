Québec- It is with sadness that the Leader of the Official Opposition, Stéphane Bédard, learned of the death of Mr. Robert Burns, who died yesterday, at the Blue Source Palliative Care House, located in Boucherville. He was 77 years old. 
"On behalf of the great family of the Parti Québécois and in my name, I offer my deepest condolences to the spouse of Mr. Burns, Lorraine, as well as his family and relatives. Robert Burns has devoted his life to the defense of workers' rights. Today we lose a man of law and a politician committed to the people of Quebec, "said Stéphane Bédard. 
Judge retiree of the Court of Quebec, allocated to the Labor Court of 1980 to 2001, Mr. Burns was one of the first seven elected members of the Quebecois Party, in 1970. MP, he held several functions in the opposition and was minister in Prime Minister René Lévesque. Renowned lawyer, he had made known in the world of work, working several years in the union community.

Robert Burns is also the father of the Political Party Financing Act. He made it adopt in 1977 as Minister of State for electoral and parliamentary reform. Considered at the time as one of the most stringent in the Western world, she was the first law to ban companies to finance a political party. 
"This Great Act has made the pride of Quebeckers. Anneless heritage of the first government of René Lévesque, it was an important step in the consolidation of political manners in Quebec and the fight against corruption, "concluded Stéphane Bédard. 
Mr. Burns' family has indicated that the details about the funeral ceremony will be communicated later.
