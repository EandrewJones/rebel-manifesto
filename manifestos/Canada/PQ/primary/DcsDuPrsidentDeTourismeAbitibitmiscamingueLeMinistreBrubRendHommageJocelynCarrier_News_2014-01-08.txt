Quebec- The Minister Delegate to Tourism, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, offers his sincere condolences to the relatives of Mr. Jocelyn Carrier, who died at the very beginning of the year and whose funeral will take place in the coming days. 
Mr. Carrier, well known to actors in the tourism industry, has been president for several years, ATR Associates du Québec (ATRAQ), an organization that includes all regional tourism associations. He occupied the post of Abitibi-Témiscamingue Tourisme President for 15 years and was still sitting on the Board of Directors of the Québec Succession Foundation. 
"Mr. Carrier's death represents a great loss for the tourism industry. For years, this man worked hard to recognize the Abitibi-Témiscamingue as a strong and attractive tourist destination in addition to all the work he has accomplished at the ATRAQ for the benefit of all regions of the Quebec. Mr. Carrier contributed to the development of our tourism industry and it was with great sadness that I learned the news of his death. I offer all my condolences to his family, his friends and his colleagues of Tourisme Abitibi-Témiscamingue, "said Minister Bérubé. 
Mr. Carrier had received the Medal of the National Assembly in 2011, thus highlighting its great contribution to Quebec's tourism development.
