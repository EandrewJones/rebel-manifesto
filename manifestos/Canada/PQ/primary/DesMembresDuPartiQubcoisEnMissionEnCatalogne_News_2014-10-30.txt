Montreal, October 30, 2014 -The Parti Québécois announces that some of its members will travel to Catalonia to observe the popular consultation on the self-determination of the region, which will be held next November 9. 
The delegation will be chaired by Daniel Turp, former member of the Parti Québécois and professor of international and constitutional law at the University of Montreal, currently a member of the Quebec's International Relations Committee. Will also be part of the Delegation Mathieu Désilets, militant from the National Committee of Young People (CNJPQ), Jean-Thomas Grantham, a member of the Executive of the Québécois Party of Taschereau, Patrice Labonté, representative young people in Outremont, as well as Other activists and activists. The mission will take place from 3 to 10 November 2014. 
"Several meetings are planned with representatives of the various movements and political parties as well as with personalities of civil society. We wish to observe the conduct of the November 9 consultation process, and establish contact with parties and independence movements that are at the origin, "said Daniel Turp. 
The CNJPQ has a call for nominations for the selection of the young activist who will join the delegation. "We received thirty applications, all of great quality. It is very encouraging to see the interest of young people for international relations. This is a unique opportunity to acquire internationally militant experience, to become familiar with the organization of an advisory campaign, to understand the social, economic and political dynamics of Catalan mobilization, and Establish sustainable links with Catalan activists and leaders, "said Leo Bureau-Blouin, president of the CNJPQ Executive. 
Transportation and accommodation costs will be the responsibility of the mission participants. The CNJPQ will pay for the cost of the young person's plane ticket.
