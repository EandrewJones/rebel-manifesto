Quebec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, congratulates the directors Jean-Marc Vallée and Denis Villeneuve for the appointment of their respective works at the 2014 edition of Oscars, event that rewards the excellence of the world's cinema productions . 
"To be nominated to the Oscars is to take a giant step in recognition of his talent. I warmly congratulate Jean-Marc Vallée and Denis Villeneuve who, by illustrating, deserve all our admiration and see their career take a major step. By the time the two filmmakers will tread the red carpet, it's all Quebec cinema that will work with them, "the minister said. 
Director Jean-Marc Vallée gets a total of six nominations for his movie Dallas Buyers Club. It is nominated among others for the feature of the year, the most prestigious category. It is a first for Quebec that one of its filmmakers is in the running for this price, especially since the film was made with a small budget. This high-acting challenge on the talent of Jean-Marc Vallée to lead his team and make his project a high quality work, comparable to the great American productions. 
Minister Kotto also welcomes the director Denis Villeneuve whose film Prisoners obtains nomination in the best direction photo category. Remember that it is a second participation in the Oscars for the filmmaker who has been nominated in 2011, in the category of the best foreign-language film, for its fires fire. 
The 86th Oscars Ceremony will take place on Sunday, March 2, at Dolby Theater, Hollywood. It will be broadcast live in 225 countries.
