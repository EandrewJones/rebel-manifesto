Sherbrooke- As a result of the National Council of the Parti Québécois, the leader of the Parti Québécois, Pierre Karl Péladeau, wanted to make the following accuracy to avoid misinterpretation. 
"The dialogue that the Parti Québécois wishes to maintain with the First Nations and the Inuit Nation, after many achievements, in particular by René Lévesque, and the agreements made, such as the peace of the brave and the James Bay Convention, will continue. in openness and common enrichment. Through this dialogue, of nation to nation, we are confident of new mutually beneficial arrangements for all the nations concerned in respect of the integrity of the territory, "said Pierre Karl Péladeau.
