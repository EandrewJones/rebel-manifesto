Quebec- The most recent job data, published this morning by Statistics Canada, show that the unemployment rate in Quebec decreased by 0.3 percentage points in August to settle under the 8% mark (7, 9%). It should also be noted that some 21 200 new full-time jobs in Quebec last month. 
"Although the situation remains stable, you have to be optimistic since since the beginning of the year, employment has had a good progression. The Quebec labor market demonstrates that the jobs created in Quebec are full-time. Indeed, from January to August 2013, employment increased by 62,200, including 52,300 full-time, compared to the same period in 2012, "said Minister of Employment and Social Solidarity, Minister Labor and Minister responsible for the Status of Women, Agnès Maltais. 
Note that in 2012-2013 and 2013-2014, the Quebec government invests more than $ 22 billion in infrastructure, a level never reached before. Recall that the GDP growth forecast is 1.3% in 2013, and that, to date, it reaches 1.4% from 2012. In addition, the employment situation is greater than what has In the November 2012 budget, and the career prospects are favorable for the next few years, including 140,000 jobs to be filled annually in Quebec. 
In addition, the work market of the metropolitan area has experienced an appreciable embellish over the past year, which has boosted the Quebec economy. In the last twelve months, it was created 28,200 jobs in Montreal, mainly in the service sector. 
The Ministry of Employment and Social Solidarity, through Emploi-Québec, acts both as a producer, user and national and regional data broadcaster in the labor market. In particular, it produces Lebulletin in the labor market in Quebec that reports monthly of the evolution of employment, the labor force and unemployment in addition to identifying information by region, by sector of activity and by profession, as well as comparisons with Canada and Ontario. This will be available at the end of the day on the website of Employment-Québec at the following address: www.emploicbec.gouv.qc.ca/imt/emploi-au-quebec.asp.
