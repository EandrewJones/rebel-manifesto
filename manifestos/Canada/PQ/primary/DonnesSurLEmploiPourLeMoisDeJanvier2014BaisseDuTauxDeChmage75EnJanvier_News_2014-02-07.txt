Québec- The latest job data, published this morning by Statistics Canada, are part of a relatively positive trend noticed in Quebec in recent months. Indeed, the unemployment rate improved by 0.2 percentage points in January compared to December 2013 to 7.5%. In addition, the employment rate has increased slightly, with 60.4%. The level of employment is always among the best ever reached. 
"Last month, 11,900 Quebecers who were unemployed found a job. This allowed the labor market to experience a gain of 12,700 jobs in January. In this regard, I see that the number of full-time jobs is stable. Also note that the finance, insurance, real estate and rental sectors start the year on a good rating with an increase of 14,00 jobs, "said the Minister of Employment and the Social Solidarity, Minister of Labor and Minister responsible for the Status of Women, Agnès Maltais. 
"Our government's priority is employment. All our action revolves around the economic priority employment priority, which aims to support creative jobs and wealth projects in all regions of Quebec. In the third year, it will create the creation of 43,500 additional jobs, for a total of 115,000. Investing in employment is investing in Quebec, "said the Minister of Finance and the Economy, Mr. Nicolas Marceau. 
The Ministry of Employment and Social Solidarity, through Emploi-Québec, acts both as a producer, user and national and regional data broadcaster in the labor market. In particular, it produces the Labor Market Bulletin in Quebec, which reports monthly of the evolution of employment, the labor force and unemployment in addition to identifying information by region, by sector of activity and by profession, as well as comparisons with Canada and Ontario. This will be available at the end of the day on the website of Employment-Québec at the following address: www.emploicbec.gouv.qc.ca/imt/emploi-au-quebec.asp.
