Québec- The most recent job data, published this morning by Statistics Canada, demonstrate that the change in employment in Quebec follows the Canadian trend for the month of July by experiencing a decrease. The unemployment rate in Quebec is 8.2%. 
However, it should be noted between July 2012 and July 2013, employment increased by 1.2%, a rising comparable to the Canadian average. In the first seven months of the year, employment increased by 70,400, including 57,400 full-time compared to the same period for 2012. 
"In Quebec, the employment and activity rates of the population aged 15 to 64 remain higher than the previous annual vertices observed. The medium and long-term perspectives remain positive because it is expected that 700,000 jobs will be available by 2016. That's why the Quebec government is working tirelessly to support people who want to integrate the labor market, "said The Minister of Employment and Social Solidarity, Minister of Labor and Minister responsible for the Status of Women, Agnès Maltais.  In the Montreal Census Metropolitan Area (CMA), the employment situation has maintained. For 12 months, it has been created by 62,100 jobs and the unemployment rate has risen at 8.3%. 
The Ministry of Employment and Social Solidarity, through Emploi-Québec, acts both as a producer, user and national and regional data broadcaster in the labor market. In particular, it produces Lebulletin in the labor market in Quebec that reports monthly of the evolution of employment, the labor force and unemployment in addition to identifying information by region, by sector of activity and by profession, as well as comparisons with Canada and Ontario. This will be available at the end of the day on the website of Employment-Québec at the following address: www.emploicbec.gouv.qc.ca/imt/emploi-au-quebec.asp.
