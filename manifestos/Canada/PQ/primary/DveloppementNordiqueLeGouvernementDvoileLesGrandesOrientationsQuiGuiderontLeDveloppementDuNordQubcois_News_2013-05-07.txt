Chibougamau - Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, and the Minister of Natural Resources, Martine Ouellet, today announced, in Chibougamau, the major government guidelines for Nordic development. On this occasion, they were accompanied by the Minister Delegate to Canadian Intergovernmental Affairs, Canadian Francophonie and Sovereignist Governance and Minister responsible for the North-du-Qu√©bec Region, Alexander Cloutier, Minister of Transport and Minister of Municipal Affairs, Regions and occupation of the territory, Sylvain Gaudreault, the Minister Delegate to Aboriginal Affairs, Elizabeth Larouche, and the Parliamentary Assistant of the Minister of Natural Resources and MP Ungava, Luc Ferland. 
"We want to develop the North in a responsible way to maximize the benefits for local communities and for all Quebeckers. To do this, we announce that the new Nordic Development Fund will allow us to invest more than $ 800 million in public infrastructure over the next five years. We will propose a new infrastructure funding framework for Northern Projects, we will put forward concrete and innovative solutions to ensure the well-being and development of communities and, finally, we will ensure a harmonious and respectful development of the 'environment. The next Nordic Development Strategy will build on solid foundation, "said Prime Minister Pauline Marois.
