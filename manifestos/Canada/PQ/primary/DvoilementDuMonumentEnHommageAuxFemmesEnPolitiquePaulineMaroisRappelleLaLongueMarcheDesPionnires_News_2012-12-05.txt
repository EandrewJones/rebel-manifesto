Quebec- The Quebec Prime Minister Pauline Marois, today participated in the unveiling of a monument in tribute to women in politics. "I was 12 when the first woman entered Parliament. I will always remember this moment when our society opened to a real democracy by considering men and women on an equal footing. It took, to get there, years of struggle as well as the determination and strength of character of the pioneers. Quebec must remember their contribution, "Ms. Marois said. 
The monument emphasizes the considerable contribution of four women to Quebec political life: Idola Saint-Jean, Marie Lacoste Gérin-Lajoie, Thérèse Casgrain and Marie-Claire Kirkland. Recall that the first three have militals for the right to vote and the eligibility of women before the law adoption by the Legislative Assembly of Quebec, on April 25, 1940. Ms. Kirkland was, for her, the first woman to be elected to the Legislative Assembly, about twenty years later. It must be adopted in 1964 of the law on the legal capacity of married women. 
"It is to these women, and to all those who accompanied them in their long march, that we owe the progress of women, in the political sphere mainly, but also in the other sectors of activity and influence . They were a source of inspiration for me, and I hope they will also be for future generations, "continued the premier. 
The National Assembly, the National Capital Commission of Quebec, Quebec City and the Women's Status Board have joined forcing the erection of this monument. They entrusted the implementation of the Quebec sculptor Jules Lasalle, who is also the author of the statue of Prime Minister Robert Bourassa, erected in 2006 on the parliamentary hill. This initiative is part of the 70th anniversary of the voting and eligibility of women in Quebec, underlined last year. The monument is installed on the south facade of the Parliament Hotel Building, overlooking the Grande Alley in Quebec City.
