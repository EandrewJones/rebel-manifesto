Quebec- The hon. Member for Bonaventure and Forest Opposition Spokesman, Sylvain Roy, urges the Liberal government not to slash the budgets of the Chief Forestier's Office, Cups that once again hit Quebec regions full force. 
"After attacking last week, last week, in the academic research budgets in the forest with the abolition of the V Partnership Research Program, as well as the abolition of the program of Support for forestry research is that the government attacks the Chief Forestier office. It's 15 jobs that will be cut off. Minister Laurent Lessard talks about a relocation of employees, but in Quebec City, at the expense of the regions, "said Sylvain Roy. 
Loss of expertise 
In the long term, the hon. Member fears that forest planning suffers from austerity measures from the Liberal government. 
"The data produced by the experts of the Chief Forestier's Office are essential for the future of the Quebec forest. It is thanks to their work that citizens have a clear portrait of the state of the forest in Quebec and that decision-makers can put in place the best possible measures to ensure the sustainability of the sector, "said Sylvain Roy. 
The member is also worried about the loss of good jobs in the region. "Not only are these cups are bad for the future of the forest - there is an important expertise, which all the citizens of Quebec suffer - they are also dramatic for jobs in forest communities. The government must take responsibility and maintain the Forestier's budget, "he concluded.
