Montreal? The Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, and the Minister of Municipal Affairs, Regions and Occupation of the Territory and Minister of Transport, Sylvain Gaudreault, today announced the addition of $ 20,213,782 To build a route to Salluit and to provide municipal services to 500 dwellings in the Nunavik territory. 
"The consequences of climate change and soil instability in Nunavik, particularly in the village of Salluit, bring the Government of Quebec to make major investments to ensure the implementation of measures that will concretely help affected populations". , argued the premier. 
The main extent that this capital envelope includes is the construction of a municipal road leading to a new sector, south of the village of Salluit. Access to this location is essential for the implementation of the intervention scenario aimed at promoting development on construction land. 
The other measure, provided for in the 2012-2013 budget, aims to serve 500 new drinking water housing, in wastewater treatment and garbage collection. To do this, 11,800 $ 800 will be invested in the Nunavik territory for municipal infrastructure and equipment. 
"Due to the considerable issues at the heart of this case and investments deemed essential for the population of Salluit, the Government of Quebec had to intervene to ensure a well-ordered development on appropriate soils. The goal is of course to improve the quality of life of communities living on the territory of Nunavik, "said Minister Gaudreault. 
Recall that on April 29, 2011, the Kativik Regional Authority and the Government of Quebec signed a five-year agreement of $ 82,300,000 to provide the 14 Nordic villages of infrastructure and equipment that improves municipal drinking water services. Wastewater, garbage collection and roads.
