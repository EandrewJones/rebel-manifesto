Acton - On behalf of the Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, the member for Johnson and Minister of Sustainable Development, Environment, Wildlife and Parks, Yves-François Blanchet, announces the allocation of a Aid of $ 77,000, three years, Acton's Regional County Municipality (MRC), for the implementation of the first cultural development agreement between the MRC and the Ministry of Culture and Communications. 
"This first cultural development agreement will enhance the full artistic and heritage potential of the Acton community, with respect for its cultural peculiarities and aspirations," said Minister Kotto. 
"This first cultural development agreement is a reflection of a very large local mobilization. The municipality of Upton, the city of Acton Vale and the MRC of Acton have joined their efforts and convinced the Caisses Populaires Desjardins de Saint-Théodore d'Acton, Saint-Nazaire d'Acton, Acton Vale and From the Black River to invest in the well-being of their community. Because this is that it is ultimately: improving the quality of life of the population and promote its sense of belonging, "said Minister Blanchet. 
The agreement aims at achieving five major objectives, promoting, supporting and developing cultural tourism; Raise awareness among the patrimony and history of the region by projects aimed at heritage knowledge and enhancement; promote the achievements of artists, organizations and cultural stakeholders to make them aware of the population; Foster the cultural commitment of the population to ensure artistic succession and to anchor the sense of belonging to the community; And, finally, preserve and develop cultural organizations and achievements. 
The financial assistance paid comes from the programAide to the partners-share initiatives of the Ministry of Culture and Communications. It represents 46% of the total amount of the Triennial Agreement of $ 169,000. Caisses populaires and municipal partners pay $ 92,000 for this agreement.
