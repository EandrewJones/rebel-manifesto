Quebec, on August 18, 2016 -The Member for Taschereau and spokesperson for the Partiécois on secularism, Agnès Maltese, reminds the government that there is an emergency to take action to counter the phenomenon of young people trained here in Quebec. to fundamentalism and religious radicalism. 
"The examples of young Quebecers gone abroad accumulate. It is now clear that recruiters identified here, in Quebec, to inculcate radicalism. While the Liberal government ignores the threat and that the CAQ is distracted by the port of Burkini and promotes the introduction of a beaches police, we must act, and quickly. We must counteract this much more real and direct threat that takes shape: the fundamentalist speeches that circulate and the radical preachers working in Montreal and who embroidates our young people, "said Agnès Maltais. 
"These individuals reject our values of secularism, openness and equality between women and men, without the government coming to put them back to order. This softness promotes the rise of religious fundamentalism, which results in the multiplication of controversy, such as the one surrounding the port of Burkini. This is a consequence that masks the real issue: the work of laying the secularism that these radicalization agents causing the apology of a rigorous interpretation of Islam, "continued the spokesperson. 
"While the Prime Minister and the Liberal government attack freedom of expression and accuse the lack of openness of Western societies to be the cause of Islamist terrorism, we learn this morning that two of our young people were victims , right here in Montreal, of radical preachers. Religious fundamentalism poses important integration and safety issues. We must look at the phenomenon, know what is happening on the ground. It must be done for our young people. The Liberal government promised actions, but we are still waiting, "concluded the spokesperson.
