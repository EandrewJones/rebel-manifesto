Quebec, on February 12, 2020 - As committed to it at the pre-parliamentary wing of the Parti Québécois parliamentary wing, the member for Matane-Matapédia, parliamentary leader and spokesman for the Quebecois in French language , Pascal Bérubé, introduced today in the House Bill 591, An Act to amend the Charter of the French Language to specify under what circumstances an employer may require access to a job or a position the knowledge. a language other than the official language. 
IN SHORT 
"The bill we filed this morning provides a solution to a problem that was increasingly increasing: the systematic requirement of English to hiring. It aims to modify the Charter of the French language to specify under what circumstances an employer may require the knowledge of a language other than French, the only official language in Quebec, "said Pascal Bérubé. 
Indeed, the bill provides that in order to demand a specific knowledge or level of knowledge of a language other than French, the employer must demonstrate two things: that participation in the usual activities related to the position, on the one hand. , and that the accomplishment of the task, on the other hand, require such knowledge. "Some employers had become accustomed to requiring English because his knowledge could be useful. From now on, they will only be able to do so if this knowledge is essential, indispensable, "summed up the parliamentary leader of the Parti Québécois. 
"When it comes to asserting the right of Quebeckers to work in French, when it comes to promoting our common language and reinforcing law 101, the Parti Québécois always responds. In the interest of all Quebeckers, we invite the minister responsible for the French language, Simon Jolin-Barrette, to quickly call the study of Bill No. 591, "concluded Pascal Bérubé.
