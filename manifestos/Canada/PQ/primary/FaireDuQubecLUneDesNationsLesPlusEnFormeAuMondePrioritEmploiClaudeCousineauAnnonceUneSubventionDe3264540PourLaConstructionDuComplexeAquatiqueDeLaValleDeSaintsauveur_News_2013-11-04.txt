Saint-Sauveur- In the context of economic lapolitics: employment priority, the member for Bertrand and Vice-President of the National Assembly, Claude Cousineau, as well as the member for Labelle and Government Manager of the Laurentian Region, Mr. Sylvain Pagé, today announced, on behalf of the Minister of Education, Recreation and Sport, Marie Malavoy, a financial assistance of $ 3,264,540.24 for the construction of the Aquatic Complex of the Saint Valley -Saviour. 
The aquatic complex project includes the construction of a semi-Olympic pool (25 meters, 6 corridors, 2 stepping stones of 1 meter and 3 meters), a small pool with water games and beach, locker room and meeting rooms. 
"This project is of particular importance because it meets a significant lack of sports infrastructure in the region. It aims to promote the health of young people and then young people by giving access to a modern aquatic complex. I am pleased to announce this important grant that will directly contribute to the well-being of the population of Saint-Sauveur and the surrounding area, "said Cousineau, Member of Parliament for Bertrand. 
This project has been analyzed and selected according to the criteria of the sports and recreational facility support program - Phase II of the Fund for the Development of Sport and Physical Activity, which provides maximum financial assistance equivalent to half of the Eligible costs. 
"In the whole Quebec, 84 projects for the construction, renovation, development and setting up to sports and recreation standards will materialize through investments totaling $ 130 million. These amounts come from a $ 50 million envelope granted under the second component of the sports and recreational infrastructure support program - Phase II of the Ministry of Education, Recreation and Sport. At this envelope adds an additional $ 80 million to support new projects, "said Pagé, Government Manager of the Laurentides region. 
It should be noted that these investments are planned as part of the Government of Quebec's economic policy, employment priority, recently presented by the Prime Minister and the Minister of Finance and the Economy, Mr. Nicolas Marceau. This policy is intended to create jobs and wealth throughout Quebec, short and medium term. 
Of the $ 565 million in public infrastructure announced, in addition to investments for the modernization of sports and recreational infrastructure, some $ 401 million is devoted to making healthy educational infrastructures from healthier environments to learn. The realization of these projects should allow the creation of about 3,500 jobs. 
"For a year, we have multiplied our actions to improve our public infrastructure. We do even more today, determined to increase the level of physical activity and improve the physical condition of Quebeckers. By investing heavily in our sports and recreational infrastructures, we stimulate economic development while contributing to the health, well-being and quality of life. In my view, these are strong and responsible gestures, "Malavoy said.
