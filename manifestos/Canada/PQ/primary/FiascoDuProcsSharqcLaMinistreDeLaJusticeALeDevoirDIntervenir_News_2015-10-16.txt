Québec-Today, the PCPP confirmed that with respect to the Sharqc trial, the work of the police who have, for some, risked their lives to defeat organized crime has been purely and simply reduced to nothing. The Minister of Justice, Stéphanie Valley, who remained silent so far, owes explanations to these men and women, as well as to all Quebeckers, affirm Stéphane Bédard and Pascal Bérubé, respectively spokesman respectively of the Parti Québécois in terms of justice and public security. 
"By respect for investigators and police officers who have worked for years to accumulate sufficient evidence to bring serious charges against highly criminal individuals, the Minister of Justice must herself shed light on the events that have led In the failure of the Sharqc trial and act so that such a situation is happening again, "said Pascal Bérubé. 
"It is sad to see the first officer of justice in Quebec remain silent faced with one of the worst mess in our judicial history. It is more than time that it stops hiding, that it assumes its responsibilities, and that it explains to Quebeckers the reasons that led to this lamentable failure, "claimed Stéphane Bédard. 
Remember that the Sharqc trial is one of the biggest in Quebec's history and that's the second time that such a situation occurs. In 2011, 31 of the 156 accused had been released. "It's deplorable. It is to believe that this government has not taken lesson. And, moreover, in the current context, it would be urgent that the minister explains the reasons for reducing the number of prosecutors in Quebec, "concluded Stéphane Bédard.
