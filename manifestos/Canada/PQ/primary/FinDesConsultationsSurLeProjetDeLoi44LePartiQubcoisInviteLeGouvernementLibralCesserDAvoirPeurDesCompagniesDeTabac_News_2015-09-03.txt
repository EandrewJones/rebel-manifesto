Québec- So what are the consultations on Bill 44, An Act to strengthen the fight against smoking, the member for Rosemont and spokesperson for the prevention, Jean-François Lisée, presents New amendments that the official opposition intends to file in order to improve it. 
"Bill 44, deposited by the Government, is only part of the work needed to reduce smoking and havoc, which cost Quebeckers $ 1.6 billion annually and causes 16 deaths per day," said The member for Rosemont. 
"The Liberal government is reluctant to adopt the existing measure to reduce smoking in Australia, neutral packaging, as well as a moratorium on any new tobacco product. It seems to fear the reaction of tobacco companies, "added the member. 
In May, the Parti Québécois announced that it would submit amendments to that effect, supported by several of the 36 groups that came to testify since. However, the responsible minister gave no indication of having chimney on these issues. Today, the member for Rosemont has indicated that the official opposition intended to submit several other amendments. "The groups that participated in the public consultations are unanimous: Quebec must resume its place as a leader in the fight against smoking. It is this desire that will guide our interventions in future legislative steps, "he continued.
