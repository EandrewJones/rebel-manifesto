Quebec- The Minister of Justice and Attorney General of Quebec, Mr. Bertrand St-Arnaud, today tabled the National Assembly the report of the Advisory Committee on Accessibility to Justice. 
Expected in Laloi establishing the Justice Access Fund, this committee includes the mandate to advise the Minister of Justice on the guidelines and priorities for the financing of activities or projects centered on accessibility to justice. 
"I thank the members of the committee for their work. I agree with their recommendations to prioritize projects aimed at access to free or modest costs for our most vulnerable fellow citizens, as well as projects that encourage the use of alternative modes of prevention and settlement of disputes " , said the Minister of Justice. 
The Advisory Committee on Accessibility to Justice is chaired by Master Nancy Chamberland, representative of the Chamber of Notaries of Quebec. It is composed of Mrs. Geneviève Baril, representative of the citizens; Master Fernand side, representative of the community community; Master Geneviève Fortin, representative of the Barreau du Québec; And master Pierre-Claude Lafond, representative of the academic community. Ms. Sabrina Marino, from the Ministry of Justice, acts as Secretary of the Committee. 
The report can be consulted on the Quebec Ministry of Justice website: www.justice.gouv.qc.ca. 
In the wake of the report of the Advisory Committee, Minister Bertrand St-Arnaud officially launched the first call for projects. For the 2013-2014 fiscal year, it is a $ 500,000 budget that will be devoted to the financing of projects or activities corresponding to the priorities established by the Advisory Committee. 
The deadline for submitting a project is November 22, 2013. All information is available online: www.justice.gouv.qc.ca.
