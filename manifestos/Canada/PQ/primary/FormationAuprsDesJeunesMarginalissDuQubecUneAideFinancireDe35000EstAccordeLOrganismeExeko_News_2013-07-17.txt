Quebec- The Prime Minister and Minister responsible for Youth Records, Pauline Marois, announced the granting of a grant to the organizationkopour the deployment of two of its youth programs in the City of Montreal and in the three Aboriginal communities of Wemotaci, Kitcisakik and Kanesatake. The aid of $ 35,000 will be paid by the secretariat to youth. 
The community organization, which designs programs and projects aimed at promoting the development and inclusion of the most marginalized populations, targets the expansion of its IDACTION and TRICKSTER programs. Already touching more than 4500 beneficiaries in 9 of the 17 administrative regions of Quebec, 80% of whom are excluding young people or at risk of exclusion, Exeko wants to attach 500 more young people and expand their reach to 11 regions of Quebec. 
The help for Iceaction ($ 15,000) and Trickster ($ 20,000) will also serve to prepare an expansion throughout Quebec. Both programs target autonomy and the best participants, in addition to stimulating youth engagement in their living environment. 
"It is with pleasure that I announce this substantial help to support youth programs that want to make every young Quebecker a full citizen, regardless of his region, his interests or school skills," said the Prime Minister. 
The IDACTION program is a socio-educational program for people aged 15 to 35, marginalized or at risk of exclusion. As for Trickster, it is a program of social and cultural activities dedicated to Aboriginal youth aged 8 to 16 years. 
The Youth Secretariat is mandated to advise the government in the field of youth and to assist the Prime Minister in the exercise of his responsibilities in this regard. It ensures the coordination and monitoring of government action in this area.
