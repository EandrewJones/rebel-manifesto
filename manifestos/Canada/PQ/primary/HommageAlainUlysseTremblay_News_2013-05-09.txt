Quebec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, learned with sadness the death of the novelist, teacher, journalist, painter and playwright Alain Ulyse Tremblay. 
Multidisciplinary artist accomplished, Alain Ulyse Tremblay has long continued in parallel pictorial artistic approach and literary course. In all that he did, he impregnated his works with a sensitivity and a deep empathy for the humble, the little ones and the grades. Prolific author, he published some thirty novels and devoted himself to the successful youth literature, because the young readers appreciate his style both realistic and whimsical. 
Alain Ulyse Tremblay did not compromise the vocabulary, always employing the right word, even if it was unusual, sought. He has also been a great transmitter in writing, in culture and ethics in his profession of teacher in journalism at the University of Quebec in Montreal, which he had been practicing since 1996. His students loved his talent as an inspired pedagogue and his insistence. on the choice and weight of words. Many students learned from him to integrate into their style the concerns of humanism and justice, in the same way that he himself took care to embody the human condition in his fiction and his characters.
