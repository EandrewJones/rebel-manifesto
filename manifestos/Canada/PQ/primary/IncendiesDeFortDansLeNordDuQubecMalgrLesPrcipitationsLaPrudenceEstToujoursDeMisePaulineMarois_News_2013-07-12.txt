Quebec- The Quebec Prime Minister Pauline Marois, informs the population that widespread rainfall in the center and northern Quebec make the fire growth in these sectors should slow down today. The situation is more problematic on the North Shore where some places have received fewer rainfall. 
"There is a certain accaltimia over the next few days near James Bay and northern Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean. By mutual agreement, the partners responsible for civil security for the Nord-du-Québec region and the James Bay sector found that the situation is no longer problematic. With respect to the North Shore, the three fires that were considered unmatched yesterday north of the Manicouagan Reservoir and in the area of the Rivière-Aux-Outardes are contained. On the other hand, because of the less significant rainfall and flammability indices that could evolve, caution is being put throughout the eastern Quebec, "said the Prime Minister. 
No problem is reported on the electricity transport network in the James Bay or Eastern Quebec sector. Hydro-Québec continues to closely monitor the situation and carries out preventive work to secure its facilities. 
"We need to give some respite to those who protect Quebec communities, infrastructure and forests against these fires. That's why another group of fighters from Massachusetts today takes over and is added to the many supports received by Quebec in this struggle, "concluded the Prime Minister. 
As a result of improvements related to weather and work of teams fighting forest fires, the fire item will be based on the situation that will prevail. The collaboration of all essential remains. Remember that citizens can consult the following government website for the status of the situation and the instructions in force: www.urgencequebec.gouv.qc.ca.
