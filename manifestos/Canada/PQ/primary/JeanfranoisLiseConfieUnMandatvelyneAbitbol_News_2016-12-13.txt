Quebec, on December 13, 2016 -The Leader of the Official Opposition, Jean-François Lisée, and the Chief Party whip and spokesman for immigration and cultural communities, Carole Poirier, are proud to welcome Evelyne Abitbol as Special Chief Advisor for Diversity in the Parliamentary Wing Team. Ms. Abitbol will support Carole Poirier's mandate to intensify relationships with different communities and continue dialogue with them to better represent them. 
"Born in North Africa, Morocco, Evelyne has devoted a large part of his career to dialogue between cultures and civilizations, and has worked in international organizations working in the field of cooperation. She is known for her engagement in the cause of Raif Badawi, this blogger is a prisoner of opinion in Saudi Arabia. Honored, among others, by the Institute of Sépharade Culture of Canada for his tireless action in the dialogue between cultures, she also received the Arab Women Trophy, "said Jean-François Lisée. 
Currently Executive Director and co-founder of the RAIF Badawi Foundation for Freedom, she was in particular Vice-President, Strategies and Public Affairs at Saga, and Director, Government Relations and Public Affairs at Concordia University. 
Ensure the success of Quebeckers of various origins is a priority for the Parti Québécois. From the first days that followed his election, the official Leader of the Official Opposition announced an action in this direction, coordinated by Carole Poirier. "The arrival of EVELYNE demonstrates the importance we give by weaving, establishing and maintaining a direct dialogue with all Quebeckers. We will continue and even intensify our work in order to be ever listening and the tapping of citizens of all origins, "concluded Carole Poirier.
