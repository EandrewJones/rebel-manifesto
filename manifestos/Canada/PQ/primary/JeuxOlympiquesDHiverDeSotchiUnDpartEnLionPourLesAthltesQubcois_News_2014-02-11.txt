Quebec - Quebec Premier, Pauline Marois, emphasized the impressive performance of Quebec athletes, who have already harvested several medals at the Sochi Olympics. 
First, Saturday, Justine and Chloé Dufour-Lapointe dazzled the spectators around the world by winning the gold and silver medals for bumps, in acrobatic skiing. Then today, our athletes have added three new medals to this harvest, including another double in acrobatic ski, male this time. Mrs. Marois personally congratulated the Sisters Dufour-Lapointe, as well as the speed skater Charles Hamelin, for his third golden gold medal, and Alexander Bilodeau, who became the first acrobatic skier to defend his Olympic title successfully . 
"These Olympic Games promise to be memorable in terms of medal harvesting. Realize two doubled two days apart, it's just extraordinary. The Quebec nation withdraws an immense feeling of pride of being represented by athletes of such a caliber, "said the Prime Minister. 
The Minister of Education, Recreation and Sport, Marie Malavoy joined his voice to that of the Prime Minister, bringing out the brilliant performance of Mikaël Kingsbury (silver medal), and that of Marc-Antoine Gagnon , which escaped the third step of the podium. "These young people are models for all of us. They embody talent, constancy in the effort, and they show the way to many young people and adults in the pursuit of this ideal to which we aspire, to become one of the most in-form nations in the form. world.
