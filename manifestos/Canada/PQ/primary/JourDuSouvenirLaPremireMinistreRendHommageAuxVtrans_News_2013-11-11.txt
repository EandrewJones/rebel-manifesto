Québec- On the occasion of the commemoration day of the Armistice that ended the First World War, the Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, accompanied by Minister Agnès Maltese, the Chief Whip of the Government, Marjolate Dufour, and MEPs Jeannine Richard, Gilles Chapadeau, Sylvain Roy and Denis Trottier, went this afternoon at the foot of the Cross of Sacrifice, Quebec City. 
"We have to pay tribute to these women and men who have left family and friends to get to the battlefields. They have made many sacrifices to defend ideals that are dear to us, like democracy, freedom and peace. Let's take advantage of this day of commemoration to emphasize their courageous contribution, to think of people who are still at the forehead today and to honor the memory of those who have left their lives, "said Quebec's Prime Minister. 
To greet the bravery of all those who took part in an armed conflict over our history, the Prime Minister filed a wreath of flowers at the Cross of Sacrifice and observed a moment of recollection.
