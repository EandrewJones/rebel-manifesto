Quebec- In this Quebec week of families, taking place from 11 to 17 May, the hon. Member for Chicoutimi and Leader of the Official Opposition, Stéphane Bédard, as well as the member for Terrebonne and Family Spokesperson, Mathieu Traversy, Take advantage of the International Family Day, which will take place on May 15, to reaffirm their support and to invite the government to review the policies that concern them. 
"I want, on behalf of my colleagues from the Parti Québécois, to salute all the Fathers and Mothers of Quebec for their daily commitment. For a parent, in 2015, the challenges are numerous and demanding. Due to the cuts that the government imposes in the services for them and the increase in several tariffs, including child care, Quebec families must redouble ingenuity. However, they can count on the support of the Parti Québécois, who will continue to defend their interests in the face of this government that adds ear, "said Stéphane Bédard. 
Abundant in the same direction, Mathieu Traversy emphasized how much the theme chosen this year, family reconciliation and work, highlights the inconsistency of the government. "While all stakeholders argue for better work-family conciliation, the Government of Philippe Couillard strives to want to reduce initiatives and programs to make it easier for it. If it was logical and truly concerned about the well-being of families, instead of slashing the budgets allocated to the Work-Family Conciliation Action Plan under government policy for equality between women and men. He did it in the last budget, he would look for ways of strengthening Quebec's family policy, "he said. 
Finally, Stéphane Bédard and Mathieu Traversy invited the families of Quebec to participate in large numbers in the activities planned as part of the 20th Quebec Week of Families, an initiative of the network for a Quebec family.
