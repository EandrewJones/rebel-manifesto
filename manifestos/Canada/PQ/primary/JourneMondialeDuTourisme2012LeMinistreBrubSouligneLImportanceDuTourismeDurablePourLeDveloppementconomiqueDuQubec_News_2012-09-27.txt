Quebec- On the occasion of World Tourism Day 2012, which stands under the theme "Tourism and Sustainable Energy: Propeling Sustainable Development", the Minister Delegate to Tourism and Minister responsible for the Bas-St-Laurent Region, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, is pleased to highlight this important event. 
"The tourism industry is a great source of wealth that is advantageously comparable to several advanced sectors of the economy. With 11.4 billion tourist revenue estimated in 2011, a 2.4% contribution to Quebec's gross domestic (GDP) product, 423,000 jobs counted in tourism-related sectors, the 29,500 Quebec tourist companies are certainly in the heart. From our economic development, "said Minister Bérubé. 
"It is anticipated that global tourism growth will continue in the coming years around 3.5%, which means nearly 400 million new travelers around the world. Quebec must take advantage of this growth in a perspective of sustainable development to ensure the sustainability of our landscapes and attractions. This development must appeal to collective responsibility to protect our environment for the benefit of future generations and in the respect and well-being of local communities. In addition, the theme chosen this year concords perfectly with the orientations of our government in terms of clean and lasting energy, "added the minister. 
Under the aegis of the World Tourism Organization (UNWTO) since 1980, the main objective of World Tourism Day is to raise awareness of the international community to the importance of tourism and its social, cultural, political and economic value.
