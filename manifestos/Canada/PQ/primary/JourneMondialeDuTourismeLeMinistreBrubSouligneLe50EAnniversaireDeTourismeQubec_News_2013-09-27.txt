Quebec- On the occasion of World Tourism Day, celebrated since 1980, on the initiative of the World Tourism Organization (UNWTO), the Minister Delegate to Tourism, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, is pleased to highlight the 50th anniversary the creation of the Ministry of Tourism, Hunting and Fishing. It was in 1963 that the Hotel Act was adopted, and that Quebec intensified its advertising campaigns in the United States under the theme "The Beautiful Province". 
Since April 4, 1963, our tourist destination has traveled a long way, passing through the year of the exhibition, in 1967, where Quebec opens to the world and creates a tour of tourist commissioner in Paris. In 1979, the Ministry of Tourism officially recognizes the network of regional tourism associations (ATR) as privileged partners in the regions. In 1984, the Montreal Congress Palace is inaugurated. The same year, "Hello Quebec" becomes the trademark of tourism and will be followed in 1999 by the release of the official transactional site of the Ministry of Tourism: HelloBec.Com. In 2008, lastgion of sustainable development and promotion of international cruises on the St. Lawrence River is launched, creating six new stopovers. 
"Today, tourism is one of the leading global industries, and moreover, a growing economic sector. We must continue our efforts and work in consultation with our partners to take advantage of this growth and to provide an original tourist experience of international caliber to our visitors, "said Minister Bérubé. 
In Quebec, the tourism industry represents 2.5% of GDP, $ 12.4 billion of tourist revenue; 29,500 companies are linked to sectors associated with tourism, generating nearly 423,000 sector-related jobs and 141,000 direct jobs. Fourth Quebec export product, tourism is an economic sector rooted in all regions of Quebec.
