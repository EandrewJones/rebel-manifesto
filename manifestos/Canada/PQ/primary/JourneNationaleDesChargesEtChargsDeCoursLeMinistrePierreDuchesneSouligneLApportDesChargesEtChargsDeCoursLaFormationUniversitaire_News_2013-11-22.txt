Quebec- The Minister of Higher Education, Research, Science and Technology, Mr. Pierre Duchesne, takes advantage of the National Day of Officers and Lessons to highlight their important contributions in the student's school path. 
"Course loaders are essential actors of the university faculty. Their expertise and diverse experience contribute to quality university training. Thanks to their passion for teaching, they transmit knowledge to students to ensure a succession in all spheres of activity, "said Minister Duchesne. 
These people give the majority of classes at night and weekend. They are also very present out of urban centers, thus allowing better accessibility to training for adults and students throughout Quebec. 
"The personal and collective involvement of courses therefore contributes to the academic success of the students they form. They guarantee better accessibility to higher education for those who have the will and the ability to continue. It is important to take advantage of this day to emphasize it, "added Minister Duchesne. 
The contribution of the course loaders has been reported on the occasion of the Summit on Higher Education. Thus, 50% of reinvestment will be devoted to the hiring of teachers, courses and non-teaching staff. Part of the university reinvestment could result in more than 2,000 additional courses. 
On 22 November, the National Federation of Teachers of Quebec (FNEEQ-CSN) highlights the National Day of Loaders and Leads Officer by launching the second phase of a valuation campaign initiated in 2010. Under the theme I teach At the university and ..., this evolving campaign shows the great diversity of the profiles of the course loaders and makes it possible to publicize their professional expertise, an essential component of the success of the training mission of the universities.
