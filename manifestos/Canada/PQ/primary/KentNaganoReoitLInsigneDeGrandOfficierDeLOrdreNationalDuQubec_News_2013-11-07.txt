Quebec- The Quebec Prime Minister Pauline Marois, has given the bad officer's badge of the National Order of Quebec to the Kent Nagano conductor, at a ceremony today, at the National Assembly. 
Recognized worldwide as one of the most inspired and innovative interpreters in the opera and symphonic repertoire, Maestro Nagano became musical director of the Montreal Symphony Orchestra (OSM) in September 2006. Since then, he has infused his dynamism and offered the public of original creations impregnated with Quebec culture. He also led the OSM at national and international touring and trips. 
"You enrich the culture from here by establishing unheard of links between music and characters or events specific to Quebec society. You like Quebec and, by the high quality of OSM's benefits, you are looking to make it shit around the world. In addition, you would never hesitate to support expensive causes to Quebecers and Quebeckers. You are an artist, but also an exceptional man. It is an honor for us to welcome you within the national order and to give you our biggest mark of recognition, to the highest grade, that of great officer, "said the Prime Minister at the recipient at ceremony. 
Founded in 1984, the National Order of Quebec is the most prestigious recognition awarded by the Quebec state. Members of the Order are eminent personalities that have contributed to the building of a Creative, innovative and solidarity Quebec society. The order of 873 members, including 821 Quebec personalities and 52 foreigners appointed to the rank of grand officer, officer or knight.
