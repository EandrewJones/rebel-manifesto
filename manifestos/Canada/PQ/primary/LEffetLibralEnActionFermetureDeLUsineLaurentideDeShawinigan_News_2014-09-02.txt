Member for Bonaventure and Forest Opposition Spokesman, Sylvain Roy, the member for Saint-Jérôme and Spokesperson in Economics and SMEs, Pierre Karl Péladeau, and the member for Saint John and Job Spokesperson, Dave Turcotte, ask the Government to support the Shawinigan employees following the announcement of the closing of the Laurentide Forest Products factory resolved, located in the grandmother sector.

The member for Saint-Jérôme reiterated the request of the Parti Québécois for the Liberal government to file a clear plan for economic development and support for job creation. "The economic situation of Quebec has deteriorated since the arrival of the Liberal Party in power. All indicators in the labor market are red; He has lost 27,400 jobs since the end of March. For the government, it's time to think about a way to boost growth, stimulate job creation and support our regions and families, "said Pierre Karl Péladeau. 
Government must support affected employees 
For its part, the hon. Member for Saint John asks the Liberal government to say quickly to the population of Grandma and Mauricie what he intends to do for economic development and job creation in the region, hard hit In recent years by business closures. 
"The only action that the Liberals asked for employment, since their arrival in power, has been cutting into employment aid measures. They must clearly announce what they will do for this region and the workers who have lost their jobs today, "said Dave Turcotte.
