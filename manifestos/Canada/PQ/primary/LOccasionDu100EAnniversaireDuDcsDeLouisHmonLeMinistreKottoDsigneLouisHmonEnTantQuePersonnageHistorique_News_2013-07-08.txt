Louis Hémon, author and journalist born in France, was landed in Quebec, in 1911. A year later, he settled in the Lac-Saint-Jean region, where he undertook the drafting of his novel recognized as the More important from the soil school, Maria Chapdelaine: a story of French Canada. The book is inspired by the life of settlers established around Lake Saint-Jean. Following the death of the author, the novel was published in Montreal and was very successful. This book Quebec was shot several million copies, was the subject of 150 editions in addition to languages, inspired three cinematographic adaptations and a play. 
Louis Hémon is now designated as a historical character because he has been illustrated in Quebec and is considered a key author of Quebec literature.
