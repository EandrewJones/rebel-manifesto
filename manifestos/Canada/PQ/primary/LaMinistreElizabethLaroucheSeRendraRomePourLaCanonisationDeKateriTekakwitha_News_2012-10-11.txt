Quebec - Minister Delegate to Aboriginal Affairs, Ms. Elizabeth Larouche, will take part in the canonization of the Blessed Kateri Tekakwitha, on October 21 in Rome. Ms. Larouche will represent the Quebec government at this unique event, during which the first Native American saint will be devoted. 
Ms. Larouche said honored to take part in this event: "Kateri Tekakwitha remains a symbol for Quebec, especially for Aboriginal nations. I am very proud to represent my government at this historic moment. Â» 
Ms. Larouche will speak at the reception organized by the Quebec delegation, which will take place on Monday, October 22nd. It will also be an opportunity for it to exchange with representatives of Aboriginal communities and foreign dignitaries. 
Born in the United States, Kateri Tekakwitha (1656-1680) lived part of his life in New France. His tomb is exhibited in the city of Sainte-Catherine, close to Kahnawake. "It embodies both the cultural heritage of the First Nations who populated Quebec and our Catholic tradition," added the minister. 
Ms. Elizabeth Larouche was appointed Minister Aboriginal Affairs on 19 September 2012. She received the mandate to support Quebec's Aboriginal nations in their economic, social and cultural development and to ensure that relations between Quebec and the Aboriginal people end up at nation level.
