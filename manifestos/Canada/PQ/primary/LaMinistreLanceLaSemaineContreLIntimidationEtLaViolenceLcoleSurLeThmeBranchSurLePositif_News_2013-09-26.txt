Quebec- The Minister of Education, Recreation and Sport, Marie Malavoy, today launched the week against intimidation and violence at school, which will be held from September 30 to October 4. This year, the theme of the week will be connected to the positive, an invitation launched to young people to put into practice the "cyber appreciation" in their virtual exchanges. 
The Ministry of Education, Recreation and Sport has recently established a partnership with four organizations that have been dedicated to the fight against intimidation and violence, together with respect for diversity, the Jasmin Roy Foundation, the Pacific Institute and Tel-Youth. It should be noted, Quebec's Major Junior Hockey League has already expressed support for the countryside. Some of his players will even participate in exchanges on social networks. 
ThemeBranche on the positive expects to promote in young people a responsible and positive use of social media. To this end, many activities will be carried out in schools. Among these, include workshops on "cyber appreciation", a concept developed at the Primary School of Orleans of the School Board of the First Lordies of Quebec. Reference tools, an animation guide, the sitewww.brancheposition.gouv.qc.ca, video capsules and leaflets will also be made available to students. These new educational tools are offered free of charge to school boards. 
"We organize a week, but it should be known that outreach activities will continue in the other's 51. Students need to continue to mobilize to make cyberspace a pleasant and safe place where it is good to learn and have fun, "said the minister. 
Since the unanimous adoption of Bill 56, the school network is mobilizing. Currently, 99% of schools have a plan to combat intimidation and violence and have appointed a resource person to ensure its application. As prescribed by law, Quebec's primary and secondary schools are invited to achieve various awareness activities for citizenship.
