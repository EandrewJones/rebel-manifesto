Qu√©bec - Minister Delegate to Aboriginal Affairs, Mrs. Elizabeth Larouche, today met the Federal Minister of Aboriginal Affairs and Northern Development, Mr. Bernard Valcourt. This first meeting, which took place on the parliamentary hill in Quebec City, aimed to address several topics of importance for the First Nations and Inuit living on Quebec. 
"It is essential to work together to meet the many challenges facing Aboriginal communities in Quebec. I am satisfied with this meeting and I wish to continue in this direction, "said Minister Larouche. 
Ms. Larouche offered his full collaboration to his counterpart and recalled the importance that the federal government is investing in the issues concerning Aboriginal people in Quebec. In particular, the Minister addressed the issue of aboriginal police services, which is currently compromised as budgets from the federal government will remain the same until 2018. 
It was also discussed the cost of living in Nunavik. In this regard, Ms. Larouche wanted to discuss how the federal government, the Quebec government and the Nordic organizations will collaborate in order to tackle effectively and concerted this problem. 
In addition, the minister reminded Mr. Valcourt the importance of investing in education and health, two areas that are at the basis of the development of indigenous communities. 
"The Government of Quebec strives to ensure the development of indigenous communities in its territory. The federal government must nevertheless assume its responsibilities in order to provide efficient and sustainable solutions to several issues that Aboriginal people in Quebec live, "concluded Madame Larouche.
