Odanak- The Minister Delegate to Aboriginal Affairs, Mrs. Elizabeth Larouche, went today in the Abénaquise Community of Odanak on the occasion of a visit from the institution Kiuna, the first post-secondary school made by and for the first Nations of Quebec. 
"It is very stimulating for me to meet the students of the Kiuna institution, as well as the people who work there. Quebec's Aboriginal people want a bright future for their young, and education is one of the keys to offering them this opportunity, "Ms. Larouche said. 
The minister was able to benefit from a visit to the places, as a result of which she expressed her enthusiasm: "The facilities of the institution Kiuna are beautiful! I am very pleased that Aboriginal youth can attend a school of this quality. " 
Ms. Larouche then dinner and discussed with the students, with the regional leader of the Assembly of First Nations of Quebec and Labrador, Mr. Ghislain Picard. She also met with the Grand Chief of Abenakis of Odanak, Mr. Richard O'Bomsawin. 
Inaugurated two years ago, the Kiuna institution, administered by the First Nations Education Council (CNEC), offers Aboriginal students a quality education in an environment that highlights the culture and traditions of First Nations, improving Thus access to post-secondary education as well as the success and graduation rate among young people and adults who enrolls. 
The Quebec government financed the Kiuna institution up to $ 1.07 million, of which $ 33,000 comes from the Aboriginal Affairs Secretariat (SAA). 
Ms. Elizabeth Larouche also took advantage of his visit to Odanak to visit the Abénakis Museum. Open since 1962, this is the first Native American museum to open its doors in Quebec.
