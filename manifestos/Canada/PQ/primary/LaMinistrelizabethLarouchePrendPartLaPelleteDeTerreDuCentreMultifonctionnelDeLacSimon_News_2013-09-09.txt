Quebec- Minister Delegate to Aboriginal Affairs and MP Abitibe, Ms. Elizabeth Larouche, was passing through the Algonquin community of Lac-Simon today to participate in the ceremony marking the official start of the construction work of a new multifunctional center. This center will house the grocery store and community hall. The new complex project will create about twenty direct jobs, one fifteen full-time. 
"The multifunctional complex will make it possible to diversify the economy of Lac-Simon in addition to improving its well-being. Our government is proud to support this project that will certainly provide structuring spin-offs for the community, "said Minister Elizabeth Larouche, who was received by the Chief of the Algonquin Community of Lac-Simon, Ms. Salom√© McKenzie. 
The $ 921,950-funded grocery store project, which will serve the community and clientele of surrounding diet, hardware and non-prescription pharmaceuticals. Its corporate model will be based on the concept of the social economy. Thus, the Wabak solidarity cooperative will provide work to its worker members as well as property to its user members. 
An amount of $ 880,000 will renew some community infrastructure. The current community hall had become obsolete. The new center will be used to hold cultural and community activities, as well as for leisure and official meetings. 
The financial assistance given to the Anishnabe Nation Council of Lac-Simon comes from the Aboriginal Development Fund, the Aboriginal Initiative Fund IIDU Aboriginal Affairs Secretariat, in the Community infrastructure components and economic development. The objective of these programs is to support projects that may have significant benefits on the development of Quebec's Aboriginal circles, whether economically, socially or community.
