Québec- Minister Delegate to Aboriginal Affairs, Mrs. Elizabeth Larouche, met last night in Ottawa, the United Nations Special Rapporteur on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples, James Anaya, currently on mission to draw up a portrait of the situation Aboriginal people at the Canadian level. 
During this meeting, Minister Larouche informed the Special Rapporteur of the various initiatives of the Government of Quebec aimed at supporting Aboriginal communities. 
"We can be very proud of our fruitful collaborations with First Nations, Inuit and Civil Society to promote the rapprochement between our peoples. The implementation of the Governance Agreement in the territory of Eeyou Istchee-James, the development of Unplan of Action to counter racism and discrimination against Aboriginal people, as well as the deposit of a project Law Establishment The Month of Aboriginal HistoryConstitute Examples of Our Efforts, "said Minister Larouche. 
The Special Rapporteur has been particularly interested in the issue of natural resources and consultation. Minister Larouche recalled the participation of the Government of Quebec Ausommet on the territories and resources, organized by the Assembly of First Nations of Quebec and Labrador (APNQL) on December 3, 2012. She also explained the operation of the table. Québec-APNQL who has derived from it. This high-level table allows the APNQL to bring the different files on which they want to work in collaboration with the government. 
Special rapporteurs are independent experts appointed by the United Nations Council for Human Rights. The current Special Rapporteur on the situation of human rights and fundamental freedoms of indigenous peoples, Mr. Anaya, was appointed in 2008. His mandate is to submit annual reports on topics or situations of particular importance. for the promotion and protection of the rights of indigenous peoples. Mr. Anaya's visit to Canada is from October 7 to 15, 2013.
