Montreal- The Governor of Maine State, Paul Lepage, was in Montreal today to sign a multi-sectoral cooperation agreement with the Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois. This agreement comes to formalize and strengthen collaboration between the two states in various sectors of reciprocal interest including economic development, energy, transportation, safety, culture and the Francophonie. 
"Quebec and Maine not only share a history and heritage, but also the issues and challenges that have important opportunities for collaboration. I am delighted by the signing of this agreement, which testifies to our will to work together to ensure the development of relations that will be mutually beneficial, "said the Prime Minister Pauline Marois. 
This agreement will promote the development of business partnerships, stimulate the exchange of information and expertise in the field of environmentally friendly energy technologies, to encourage cooperation to improve traffic goods and persons on both sides of the border and to ensure mutual assistance for urgency or disaster management. 
Note that a joint committee formed by representatives of Quebec and Maine will be created to ensure the application of the agreement. In addition, he will be responsible for studying and approving the projects to be carried out as part of an action plan.
