Quebec- The Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, will receive in Quebec, on 14 and 15 March, the Prime Minister of the French Republic, Jean-Marc Ayrault, on the occasion of the 17th alternating meeting of the first ministers . 
"It will be a huge honor to welcome Prime Minister Ayrault to Quebec. Our relationship with France is based on a common history and a deep friendship, but also on links that embrace all the spheres of activity of our two companies. This meeting will open a new chapter of this relationship, in line with the current aspirations and issues of Quebec and France, "said the Prime Minister. 
Prime Minister Jean-Marc Ayrault will first go to Montreal on March 14 in order, in particular, to talk to Quebec's Prime Minister. The next day, in Quebec City, the two counterparts will participate in the 17th alternating meeting of the prime ministers. 
Established in 1977, the alternating meetings of Quebec and French prime ministers set the guidelines and priorities of the Franco-Quebec relationship. Several ministers of Quebec and France participate in the enlarged work session, which takes place during these meetings held alternately in Quebec and France. 
The agenda of this 17th alternating meeting of the prime ministers will be available soon.
