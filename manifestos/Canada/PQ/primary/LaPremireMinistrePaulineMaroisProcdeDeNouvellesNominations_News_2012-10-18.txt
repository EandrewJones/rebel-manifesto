Québec- Following the statement made this afternoon by Ms. Véronique Hivon, announcing that she renounced, for health reasons, to her duties as Minister delegate to Public Health and the Protection of Youth, and Minister responsible for the Lanaudière Region, Ms. Pauline Marois proceeded to the following nominations, who take effect now: 
Ms. Diane Hamelin, Member of Parliament for Masson, became a parliamentary assistant to the Minister of Health and Social Services. 
Ms. Suzanne Proulx, MP for Sainte-Rose, became a parliamentary deputy to the minister responsible for the status of women. 
Mr. Nicolas Marceau, Minister of Finance and Economy, also becomes Minister responsible for the Lanaudière region. 
On this occasion, the Premier wanted to warmly thank Ms. Hivon: "It's regretted that I see you leaving your position as minister, Véronique, but I deeply understand your reasons. I dare to believe that your fellow citizens, that you will continue to represent the National Assembly, will do the same. From the bottom of the heart, I wish you all the happiness possible in this new challenge full of promises that awaits you. »
