Montreal- The Quebec Prime Minister Pauline Marois, and the Minister-President of the Flemish Government, Mr. Kris Peeters, signed this afternoon in Montreal a joint statement on strengthening scientific and economic cooperation between Quebec and Flanders. 
"I look forward to the close links that unite Quebec and Flanders for almost 25 years. This statement is part of continuity and aims to strengthen our cooperation by focusing on key sectors, which echo the stakes of the 21st century that are sustainable development, green technologies, electric transport and energy, "said The Prime Minister. 
On this occasion, the three Research Funds of Quebec (Nature and Technologies, Health, Society and Culture) and the Flanders Scientific Research Fund renewed a scientific cooperation agreement that unequivocally marks the intensification of these exchanges in matter. innovation and scientific research. 
"In the context of globalization and international competitiveness that we know, and in which nations need to cooperate, our supported relationship with Flanders eloquently promotes Quebec's prosperity and contributes to the dynamism in innovation by fruitful technological exchanges. who take advantage of companies here, "said Pauline Marois. 
Remember that the Prime Minister had been first maintained with Mr. Peeters in Davos at the World Economic Duforum in January 2013. This visit to the Minister-President of the Flemish Government makes it possible to reiterate the interest that Quebec bears to Flanders and marks the dynamism of this relationship.
