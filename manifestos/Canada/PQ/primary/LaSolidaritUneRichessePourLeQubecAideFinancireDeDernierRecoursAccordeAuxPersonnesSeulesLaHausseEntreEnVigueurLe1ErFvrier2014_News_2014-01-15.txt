Québec- The Minister of Employment and Social Solidarity, Minister of Labor and Minister responsible for the Status of Women, Ms. Agnès Maltais, recalls the entry into force on February 1, 2014, a gradual increase in aid Given to single persons, without constraints to employment, providers of last resort financial assistance. Through this increase, single people will benefit from an additional $ 600 per year by 2017 for a total annual investment, ultimately, about $ 71 million. 
"Important progress has been made in Quebec in the fight against poverty to continue in this direction, our government has made a commitment last May to increase financial assistance for single people. Indeed, their situation remains particularly worrying because it has continued to deteriorate over the last ten years. We hear over with this increase that increases their disposable income, "Minister Maltese explained.

This measure is part of the government guidelines on which the "solidarity: a wealth for Quebec" pillar is based, one of the four pillars of government action, with integrity, prosperity and identity. These guidelines, made public on October 30 by the Premier Pauline Marois, will guide the government's action in the implementation of measures aimed at improving the well-being of the most vulnerable individuals and families. The government wants to give a new impetus to solidarity, an inseparable value of collective wealth. 
This draft regulation amending the Personal and Family Assistance Regulations was formalized today during its publication in the Official Gazette du Québec. This follows a prepublication on November 1, 2013, which was accompanied by a period of 45 days provided for public consultation purposes.
