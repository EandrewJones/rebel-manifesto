Montréal - The National Committee of Young People of the Parti Québécois (CNJPQ) salutes the law of law 13concentering the non-constituency vote in the premises of post-secondary institutions. "The government has just made an important gesture for democracy. This new law will promote the vote of young voters, "said Frédéric St-Jean, President of the CNJPQ. 
This is a long-standing concern of the CNJPQ. It was also shared by all the youth wings of political parties of Quebec last year, on the eve of the general elections. This idea has been echoing the ears of Premier Pauline Marois, who had filed her request in an open letter in duty on November 30, 2011. Even the Chief Electoral Officer of Quebec (DGEQ) was positioned. in favor of the project. "We are pleased to see that the Government of the Parti Québécois, contrary to its predecessor, takes into consideration the voice of young people and acts accordingly," said St-Jean. "This is a project that transcends generations and encourages the greatest possible voter turnout," he added. The CNJPQ is also satisfied that all parliamentarians have followed suit and unanimously voted in favor of this new law. "It demonstrates the irreproachable work of the Minister of Democratic Institutions and Citizen Participation, Bernard Drainville, who has rally all his colleagues behind the voice of young people". 
Only 62, 1% of voters from 18 to 24 argued their right to vote in 2012, the CNJPQ is of the opinion that we must do everything possible to promote the increase in their participation. "With the adoption of Law 13, it is nearly 450,000 young people who will directly have access to a voting office in their school institution, it is a major investment in our democracy." to conclude Mr. St-Jean.
