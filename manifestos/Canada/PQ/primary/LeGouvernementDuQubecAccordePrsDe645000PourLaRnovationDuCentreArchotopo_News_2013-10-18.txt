The Bergeronnes - The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, accompanied by the member for René-Lévesque and head of the North Shore, Marjolan Dufour region, today announced the allocation of financial assistance from $ 644,700 Aucentre Archeo-Topo, for the renovation and setting up to its facilities. 
"The archaeological heritage of the Côte-Nord has extraordinary richness. With the support we bring today, the Archeo-Topo Center can pursue its excellent work to familiarize and initiation to archeology, for the benefit of citizens and visitors, "said Minister Maka Kotto. 
Built in 1995, the Archeo-Topo Center is located in the Bergeronnes, on the edge of the St. Lawrence River, which constantly exposes it to the great winds. The amounts invested will make it possible to realize important work and to guarantee the preservation of his collections. 
The center currently hosts 3500 visitors a year in 184 days of opening. The renovation will bring up to 210 the number of opening days, thereby increasing the cultural offer in Haute-Côte-Nord. 
The participation of the department is $ 644,700, with its program helps assets. The municipality of the Bergeronnes and the distribution corporation of the archeology of the North Shore also participate in the project. 
"I look forward to today's announcement since, in addition to enhanced the cultural offer on the North Shore, it will increase Quebec heritage accessibility on the territory," said MP Marjolan Dufour.
