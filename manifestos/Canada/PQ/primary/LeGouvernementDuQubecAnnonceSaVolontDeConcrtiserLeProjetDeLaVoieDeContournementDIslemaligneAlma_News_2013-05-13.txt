For 2013-2015, the Ministry of Transport provides for estimated investments between $ 2.5 million and $ 3 million, mainly for preparatory activities and to initiate acquisitions. 
Until now, the project has received a favorable orientation from the Office of Public Audiences on the Environment, while the Quebec Agricultural Territory Protection Commission has given its authorization for the continuation of the project in the summer of 2012. The Final draft is completed and the budget availabilities recorded in the 2013-2015 programming will be used to prepare the final plans and quotes. "I am very happy to reiterate my desire to move forward with this project and to allow the advancement of the preparatory stages that will, as soon as possible, ensure the beginning of the work," said Minister Sylvain Gaudreault. 
Given its cost, greater than $ 40 million, the project of Isle-Maligne bypass is subject to the [Governance Framework Policy of Major Public Infrastructure Projects [(http: // www. tresor.gouv.qc.ca/infrastructures-publiques/governance-des-projects-dinfrastructure/).
