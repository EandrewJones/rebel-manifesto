Quebec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, and the Minister responsible for the Capitale-Nationale and Chaudière-Appalaches, Minister of Labor, Employment and Social Solidarity and Minister responsible for the Status of women, Agnès Maltese, are pleased to announce the award of financial assistance from $ 206,700 to Quebec's Jewelry School and the National School of Québec City for the purchase of specialized equipment. 
"The Government of Quebec recognizes the importance of equipping our training schools in art trades of favorable conditions to ensure their sustainability. Thus, by their creative talents and skills, the students who frequent them contribute to the maintenance and development of a know-how that sets us apart, "said Minister Maka Kotto. 
"The jewelery school and the School of Lutherie are two tangible evidence of cultural dynamism and the Capitale-Nationale's artistic innovation capacity. By its professionalism, the staff of the two schools allows the development of slopes of excellence and forms a creative and dynamic succession, "said Minister Agnès Malteis. 
The financial contribution of the Ministry of Culture and Communications, $ 206,700, is paid under the Capital Assistance Program. It will allow the two institutions to have a state-of-the-art equipment. 
These workshop schools provide technical education in crafts in partnership with Cégep Limoilou in Quebec City. They are supported by the operation by the Cultural Business Development Corporation (SODEC), as part of its program of assistance to workshops in craftsmen. This program is for 14 accredited workshop schools as part of the National Training Training Plan. The annual grant granted by the SODEC serves to the development of research, dissemination and support activities in the organization.
