Daveluyville- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, and the Minister of Sustainable Development, Environment, Wildlife and Parks and Minister responsible for the Mauricie Region and the Center-Center Region. Quebec, Yves-François Blanchet, announced a $ 500,000 grant to the city of Daveluyville for the removal of the library into high school. 
"Libraries are places of exchange, meeting, discovery and knowledge that contributes greatly to the cultural dynamism of our communities. I salute the initiative of the three municipalities who have decided to unite their efforts and to redevelop this place between the walls of the Sainte-Anne high school, since this new location will give young people privileged access to the books, "said the Minister Kotto. 
"A library is a resource needed for the cultural and social development of communities. The municipalities of Daveluyville, Maddington and Saint-Anne-du-Sault have understood it well by working together to offer a quality service to their citizens and citizens and to enrich the cultural life of the Center-du-Québec Blanchet added. 
Currently located in the basement of an elderly residence, library facilities will be moved and modernized to offer the population an adequate service that matches the quality standards established by the Quebec government. In addition, a service agreement signed by the municipalities of Daveluyville, Maddington and Saint-Anne-du-Sault will allow more users to benefit from the services offered by this new library. 
The $ 500,000 financial assistance granted by the Ministry of Culture and Communications comes from the Capital Assistance Program that allows for projects that promote creation and the dissemination and radiation of culture in the regions.
