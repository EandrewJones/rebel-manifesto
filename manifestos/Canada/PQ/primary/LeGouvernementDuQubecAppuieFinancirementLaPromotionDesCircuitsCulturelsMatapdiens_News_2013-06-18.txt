Member of Parliament for Matane-Matapédia, Minister responsible for the Bas-Saint-Laurent Region and Minister Delegate to Tourism, Pascal Bérubé, on behalf of the Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, attended today at the launch of the mobile application to promote Mathedian cultural circuits. The Quebec government has invested $ 12,745 in this project, a sum that comes from the first cultural development agreement between the Matapédia MRC and the Ministry of Culture and Communications, to which Caisse Desjardins Valley of Matapédia. 'Is joined. 
"Cultural development agreements allow us to act in consultation with local and regional authorities to promote the development of the arts and culture in our communities. Thanks to the realization of the Mathedian cultural circuits, an innovative project that uses new technologies, the MRC de la Matapédia makes the bet that this online cultural promotion will join a larger number of tourists and will have the effect of inciting them to discover the Many attractions in the region, "said Minister Kotto. 
"I hope that access to these virtual cultural circuits will cite the curiosity of visitors of passage and tourists and will encourage them to extend their stay in the region. Because, in terms of cultural tourism, the Matapédia Valley has a lot to offer, and it is its points of interest that the designers of this mobile application wanted to make known, "said Minister Bérubé. 
Available for free For the Androidet for Apple products, the application Cultural Circuits Matapediens are available in four themes: built heritage, salmon fishing, arts and craftsmen and agrotourism. All these tours offer historical headings, informative texts and photographs on the tourist attractions of the region and on the places to visit. A fifth circuit, having the theme the landscapes, is in progress. The electronic application is scalable and allows the addition of circuits as well as its update.
