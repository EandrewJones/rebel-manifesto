Quebec, on July 9, 2014 -The MP for Rousseau and the official opposition for the finance, Nicolas Marceau, unacceptable judge the federal project to create a Canadian Securities Commission and enjoins the Quebec government to challenge it in court. 
The cooperative scheme pushed by the Federal and Ontario, far from simplifying the supervision of the financial sector, rather resulted in the true system of cooperation between the provinces, which already exists and works very well. 
"The Federal Project, in addition to encroaching about the skills of Quebec, creating a double structure that would be nothing but a loss of time and money for businesses. The Government of Quebec must immediately announce that it will contest it before the courts, "said Nicolas Marceau. 
While announcing his project under the name of "cooperative regime", the Federal Minister of Finance does not hide his true intentions by saying, "Today's understanding is an important step in setting up an organization. single regulatory nationally ". This clearly contravenes the judgment of the Supreme Court. 
"While a majority of provinces prefer to keep the passport in securities, the Government of Quebec must act now to enforce its skills and pass the message that the federal project to focus the regulation of financial markets in Ontario will never be accepted In Quebec and will not be able to move forward, "concluded the member for Rousseau. 
Recall that the securities sector is crucial for the entire Montreal and Quebec economy. The financial sector is 3000 companies in Montreal, 154,000 jobs in Quebec, including 100,000 in Montreal, which is 16th in the world's financial centers. It's 6% of Quebec GDP.
