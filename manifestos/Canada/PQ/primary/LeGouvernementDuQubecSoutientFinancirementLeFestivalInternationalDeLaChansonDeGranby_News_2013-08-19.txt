Quebec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, and the Minister Delegate to Tourism, Pascal Bérubé, are pleased to announce the award of financial assistance of $ 180,000 in support of the International Song of Granbyqui will take place from 4 to 14 September. 
The International Festival of Granby's song hosts this year from the succession artists from several Canadian provinces. The event constitutes for these 24 semi-finalists a powerful stepping stone. Several authors, composers and performers experienced their first moments of glory and continued successful careers. 
"I am happy to associate myself with this big celebration of French-speaking music. The Granby Song International Festival is part of the Cultural Landscape of Quebec for 45 years already. The reputation acquired by the festival is properly phenomenal. Succession as well as established stars find an enthusiastic audience that continues to grow from year to year, "said Minister Kotto. 
"The Granby Song International Festival has become, over the years, a must-see event for all francophone song enthusiasts. He makes us discover new talents that will become outstanding ambassadors of the Quebec song abroad. I invite the people and visitors to participate in the many shows and take advantage of the opportunity to discover the many tourist attractions of the Eastern Townships, "said Minister Bérubé. 
The contribution of the Government of Quebec is distributed as follows: $ 125,000 from the Cultural Business Development Corporation Development Corporation (SODEC) program, and $ 55,000 from the tourism industry development program - Financial Assistance to Festivals and Tourist Events of Tourisme Québec.
