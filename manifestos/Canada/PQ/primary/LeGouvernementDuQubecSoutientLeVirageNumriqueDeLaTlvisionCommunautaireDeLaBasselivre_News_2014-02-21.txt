Gatineau - On behalf of the Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, the Minister of Public Safety and Minister responsible for the Outaouais Region, Stéphane Bergeron, announces the allocation of $ 126,500 financial assistance to Community television of Lower Hare, for the acquisition of specialized equipment that will allow it to take the HD turn. 
"Community media play a vital role in developing the sense of belonging to an environment. In the age of 2.0, it is essential to continue to support these information officers who perform remarkable work with citizens from all regions of Quebec, "said Maka Kotto. 
The acquisition of equipment adapted to the HD turn will improve the quality of diffusion of the productions of this television station, the oldest of the Autonomous Community televisions in Quebec and the only one present on the territory of the Outaouais. 
"This important technological shift had become absolutely necessary to allow Community television of Lower Hare to maintain its partnership with the Videotron cable and its MATV channel in Outaouais. It can continue to offer the population of the region a diversified program and reflecting its reality, "said Stéphane Bergeron. 
The Ministry of Culture and Communications grants $ 126,500 to this project, the total cost estimated at $ 158,220, under its Capital Assistance Program. Community television of Lower Lower Hare completes funding. 
As for the oldest autonomous community television of Quebec, the Lower Hare TVC, it welcomes this announcement of the Ministry with enthusiasm, since it will allow it to maintain its services well beyond its forty-two years of existence; Thanks to this new technology of the HD. "On behalf of the Board of Directors, the Lower Hare TVC team and the entire population concerned, we wish to thank the Government of Quebec and its Ministry of Culture and Communications for this important contribution," concluded The President, Sylvain Pilon.
