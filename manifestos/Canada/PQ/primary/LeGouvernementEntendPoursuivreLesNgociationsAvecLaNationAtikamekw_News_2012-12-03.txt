Montreal - Québec Prime Minister, Ms. Pauline Marois, announced this morning that his government was firm intention to pursue bilateral negotiations with the Atikamekws communities of Manawan, Wemotaci and Opitciwan, as well as the Council of the Nation atikamekw . The Government of Quebec intends to finalize the process leading to the signing of the principle agreement concerning the management of natural resources in NitaSkinan in order to arrive, by July 2013, to the signing of a final agreement. 
This announcement intervenes in the context of the Summit on the territories and resources that takes place today in Montreal. The Prime Minister, accompanied by the Minister Delegate to Aboriginal Affairs, Mrs. Elizabeth Larouche, as well as several ministers from his Government, reiterated his willingness to continue trade. "My government is putting on dialogue to reach a common vision and carrier solutions that will facilitate the establishment of a nation-nation relationship. I am confident that negotiations will lead quickly to concrete results, "the Prime Minister said. 
These negotiations concern the issues relating to the sharing of profits related to the exploitation of natural resources, the participation of atikamekws in economic development, as well as the mechanisms of participation in the management of the territory. 
The Minister Delegate to Aboriginal Affairs added that "these bilateral negotiations are aimed at achieving tangible and mutually beneficial results. Some Aboriginal nations closely follow these negotiations that will probably serve as a model, marking a new starting point between our nations. » 
The Summit on Territories and Resources is organized by the Assembly of First Nations of Quebec and Labrador. It marks the starting point for a renewed relationship that is based on mutual respect between the Government of Quebec and Aboriginal people.
