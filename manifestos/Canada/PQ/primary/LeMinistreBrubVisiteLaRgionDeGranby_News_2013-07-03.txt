Québec- Minister Delegate to Tourism, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, visited yesterday, two major tourist attractions in the Granby region, the Granby Zoo which celebrates 60 years this year, as well as the country orchard. 
Currently on tour of Quebec to meet tourist actors and take, in the field, all the measure of Quebec's expertise in tourism, the minister has been impressed by the scope and quality of these two attractions. "I get up my hat at the Granby Zoo and the country-style orchard who, each in their niche, are true models! He wrote with enthusiasm. 
At the zoo, he was able to admire the aviary of the Loriquets, recently inaugurated. This is part of a set of improvements totaling $ 4.5 million and for which Tourisme Québec granted $ 500,000. "We must recognize the audacity of this immense development project," says Bérubé. The Granby Zoo is positioned advantageously and is even more attractive to Quebec visitors, but most importantly, for tourists in the south and west of our borders. " 
The minister wished to take advantage of his visit to the site to greet the extraordinary work of Ms. Joanne Lalumière who recently announced his departure from the General Management. "Madame Lalumière has all my admiration and gratitude for his great contribution to tourist development of his region and all Quebec," said Mr. Bérubé ".
