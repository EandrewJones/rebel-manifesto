Qu√©bec- The Minister of Justice and Minister responsible for the application of professional laws, Mr. Bertrand St-Arnaud, presented today to the National Assembly law No. 49- An Act to amend various professional laws and others legislative provisions in the field of applied sciences. 
The bill responds to one of the recommendations of Johnson's lament, set up following the collapse of the Viaduct of the Concorde, that relates to mandatory surveillance of engineering and architecture work. He also echoes the report of Coroner Jacques Ramsay in a case of collapse of a concrete beam having caused a death. 
"From now on, any workforce of a large scale, both in the public and private sphere, must be subject to a compliance review. The proposed amendments will thus contribute to the quality of engineering and architectural works, "said the Minister responsible for the application of professional laws. 
The proposed legislative changes also aim, in particular, to update the professional fields of the agronomists, architects, chemists, geologists and engineers as well as to reserve their high-risk activities of injury. 
Other provisions will also enable the professional orders concerned to authorize the exercise by professional technologists, certain activities that are reserved for their members, so sailing the contribution of professional technologists to this sector of activity. 
"Increasing knowledge and the evolution of practices and technologies made it essential to modernize professional practices in the field of applied sciences," concluded Bertrand St-Arnaud.
