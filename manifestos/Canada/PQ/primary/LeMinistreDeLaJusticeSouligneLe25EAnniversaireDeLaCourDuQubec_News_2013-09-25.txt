Quebec- The Minister of Justice and Attorney General of Quebec, Mr. Bertrand St-Arnaud, today highlighted the 25th anniversary of the creation of Quebec Court. 
This Court was born in 1988 from the Union of the Provincial Court, of strictly civilian competence, the Court of Peace Sessions, the Criminal Affairs, and the Youth Tribunal. However, the history of the Court of Quebec is much longer and goes back as far as in the 18th century. 
On the occasion of a motion presented to the National Assembly in the presence of the Chief Justice, the Honorable Elizabeth Corte, the Associate Chief Justice, the Honorable Mario Tremblay, the Deputy Chief Justice in the Civil Chamber, Hon. Pierre E. Audet, the Deputy Chief Justice in the Chamber of Youth, the Honorable Claude C. Boulanger, the Assistant Chief Justice to the Criminal and Criminal Chamber, the Honorable Danielle Côté and the Chief Justice Assistant responsible for municipal courses, the Honorable André Perreault, the Minister of Justice highlighted the key role of this Court which represents the most important Tribunal du Québec with respect to its number of judges, its volume of business treated and the diversity of fields of competence. The Quebec Court is also active in a considerable number of projects with the ultimate goal of better access to justice. 
"This Court is one of the pillars of our law society and our democracy. I want a tribute to be returned to its members, recognized for their independence, impartiality and integrity, as well as their teams that are dedicated daily for better justice, "concluded the Minister of Justice.
