Quebec- The Minister of Health and Social Services and Minister Responsible for Seniors, Dr. Réjean Hébert, takes advantage of the 7th International Day of Rare Diseases to recall the importance of sensitizing the population of Quebec to the situation of people with illness Rare and reiterates the government's willingness to offer them, as well as their loved ones, the best support possible. 
"We are willing to offer people with rare diseases and their loved ones the services they need. Because of their rarity, these diseases represent a challenge for the Quebec health network, both in terms of their treatment and their diagnosis. This is a challenge we can relate together, in partnership with the many organizations invested in this case, "said Dr. Hébert. 
According to the National Institute of Excellence in Health and Social Services (AESSSS), a rare disease is a disease that affects a person in 2,000. There are thousands, and it is estimated that 80% of them are Genetic origin. Moreover, despite their rarity, these diseases affect, in total, between 6 and 8% of the population. The majority of them affect children, including 30% of whom see their life expectancy reduced to five years and under. 
In recent years, several initiatives have been put in place in Quebec aimed at providing the best possible services to people with rare diseases. For example, new diseases have been included in the Quebec Blood Neonatal Screening Program to ensure their care more quickly. In addition, the Ministry of Health and Social Services has provided financial support in Orphaet Québec, a Quebec division of an international program aimed at constituting a rare disease database to facilitate access to information and collaboration.
