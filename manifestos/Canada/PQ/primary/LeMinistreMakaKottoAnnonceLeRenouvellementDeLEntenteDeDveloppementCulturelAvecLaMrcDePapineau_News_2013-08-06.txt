Quebec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, is pleased to announce the renewal of the cultural development agreement with the Papineau MRC. The financial assistance of $ 126,000 granted over three years by the Department will promote the development of the arts and culture in the MRC. 
"To note the positive impact of the previous agreements with the MRC of Papineau, this Agreement will make it possible to develop the cultural offer of the region in addition to contributing to the conservation and development of its heritage. Across Quebec, these agreements open meetings between creators, creators and the public to make culture live, "said Minister Kotto. 
Located in the Outaouais region, the MRC of Papineau brings together 24 municipalities and 22,000 inhabitants. Renewed today by the Ministry for a second time, this agreement is very important for this rural RCM. The latter recently adopted a cultural policy and an action plan to support the dynamism of community stakeholders. 
"I want to congratulate the MRC of Papineau for the concrete actions it poses to ensure the development of its cultural heritage. The financial assistance announced today will allow him to continue his work and to radiate the cultural vitality of the Outaouais region, "added the Minister of Public Safety and Minister responsible for the Region, St√©phane Bergeron. 
The agreement made today includes an investment totaling $ 324,300 which is distributed as follows: $ 126,000 from the Ministry of Culture and Communications, $ 132,300 of the CLD $ 45,000 MRC, $ 45,000 of Papineau and $ 21,000 of the Desjardins Caisses of the Petite-Nation. A first cultural development agreement was concluded for the period 2006-2009 with the MRC and for which the Department granted $ 45,000, followed by a first renewal totaling $ 75,000 for the years 2009-2012.
