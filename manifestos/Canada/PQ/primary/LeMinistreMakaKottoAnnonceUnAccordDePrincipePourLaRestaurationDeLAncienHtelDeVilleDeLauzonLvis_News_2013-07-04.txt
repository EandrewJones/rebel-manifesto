Québec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, announces a $ 228,500 principle agreement for the Del'anien Lauzon Town Hallway project in Lévis. This financial assistance will come from the Quebec Cultural Heritage Fund, as part of the Program Assistance to Capital. 
"It is important to preserve this building that is an undeniable heritage and historical interest. The restoration of the building will enhance an important element of the built heritage of the Lauzon area of the city of Lévis. This project will help revitalize the heart of this neighborhood that includes some of the oldest buildings in the region, "said Minister Maka Kotto. 
Erected around 1875, the building has been protected since 1975. The former Lauzon Town Hall is of a heritage interest for its architectural value. Style Second Empire, it is representative of public and institutional buildings erected at the end of the nineteenth century. It also has great historical value related to the public and community functions it has occupied. 
The building has not been the subject of significant work since 2001. The agreement in principle will make it possible to continue the definition of the project and to complete the financial assembly for the restoration of the external envelope of the building and the strengthening of a part of its structure.
