Quebec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, is proud to support amateur cultural practice in areas as varied as music, dance, literature, heritage and cinema, by attributing financial assistance totaling More than $ 1 million to eleven national agencies of young amateur succession and cultural leisure. 
"Synonymous with quality of life and creativity, the cultural leisure energizes Quebec society and increases the commitment of citizens and citizens in the cultural development of their community. It is then essential to facilitate access to the greatest number. This investment is also part of our desire to support the artistic and creative vitality of young people, since the practice of a cultural leisure is not only trainer, but it also cultivates self-esteem and sense of identity, "said Minister Kotto. 
Payed under the Functioning Assistance Program for national agencies of the young amateur and cultural leisure, this grant of $ 1,160,160 will allow eleven organizations to continue their mission, or to support and promote the activities of hundreds of regional cultural recreation organizations. The latter also provide supervision of cultural practices and organize different activities, where participants can exchange, develop their knowledge, improve their performance and promote their sector of activity. 
These organizations contribute greatly to increasing accessibility and participation in culture in all regions of Quebec. Among these, nine are national cultural recreation associations bringing together choirs, harmonies, symphonic orchestras of young people, theater troops and dance, literary societies, folklorists, parallel movie theater, musical sets and history or genealogy societies. As for the two agencies of the young amateur, flight and macadam responsibility as well as the intercollegial network of socio-cultural activities of Quebec, they support the development of artistic succession by organizing events or promoting the meeting with professional artists. 
This investment is added to the other commitments of the Government of Quebec aimed at supporting the influence of the arts of youth, such as the updating of the Culture-Education Memorandum of Understanding with the Ministry of Education, Recreation and Sport. and the allocation of a refundable tax credit for the artistic and cultural activities of young people aged at least 5 years, but not yet reached the age of 16, announced in the 2013-2014 budget of the Government of Quebec.
