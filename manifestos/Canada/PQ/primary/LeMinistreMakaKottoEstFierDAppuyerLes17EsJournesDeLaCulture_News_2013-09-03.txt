Quebec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, today announced the award of $ 315,000 financial assistance to the body culture for all, for the holding of the 17th edition of the Days of Culture , which will take place from 27 to 29 September. 
"The days of culture are a favor for the promotion of cultural actions throughout Quebec and their integration into the daily life of our fellow citizens and our fellow citizens. On behalf of the Government of Quebec, I want to thank the organizers, especially culture for all, as well as artists, craftsmen and all cultural workers who make the success of this significant event for our culture and our national identity, " Underlined Minister Kotto during the event launching activity. 
Created in 1997, the Days of Culture aim to raise awareness of the importance and need for greater access to the arts and culture for all citizens and citizens. Annually, more than 3,000 free activities are held in some 350 municipalities in Quebec. Last year, 220,000 people took part in the activities offered for the 16th Days of Culture. 
The non-profit organization culture for all is mandated to coordinate the holding of the Days of Culture, established by Decree of the National Assembly, in order to emphasize the importance of culture for the identity and development of Quebec. Culture for all is to help make arts and culture recognize as essential dimensions of individual and collective development, promoting citizens' participation and citizens in cultural life.
