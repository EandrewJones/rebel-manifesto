Quebec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, welcomes the holding of a parliamentary committee that will focus on the relevance of regulating the public selling price of printed and digital new books and the evaluation of its impacts. 
"It is imperative that this regulatory perspective is the subject of a public debate while keeping in mind that the book remains the largest cultural industry in Quebec, with sales rated at nearly $ 800 million a year. In addition, our goal is to provide the best access to the book for citizens, to ensure its diversity and the vitality of Quebec book-chain actors, "said Minister Kotto. 
Remember that the public selling price regulation of new printed and digital books subject to retailers for the sale of novelties, for a specified period, at the price set by the publisher or the importer. 
The Parliamentary Committee will take the form of special consultations with representatives of the Book, Consumer Associations, Retailers and Certain Stakeholders from the Economic, Legal and Business Environments. It will measure the cultural, socio-economic, commercial and legal impacts of such regulation, to study the application parameters and recommend any other necessary measures. 
The parliamentary committee will begin on August 19th. A consultation document reporting the multiple facets of a sales price regulation of the new printed and digital books has been prepared by the Ministry of Culture and Communications to create reflections and is available on the website of the 'National Assembly.
