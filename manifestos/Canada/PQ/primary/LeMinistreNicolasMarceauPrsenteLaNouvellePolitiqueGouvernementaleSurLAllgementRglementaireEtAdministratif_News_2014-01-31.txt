Quebec- As part of the 2014 paperwork awareness week organized by the Canadian Federation of Independent Company, the Minister of Finance and the Economy, Mr. Nicolas Marceau, today released the new governmental on regulatory and administrative relief. 
"Legislative and regulatory measures are needed to ensure the proper functioning of the state, but they have repercussions on businesses. As part of the 2013-14 budget, our government is committed to action to reduce their impact by implementing all the recommendations of the Regulatory and Administrative Simplification Working Group. The adoption of a more modern policy is in this direction, "said the minister. 
The new policy aims to reduce the cost of adopting legislative or regulatory standards for companies. Among the changes made, the big novelty is in the fact that all regulatory projects affecting companies will now be subject to a regulatory impact assessment by the relevant department or agency that will be published systematically. 
In addition, the regulatory burden of the new measures will have to be modulated according to the size of the companies and should not be more restrictive than that of the trading partners of Quebec, including Ontario. Finally, departments and agencies will need to develop a standards revision mechanism according to their specificities. 
"The Governmental NEWFULTICAL ON REGULATORY RELATING AND ADMINISTRATED TO IMPROVE THE PROCESS FOR THE EDUCATION OF LAWS AND REGULATIONS COUNTING COMPANIES, in addition to making it more transparent, and to meet the expectations of the business community", has concluded The Minister.
