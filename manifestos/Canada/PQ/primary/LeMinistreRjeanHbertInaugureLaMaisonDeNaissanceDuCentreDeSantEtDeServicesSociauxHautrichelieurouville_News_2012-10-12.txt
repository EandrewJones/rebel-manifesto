Richelieu- The Minister of Health and Social Services, Réjean Hébert, in the presence of the Minister of Justice and the member for Chambly, Bertrand St-Arnaud, of the member for Iberville, Marie Bouillé, and the hon. Member for Saint-Jean Dave Turcotte, today has inaugurated the first birthday in Montérégie. Focused in the local premises of the local community service center (CLSC) of Richelieu, this new birth house, which has four bedrooms and five consultation rooms, will welcome its first patients on October 17, 2012. The six midwives who Will be able to assist each year up to 240 women for their delivery. 
"The implementation of this midwives service in the region meets the needs of the population and is part of the line with our priority to facilitate access to health care and services. This will allow both to improve the provision of services in perinatality, strengthen the use of the first line for prenatal monitoring and to develop midwifery services so that they can track And to attend the 10% childbirth of pregnant women in Quebec, "said Minister Réjean Hébert. 
In addition to deliveries, the birthplace will provide pregnancy monitoring services, particularly with women living in vulnerability, and would issue for consultation and knowledge transfer activities. Midwives will continue to assist the deliveries at the Hospital of Haut-Richelieu at the request of women who wish. 
Remember that the development project of this birthday has been jointly funded by the Ministry of Health and Social Services, the High-Richelieu-Rouville Health Foundation and the Montérégie Health and Social Services Agency for The respective amounts of $ 804,000, $ 300,000 and $ 270,000. The recurring annual operating budget of $ 1,065,312 will be shared equally between the department and the agency.
