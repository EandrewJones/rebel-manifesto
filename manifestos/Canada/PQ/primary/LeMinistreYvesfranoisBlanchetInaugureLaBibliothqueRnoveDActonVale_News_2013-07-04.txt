Acton Vale- On behalf of the Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, the member for Johnson, Minister responsible for the regions of Mauricie and Center-du-Québec and Minister of Sustainable Development, the Environment, Wildlife And parks, Yves-François Blanchet, today proceeded to the official inauguration of the Library of Acton Vale. The event took place in the presence of the Mayor of Acton Vale, Éric Charbonneau. 
"Libraries are places of knowledge and playback that play a vital role in the development of knowledge and the intellect of our young people. The freshly renovated library of Acton Vale will meet the needs of its population. Children as well as adults will be offered a wide selection of books, documents and services in an accessible and welcoming place, "said Minister Kotto. 
"The renovation of the municipal library makes it possible to equip citizens and citizens of the city of Acton Vale and the region of a dynamic library, which meets higher quality standards. Located in one of the most beautiful historic buildings in the region, the library has been renovated with a sustainable development. I want to emphasize the city's determination to make its library an indispensable resource for the cultural influence of its environment and I greet the volunteers for their unwavering commitment to this high place of knowledge, "said Minister Blanchet. 
Open to the public for a few weeks, the library has been completely redone during the year. The improvements make it possible to better welcome the different clienteles and to offer users an attractive place of the most functional. The project benefited from the support of the Biblio network of the Montérégie. 
The investment of $ 127,400 granted by the Ministry of Culture and Communications comes from the Program Assistance to Capital Assets.
