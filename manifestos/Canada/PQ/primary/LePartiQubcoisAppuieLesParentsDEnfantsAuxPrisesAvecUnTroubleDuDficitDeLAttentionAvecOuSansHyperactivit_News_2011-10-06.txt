Quebec -It morning at the National Assembly, the member for Drummond, Yves-François Blanchet, filed a petition, signed by 203 Quebeckers, claiming the Minister of Health and Social Services and the Minister of Education. They give people with attention deficit disorder with or without hyperactivity (ADHD) and their families, resources, services and access to the treatment they need. 
"People are mobilized in the field. The efforts made by Ms. Marie-Michèle Lemaire, director of Panda Mauricie / Center-du-Québec, to raise awareness of the population is colossal. The situation that parents of ADHD children live in everyday life is difficult and they are trying to get more resources from the government to help them. We support them in these difficulties and ask the government a better listening for them, "said the deputy sponsor of the petition, Yves-François Blanchet. 
"The government must understand the clear message sent to it by the 203 signatories of the petition. More than ever, these children and their parents must feel that the government is there to support them and that it will deploy the support services needed to continue a family dynamic focused on the best possible development of children and their success " , added the official opposition spokesman for social services and Matapédia deputies, Danielle Doyer. 
"There are currently professionals in the school network. These children, however, must have access to professional services since this support is essential for their academic success, "deplored the member for Jonquière and the official opposition spokeswoman in primary and secondary education, Sylvain Gaudreault. The hon. Member recalls that in his program adopted last April, the Parti Québécois proposes to increase the number of professionals and support employees to meet students with special needs or in difficulty, both in class and service. keep.
