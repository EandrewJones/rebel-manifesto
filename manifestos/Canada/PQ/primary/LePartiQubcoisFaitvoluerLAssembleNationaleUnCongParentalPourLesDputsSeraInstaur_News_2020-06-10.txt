Quebec, on June 10, 2020 - The member for Joliette and spokespersons of the Quebecois Family Party, Véronique Hivon, greatly rejoices to have taken unanimously, with the collaboration of the other three parliamentary groups, a motion providing for the establishment of parental leave for MPs and members of the National Assembly, at the latest next December. 
IN SHORT 
"All political parties recognize the importance of the family challenges posed by the member's work. In terms of family-work conciliation in Parliament, it is time to finally spend speech on acts; It is a message all the more essential that we want a representative national assembly of Quebec society, with a large number of women and young people for whom this issue can be decisive in the choice to present or not in politics. It is therefore simply to enable women and men who have the position of MP or hon. Member to be treated in a similar way to other citizens, "said Véronique Hivon. 
"The Montreal Municipal Council, the House of Commons as well as several parliamentary assemblies elsewhere in the world offer parental leave to their members. I am proud to see Quebec follow the step and finally reflect the 21st century family reality, with all the challenges it involves, whether one is a member of Parliament or not, "she added. 
The motion, as adopted, reads as follows: 
"That the National Assembly affirms its willingness to establish parental leave for the members and that this principle is recognized in the Labor Conditions Act and the Pension Plan of the Members of the National Assembly and the Code of Ethics and ethics of the members of the National Assembly. 
That the Office of the National Assembly entrusts the responsibility of determining the specific modalities of the independent committee on the conditions of work of the members, which will have to make a priority mandate so that it can be put in place at the latest at the latest. In December 2020. "
