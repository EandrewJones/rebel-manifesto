"On my behalf as well as that of the members of Parliament and the great family of the Parti Québécois, I extend my deepest condolences to the family and friends of Jean Alfred. Tireless worker, he was a real man of action, totally devoted to his citizens. I also salute the free thinker he has been, "said Pierre Karl Péladeau. 
Last Saturday, during the funeral of Mr. Alfred, many recalled his tenacity, his commitment and his will to make Quebec a country for all. "We will remember Jean Alfred long, especially because of the intense struggle he has done in favor of social justice. For him, everyone was on an equal footing; It was important to him if you were rich or poor, woman or man, young or elderly, from here or elsewhere. In his mind, every person was entitled to happiness, and this conviction animated him throughout his life, "said Martine Ouellet. 
For his part, Maka Kotto recalled how much Jean Alfred could be a model, an inspiration, not only for the members of the Haitian community of Quebec, but also for all newcomers. "He traced a way that many others borrowed. Following his example, many women and men who came to Quebec have managed to have a place of them. First black elected at the National Assembly of Quebec, Jean Alfred demonstrated that the origins of a person should not slow down his ambitions, "he said. 
Among the achievements of Jean Alfred, mention his role in the creation of Gatineau's hospital and airport, as well as in the extension file of Highway 50.
