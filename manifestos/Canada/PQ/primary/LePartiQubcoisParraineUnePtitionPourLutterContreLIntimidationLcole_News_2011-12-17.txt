Quebec -In the initiative of a 13-year-old High school student The Academy Les Estacades in Mauricie, particularly worried about the scourge of violence in school, the member for Champlain, Noëlta Champagne, sponsors a petition that claims From the Liberal government an effective action plan to better combat intimidation at school. 
"This phenomenon of intimidation at the school is of great concern to us as elected to the National Assembly, but also concerns young people, it does not matter whether they are victims or witnesses of intimidation. It is together, collectively, that we will be able to stop this growing scourge, as long as the government admits that the situation is getting worse, "Christmas Champagne said. 
The member for Champlain was particularly touched when she was approached some time ago by the young emanuous student. "Our young people have the taste to learn in an environment conducive to academic success, safe from any intimidation. We must do everything to offer them a serene place to learn and socialize between them, without being on their guards constantly, especially when we know the disastrous consequences that this intimidation can sometimes lead, "added Christmas Champagne. 
The deadline for signing the electronic petition on the website of the National Assembly is February 9, 2012. "The Liberal government's action plan against intimidation at school is a failure, qualified as an empty shell by the stakeholders. Thus, we invite everyone, school leaders, teachers, parents, young people, to sign this petition. Let's unite our voice and our efforts to make understanding of the Liberal government and its Minister of Education, Line Beauchamp, the importance of presenting a new action plan quickly, "said the member for Jonquière and spokesperson for the Official opposition in primary and secondary education, Sylvain Gaudreault.
