Quebec, October 28, 2014 -The MP for Terrebonne and Family Opposition Spokesman, Mathieu Traversy, announces that he will table tomorrow a motion asking the Liberal government to reiterate his commitment to Quebec family policy.

"What we see from this government, it is direct attacks on the measures of family policy. The Minister of the Family, Francine Charbonneau, delayed the development of thousands of custody places very awaited by families. In addition, the government suggests the doubt and uncertainty about these services, not to want to cut in the Quebec Parental Insurance Plan. We are told that everything is on the table. Fortunately, mobilization continues throughout Quebec; Families are due to their family policy, "said Mathieu Traversy.

The motion reads as follows:

"That the National Assembly reiterates its commitment to Quebec's family policy;

It urges the government to fully preserve the measures of this policy and to maintain the four pillars of the child care network: quality, universality, accessibility and development. Â»

The official opposition wants all the parties represented to the National Assembly consent to this motion and that the hoped debate takes place.
