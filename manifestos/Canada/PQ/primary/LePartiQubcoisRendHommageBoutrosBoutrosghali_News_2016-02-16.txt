Québec- Learning with regret the death of the former Secretary-General of the United Nations (UN), Mr. Boutros Boutros-Ghali, the leader of the official opposition, Pierre Karl Péladeau, and the spokesman of the Quebecois Parties in International Affairs, Carole Poirier, wanted to make the following statement: 
"On behalf of the Parti Québécois, we offer our sincere condolences to the family and relatives of Mr. Boutros Boutros-Ghali. The commitment of this intellectual, Francophone and Francophile brilliant, as Secretary-General of the United Nations, from 1992 to 1996, marked international diplomacy. 
Mr. Boutros Boutros-Ghali was also the first Secretary General of the International Organization of La Francophonie, from 1997 to 2002. Let's remember that he has ardently defended the Place de la Francophonie on the international scene and that it is As such, moreover, that he has received an honorary doctorate from the University of Quebec, in 2007. "
