Montréal - The leader of the Parti Québécois, Pierre Karl Péladeau, and the member for Taschereau, Agnès Maltese, are paying tribute, on behalf of the staff and members of the Parti Québécois, Jacques Chabot, long-standing political organizer and independence activist The first hour, died suddenly at the end of the afternoon on April 27, 2016. 
"Jacques has contributed all his life to the realization of his vision of an independent, prosperous and solidarity Quebec. It is with great sadness that we learned the news of his death. Today we lose an indefatigable activist, a sensitive organizer and an immense political expertise. All our political family is now in mourning. On behalf of the Parti Québécois team, permanence and parliamentary wing, I offer my sincere condolences to the family, colleagues and friends of Jacques Chabot, "said Pierre Karl Péladeau. 
Jacques Chabot also worked as a political advisor to the member for Taschereau, Agnès Maltais. "Animated by a real passion for political commitment and the service to the population, Jacques deeply aspired to share his knowledge and to form the political succession. It is also a mentor for many that we lost yesterday, "added Maltais.
