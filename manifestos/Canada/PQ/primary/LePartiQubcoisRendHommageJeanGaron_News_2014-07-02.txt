A lawyer and economist, Jean Garon was a member of the riding of Lévis from 1976 to 1998. Minister of Agriculture in the first Government of René Lévesque, he held this position until 1985. He became Minister of Education from 1994 to 1996. Subsequently, he was Mayor of Lévis from 1998 to 2005. 
Jean Garon was at the origin of many reforms for the agricultural environment, including the law on the protection of the territory and agricultural activities, commonly referred to as the Agricultural Zoning Act. This essential law assurated, among other things, the preservation of agricultural land, very coveted at the time. It also allowed the sustainability and the development of sustainable agriculture in Quebec.
