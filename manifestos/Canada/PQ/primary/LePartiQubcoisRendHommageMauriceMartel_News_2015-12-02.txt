Quebec- The Leader of the Official Opposition, Pierre Karl Péladeau, and the hon. Member for Richelieu, Sylvain Rochon, wanted to pay tribute to the former member of Richelieu Maurice Martel, who died on December 1st. 
He was a member of the riding of Richelieu from 1966 to 1970, under the banner of the National Union, and then from 1976 to 1985, under the Quebecois Party. He was also Minister of Revenue in the firms of the Premiers René Lévesque and Pierre Marc Johnson. 
"On behalf of the whole party of the Parti Québécois, I extend my deepest condolences to the family and relatives of Maurice Martel. The region of Sorel-Tracy loses an important builder, a man engaged in his community, during and after his political life. As the first member of the richer of Richelieu, he served his fellow citizens with class throughout his career, as a member of Parliament and as a pharmacist, "said Pierre Karl Péladeau. 
"For a good hour and a half, on the occasion of my election campaign, I had the pleasure of exchanging with him on the issues of the riding, the future of Quebec, the role and the life of MP. He was generous with me of his long experience and advice. It is today a whole region that is mourning. In mourning a sincere politician, a convincing and convincing sovereignist, an excellent pharmacist and a particularly endearing gentleman, "said Sylvain Rochon.
