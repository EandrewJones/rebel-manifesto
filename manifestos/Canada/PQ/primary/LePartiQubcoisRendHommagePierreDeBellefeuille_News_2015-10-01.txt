Quebec- The member for Saint-Jérôme and Leader of the Official Opposition, Pierre Karl Péladeau, wanted to pay tribute to the former member of Deux-Montagnes, Pierre de Bellefeuille, deceased yesterday. 
"On behalf of the whole family of the Parti Québécois, I offer my deepest condolences to Ms. Thérèse Romer, Joint of M. de Bellefeuille, his family and his loved ones. Pierre de Bellefeuille was a member of Parliament engaged for his community and for the independence of Quebec. Man of conviction, he has always ardently defended the French language and our culture, "said Pierre Karl Péladeau. 
After a career in journalism, during which he was writer-in-chief of Maclean's magazine, from 1960 to 1964, then animator of the issue of the Lion, at Radio-Canada, Pierre de Bellefeuille was the first member of the Parti Québécois in the Constituency of two-mountains, from 1976 to 1984. He was first of all the Parliamentary Assistant of the Minister of Cultural Affairs, and then to occupy the same position with the Ministers of Intergovernmental Affairs and International Relations. He left the Caucus of the Parti Québécois in November 1984 and sat as an independent member to the 1985 elections. In 1997, he founded, with Jean-François Nadeau, the satirical newspaper Le Couac. 
"M. de Bellefeuille has also co-written the wearing that independence is not made, it remains in Foisn 1998; This title, very evocative, illustrates the determination of man. I salute the audacity that he had to affirm his opinion on the constitutional status of Quebec with as many space, "concluded Pierre Karl Péladeau.
