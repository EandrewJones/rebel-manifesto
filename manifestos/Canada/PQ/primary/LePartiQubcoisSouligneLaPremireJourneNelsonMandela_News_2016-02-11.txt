Quebec- Parliamentarians of all the political courses represented at the National Assembly pointed out, today in the House, the first day Nelson Mandela, designated as such following the unanimous adoption on 12 June 2015 of a Law sponsored by the member for Bourget and spokesman for the Parti Québécois in immigration and cultural communities, Maka Kotto. 
"The fight led by the one who was the President of South Africa from 1994 to 1998, but especially the father of a multiethnic and democratic South Africa, is a model for those who understand that freedom, justice , human rights, fraternity and equality between peoples constitute universal values. To dedicate this day of February 11 is to recognize his exceptional contribution to the defense and the promotion of these values that are also expensive to the Quebec people, "said Maka Kotto. 
The hon. Member for Bourget also recalled that at the time, Quebec had endorsed Mr. Mandela. "Several Quebec political figures have been in support of Nelson Mandela, including René Lévesque, Robert Bourassa, Jean Doré and Brian Mulroney. Their concrete actions, like others around the world, have been significant and allowed, on 11 February 1990, the liberation of Mr. Mandela after long years of incarceration, after years spent dismantling the institutional political system of Racial segregation was apartheid, "he continued. 
"Nelson Mandela's struggle remains a source of inspiration. It teaches us that whatever the obstacles we encounter, we must always aspire to more freedom, democracy, equality. In this matter, I believe Quebec is a good example for the world, "concluded Maka Kotto.
