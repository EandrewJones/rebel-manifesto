Passage in Rosemont, the leader of the Parti Québécois, Jean-François Lisée, announced that a government of the Partician party would promote the installation of greenhouses on the roofs of urban buildings, giving five-year land taxes. to the owners who install. 
IN SHORT 
"There are thousands of empty roofs in Montreal and everywhere in Quebec. Often, this space could produce fruits and vegetables. At the Québécois Party, we want to create an "urban agriculture" reflex, initially for new constructions. We will encourage people to build greenhouses on the roofs of buildings. How? 'Or' What? By giving the owners who install a greenhouse a lot of taxes for five years, "said Jean-François Lisée. 
The initiative will have multiple benefits. "Encouraging urban agriculture is to encourage local purchase, is fighting the islands of heat is to reduce GHGs. It is to give themselves the means so that Quebeckers have easy access to fresh foods, local products, even in the city! In addition, by twinning this measure with the lunches in schools, everyone will be winning, "chained Jean-François Lisée. 
With this measure, the Parti Québécois focuses particularly on the large projects of commercial or industrial buildings, but does not exclude those of the residential sector. "What we propose to Quebeckers is to start a great movement for urban agriculture. We will gradually go: First, we want the new buildings to include greenhouses, "concluded the leader of the Parti Québécois.
