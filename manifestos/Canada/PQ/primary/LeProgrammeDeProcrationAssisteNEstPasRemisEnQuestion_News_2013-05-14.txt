Québec- The Minister of Health and Social Services, Réjean Hébert has required to take stock of information that circulates about the Quebec Assisted Processing Program. "The assisted procreation program is not questioned. The objective of the mandate conferred on the Commissioner for Health and Wellbeing is to provide me with a view mainly on ethical and social issues and tags that may - where appropriate - frame the program, particularly taking into account the evolution Extremely fast technology, "said the Minister of Health and Social Services. 
"After 3 years of implementation of a new program, it is normal, as evidenced by the law, to take stock of its implementation and see how it is possible to improve the efficiency and the Performance of this program. To do this, it will be interesting to carry out a comparative analysis of what is happening elsewhere in the world and in the Canadian provinces and to gather expert advice on this issue, "said the minister. 
In this context, the Minister considered relevant to seek an independent look and asked the Commissioner to study the situation under Article 9 of LALOI on clinical and research activities. 
The Minister did not impose a fixed schedule for the completion of the work. He pointed out that the Commissioner enjoys all the latitude to carry out his work in the days he deems necessary. 
Remember that the Quebec Assisted Probréation Programs was set up in August 2010. During the 2012-2013 fiscal year, nearly 8,000 in vitro fertilization cycles were completed in Quebec.
