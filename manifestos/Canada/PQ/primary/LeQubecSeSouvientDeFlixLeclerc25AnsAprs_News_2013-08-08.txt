Quebec- On this day marking the aged of the Death of Félix Leclerc, the Government of Quebec wishes to pay tribute to this great artist still present in the spirit and heart of Quebeckers. With this songwriter-songwriter, the Quebec song acquired its first letters of nobility. Throughout his artistic career, Félix Leclerc has made a great deal of shaping our culture. 
"Félix Leclerc has left us an off-dimensional work, vibrant of authenticity and marked with a fleur de lily. We must recognize in him a fervent defender of his country and a lover of his French language, as well as a true ambassador of our cultural identity. Certainly, his shoes have traveled a lot and Quebec has radiated the rhythm of his steps, "said the Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto. 
As early as 1950, this talented poet and this exceptional voice raised the admiration of young Jacques Brel, Georges Brassens and Guy Beart. In Quebec, he paved the way for Gilles Vigneault, Jean-Pierre Ferland and Claude Léveillé.The little happiness; me, my shoes; the tour of the island; His collections and other writings are all precious fragments of our cultural memory. From one decade to another and still today, it remains inspired for many of our Quebec artists. 
Remember that in 1985, Prime Minister René Lévesque appointed the Grand Officer of the National Order of Quebec, the highest distinction awarded by the Quebec government. Added to this mark of recognition a myriad of prices received here and in France and a radiance beyond the Francophonie. Buildings up to the public places, there are no longer the number of locations that bear its name, thus expressing the deep attachment of Quebec for the poet. 
This day of commemoration marks a moment of essential recollection so that Quebec remembers the great Félix Leclerc and its "forty-two thousand" words to dream, to elevate the spirit and to tell beautifully who we are. For a long time, his melodies will resonate in our cultural history.
