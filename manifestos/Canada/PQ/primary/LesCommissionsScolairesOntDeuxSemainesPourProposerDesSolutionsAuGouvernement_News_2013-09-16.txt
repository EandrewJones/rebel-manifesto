Montreal- The Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, accompanied by the Minister of Education, Recreation and Sport, Marie Malavoy, met with representatives of the Federation of School Boards in Quebec and the Association of Anglophone School Commissions Association This morning in Montreal to express the dissatisfaction of the government with regard to how they have assumed the management of the school tax in 2013. 
The Prime Minister and the Minister convened school boards in 15 days for the government of concrete solution for short and medium term. The government will accompany school boards in this work.

The government asks school boards to review the options available to them. However, the Prime Minister and the Minister showed a medium-term opening on the revision of the tax system that is currently prevailing. 
Recall that, in the context of fiscal balance, the government has put an end to additional equalization assistance, a temporary measure introduced in 2006 to reduce the impact of land values increases in Quebec. The perverse effects of this measure justified its abolition by the Government in order to restore the fiscal fairness between the school boards, as well as between the municipalities of the same school board.
