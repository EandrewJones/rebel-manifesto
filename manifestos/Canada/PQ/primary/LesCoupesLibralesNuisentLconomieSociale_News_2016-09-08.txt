Quebec, on September 8, 2016 -Harold Lebel, Member of Parliament for Rimouski and spokesperson for the Social Economy, was present at the World Forum of the Social Economy in Montreal, where several participants emphasized that the abolition of Cré, CLDs and other regional structures harms the development of the social economy. 
"The social economy goes through local development, anchoring projects in the community, democratic management and collective property. It takes mobilizing agents and regional consultation. The abolition of Crees and CLD has had a dramatic effect for the development of the social economy in several regions of Quebec, "said the hon. Member for Rimouski. 
Recall that the recognition of the social economy in Quebec dates from an economic summit held by the Government of the Parti Québécois in 1996. It is also a government of the Parti Québécois who adopted the first framework law on the social economy, In 2013. "We are proud to participate in the 2016 World Social Economy Forum. The Parti Québécois has always supported the development of the social economy and we will continue to do so when we train the government again," concluded Harold Lebel.
