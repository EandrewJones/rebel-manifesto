Montréal- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, and the Minister of International Relations, Francophonie and Foreign Trade and Minister responsible for the Montreal Region, Jean-François Lisé, announce a financial assistance totaling $ 87,000 , as part of the launch of M for Montreal, which will take place from 20 to 23 November 2013. 
The Government of Quebec thus contributes to the rise of emerging artists on the international scene and highlights the cultural vitality of Quebec, which is distinguished by its creativity and its musical avant-garde. "M For Montreal is an exceptional showcase for our new talents and can serve as a bridge for the development of their careers at the international level," said Minister Kotto. 
M for Montreal, in addition to being a crossroads of meetings and exchanges between industry representatives and artists, is a real springboard for our musical succession. "M for Montreal is a flagship time for Quebec's musical culture and for its internationally radiation. The original mission and the avant-garde programming of this event confirm the creativity and awareness of our international cultural metropolis, "added Jean-François Lisée. 
The Cultural Business Development Corporation (SODEC) grants $ 35,000 to M for Montréal, in addition to $ 25,000 from the International Project Assistance Program of the Ministry of Culture and Communications and 27 $ 000 from the Fund of Initiative and Radiation of the Metropolis of the Secretariat in the Metropolitan Area. 
In parallel will be held the 3rd edition of Mundial Montreal, which celebrates all the richness of the world's music. Organized by the founder of M for Montreal, the event benefits from $ 25,000 from the SODEC.
