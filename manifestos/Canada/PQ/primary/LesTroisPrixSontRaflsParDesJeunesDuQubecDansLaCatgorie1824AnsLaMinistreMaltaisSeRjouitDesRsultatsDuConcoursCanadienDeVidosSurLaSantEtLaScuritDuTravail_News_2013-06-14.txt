Quebec- The Minister of Labor, Employment and Social Solidarity and Minister responsible for the Status of Women, Agnès Maltais, is pleased to point out that in category 18-24 years of the Canadian Contest of Videos on Health and Labor safety for young workers, the three winning videos come from Quebec. 
"Quebec stands out clearly by the quality of production of awareness videos that have been submitted to the contest. Our young videographers have enough to be proud of their works, especially since they contribute to informing other young people about their rights to healthy and safe workplaces, "said Minister Maltese. 
Hundreds of videos from across Canada have been submitted to the competition, illustrating the importance of setting up safe working methods, using planned protective equipment, understanding and complying with safety rules and reporting. dangerous situations, incidents or injuries. The Quebec competition component was orchestrated by the CSST. 
"I look forward to the results of this contest, which reflect both the talent of our young Quebeckers, but also undeniably the assiduous awareness work of the CSST. The sustained efforts of the latter with the 24-year-old clientele for many years have brought fruit. The use of social media, by young people, in ever-increasing number, is certainly part of this success, "concluded Minister Maltese. 
The winners of the contest, in category 18-24 years, are: 
1st prize - $ 3,000 Change of life - Jonathan Phoenix Boulard and Rémi D. Vincent- Lanaudière 
2nd prize - $ 2,000 preachments - Aleck Giguère and Anthony Fréchette- Mauricie and Center-du-Québec
