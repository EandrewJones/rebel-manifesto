Quebec- The member for Chicoutimi and Head of the Official Opposition, Stéphane Bédard, the hon. Member for Hochelaga-Maisonneuve and spokesperson for international relations, Carole Poirier, as well as the member for Bourget and spokesperson Immigration, Maka Kotto, expressed, on behalf of their colleagues deputies of the Parti Québécois, their solidarity with the Haitian people, while the earthquake of January 12, 2010 commemorates. 
"Today, all our thoughts go to Haitians, who continue to work on the complete reconstruction of their country. Five years ago, hundreds of thousands of lives have been mowed or upset. We remember those who have disappeared, just as we express our solidarity at survivors, "said Stéphane Bédard. 
"This is not the first time this people is called upon to get up following a tragedy. In complete resilience, each time he looks at the future with determination and courage. January 12, 2010 can not be forgotten; He will remain forever engraved in our memories. Today, big challenges remain. Quebec and international commitment to Haiti must continue and reconstruction, be supported as much as possible, "continued Carole Poirier. 
MPs ask the Couillard government to reiterate in Ottawa the importance of maintaining and increasing humanitarian aid in Haiti. While the federal government lifted the removal moratorium last December, the Quebec government must do everything to support Haitian refugees in their immigration Canada. 
"Our Haitian compatriots, those who have chosen Quebec as a hostland, can count on the support of Quebecers. On this day of commemoration, we are with them; In addition to reaffirming our unwavering friendship, we assure them of our fraternal solidarity, "concluded Maka Kotto.
