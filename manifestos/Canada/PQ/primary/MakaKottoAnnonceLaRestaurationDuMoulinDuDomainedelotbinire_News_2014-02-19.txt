Lotbinière- Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, Announces Funding Assistance of $ 66,600 for the Lotbinius Mill Restoration Project, ranked Heritage Building under the Act Cultural Heritage. 
"Built more than two centuries ago, the Moulin du Domaine-de-Lotbinière is one of the heritage remains of the Chaudière-Appalaches region. By contributing to his restoration, we assure Quebeckers and Quebeckers access to this witness in our history, "said Minister Kotto. 
Located in the Municipality of Lotbinière, on the edge of the St. Lawrence River, the Moulin du Domaine-de-Lotbinière was ranked in 1964. This is an old water mill that was used to grind the flour. Erected in 1799, this imposing building requires significant restoration work related to the structure of the building as such, but also affect masonry, both chimneys and roof. 
The restoration project is estimated at $ 166,500. The contribution of more than $ 65,000 from the Ministry of Culture and Communications is part of the Quebec Cultural Heritage Fund. The owner of the house completes the financing. 
Additional information regarding the Moulin du Lotbinière as well as all heritage elements entered in the cultural heritage register are available on the website of the Cultural Heritage of Quebec (RPCQ), atwww.patrimoine-cultural.gouv. QC.ca. This dissemination tool of the Ministry of Culture and Communications makes it possible to discover the richness and diversity of the Quebec cultural heritage by making it accessible to all.
