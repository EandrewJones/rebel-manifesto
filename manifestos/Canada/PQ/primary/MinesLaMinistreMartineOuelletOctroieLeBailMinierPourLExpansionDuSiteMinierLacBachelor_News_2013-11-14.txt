Montréal- The Minister of Natural Resources, Head of Nordic Development, Mrs. Martine Ouellet, accompanied by the Parliamentary Assistant to the Minister of Natural Resources (Northern Affairs Component) and MP, Mr. Luc Ferland, today announced today The granting of a mining lease at Métanor Resources inc. For the expansion of its gold mining site Bachelor. 
"The expansion of this mining site means maintaining 250 jobs for the next four years. This is good news for the Nord-du-Québec region where the mining industry is an important driver of economic development, "said Martine Ouellet. 
Located in Nord-du-Québec, about 225 km from Val-d'Or, the Mine Lac Bachelor will immediately begin its extraction activities. The gold ore will be treated and melted at the treatment plant on the site of the mine. Total early production is more than 200,000 ounces of gold. 
The company's investment for the Mine Lake Bachelor expansion project is $ 96 million. 
"The Expansion of the Bachelor Lake Site will have a considerable impact for the North-du-Québec region. This is an important development lever. Our government is placing concrete actions to contribute with partners to grow in the region. Our goal is to deploy the tremendous potential for economic and social development of the North. And the least we can say is that the Métanor resource project responds to this goal, "concluded Luc Ferland.
