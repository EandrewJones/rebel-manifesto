Quebec- The Minister of Finance and the Economy, Mr. Nicolas Marceau, today presented the economic and financial update of spring 2013. "I confirm that the budget balance will be reached in 2013-2014 and that It will be maintained thereafter, as planned in the last November budget. For the year 2012-2013, the deficit goal is maintained at $ 1.5 billion, "he said. 
In terms of economic growth, after an increase of 1.0% of real GDP in 2012, economic growth in Quebec is expected to be 1.3% in 2013 and 1.8% in 2014. For these two years. , it is a downward revision of 0.2 percentage points in relation to the budget forecast of November 2012. The minister indicated that the economy continues to grow, but at a more moderate pace than expected Due to the fragile global economic environment and uncertainties related to the financial and budgetary situation in the euro zone and the United States. 
In addition, the minister emphasized that the labor market remains robust in Quebec. "It has been created 30,800 jobs in 2012, an increase of 0.8% compared to 2011. The unemployment rate remained stable at 7.8%, but it should fold next year, passing 7 , 5% in 2013 and 7.4% in 2014, "he said. 
The minister confirmed that the government intends to respect the budgetary objectives established last November, despite adjustments to government revenues. Follow-up of budget revenues and the downward revision of the economic outlook led to a decline in self-government revenues of the order of $ 250 million in 2012-2013 and $ 565 million in 2013-2014. 
"These gaps to be gained will be fully offset by a downward revision of the debt service because of low interest rates, an increase in equalization revenues resulting from the updating of economic and fiscal data and finally by the Partial use of the provisions for contingencies that had been incorporated into the last November financial framework, "he explained. 
The minister has indicated that rigorous spending management should be maintained. "The government has asked the necessary gestures to resort significant overruns in September 2012. The expenditure objectives established last November are retained. The level of program expenditures in 2012-2013 is still expected at $ 62,642 million, a growth of 1.9%. Growth will also be 1.9% in 2013-2014, "he explained. 
The minister reiterated that the government is pursuing debt reduction goals, to reduce gross debt to 45% of GDP and debt representing accumulated deficits at 17% of GDP as of March 31, 2026. 
He pointed out that the incomes arising from the price indexation of heritage electricity as of 2014, all the mining royalties beginning in 2015-16, the incomes arising from the rise in net income of Hydro-Qu√©bec linked At the closure of the Gentilly-2 nuclear power plant from 2017-2018 and revenues arising from the increase in the specific tax on alcoholic beverages will be paid to the Generation Fund. For example, for 2013-2014 and 2014-2015, amounts of nearly $ 1.4 billion will be paid to the Debt Reduction Generations Fund. 
The minister recalled that at the end of the higher education summit, held on February 25th and 26th, the government confirmed that it will invest nearly $ 1.8 billion over seven years For the financing of the universities and that it will index the tuition fee to the pace of the growth of disposable income per capita to take into account the capacity to pay Quebeckers. "Our government confirms its commitment to universities by providing stable and sustainable funding for an average annual growth of 4.2% by 2018-2019," he explained. 
The Government has also undertaken to improve financial assistance for students, including an increase in the income threshold beyond which parental contribution to the loan and scholarship system is considered a little more than $ 28,000 at $ 45,000. Additional improvements will be made to study financial assistance. 
"In the long run, the increase in the threshold of the parental contribution will benefit some 49,000 students, who will see their financial assistance increase by $ 1,350 per year on average," said the minister. 
The bonus of financial assistance to studies will be offset by reducing the tax credit rate for tuition and testing of 20% to 8%. 
"After four years of deficits, Quebec will return to a balanced budget in 2013-2014. Today's update keeps this goal while taking into account the evolution of economic conditions. It is necessary to pursue rigorous management of our expenses. Thus, we will reach the objectives we have set ourselves and we will find a balanced budget, "concluded the minister.
