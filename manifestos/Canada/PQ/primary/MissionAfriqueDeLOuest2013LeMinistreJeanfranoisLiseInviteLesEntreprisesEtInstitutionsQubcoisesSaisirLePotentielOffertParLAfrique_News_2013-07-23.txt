Quebec- In preparation for the multisectoral mission in West Africa that it will lead from 6 to 15 September, the Minister of International Relations, Francophonie and Foreign Trade, Jean-François Lisée, invites Quebec companies to enjoy Possible business and institutional business opportunities in this African region. 
"Africa is the continent that, at the moment, is in full take-off. It is time to demonstrate that in Quebec we have the will, the lucidity and the ability to be part of this development, "said the minister in a short audio message used to explain the importance of this mission ( Why Africa.mp3). 
Participants in this commercial and institutional mission can hire or consolidate business ties in Senegal (Dakar), Côte d'Ivoire (Abidjan) and Burkina Faso (Ouagadougou), three countries. High development potential. 
Companies and institutions wishing to obtain more information on this mission or wishing to participate are invited to consult with: www.export.gouv.qc.ca/en/agenda/mission-en-Africa. 
The media who wish to obtain more details on this mission may seek an interview with the Export-Quebec Director General, Alain Proulx, by contacting:  Solen Lemay, the Communications Branch of the Ministry of International Relations, at 418 649-2400, ext. 57550, or by email to solene.lemay@mri.gouv.qc.ca before July 26, 2013 at 4 pm.
