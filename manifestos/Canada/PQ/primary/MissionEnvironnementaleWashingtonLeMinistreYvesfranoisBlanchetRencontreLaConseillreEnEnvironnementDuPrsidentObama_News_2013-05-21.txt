The fight against climate change has been at the heart of the interviews between the Minister and the President Obama's advisor. Minister Blanchet reported on the many initiatives in Quebec to reduce its greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, including the implementation of the Quebec Cap System and GHG Emissions Trading. Quebec will link its carbon market with that of California on January 1, 2014 as part of the Western Climate Initiative and is actively working to expand this market, already the largest in North America with about one seventh population of Canada and United States, other partners on the continent. 
The minister also mentioned that transportation electrification was one of the priorities of the Government of Quebec. Minister Blanchet and Ms. Sutley discussed the importance of reducing the reliance on their respective jurisdictions to fossil energies, which are largely responsible for climate upheavals that afflict our continent and the world. 
The minister welcomed the intent expressed by President Obama, during his last speech on the State of the Union, to put the fight against climate change at the forefront of the American political agenda. "I expressed to Mrs. Sutley our desire to go further.
