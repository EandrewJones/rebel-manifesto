Quebec, February 4, 2019 - 
The member for Matane-Matapédia and parliamentary leader of the Parti Québécois, Pascal Bérubé, brought some adjustments to the distribution of spokespersons, on the eve of the beginning of the parliamentary session. 
· Méganne Perry Melançon, Member of Parliament for Gaspé, will be a spokesperson for culture and communications; · Joël Arseneau, a member of the Madeleine Islands, became spokesperson on French language; · Harold Lebel, MP From Rimouski, will bring regional development and rural development files; · Martin Ouellet, Member of Parliament for René-Lévesque, will be responsible for the issues affecting the work. 
"The two newly elected deputies of our political training, Megan Perry Melançon and Joël Arseneau, will have more responsibilities, having quickly proven their value and their ability to defend the files that are entrusted to them. Culture, communications and the French language are areas of primary importance to the Parti Québécois; We today associate them with spokespersons, we can no longer vigilant and dynamic, who will not hesitate to climb to the niche to assert our points of view, "first indicated the parliamentary leader. 
"Harold Lebel takes over of regional development and rural development. He has already made a first proposal in connection with these, at our missing caucus, in order to respond to the labor shortage and to encourage seniors to remain in the labor market, two major challenges in our regions. Finally, Martin Ouellet will henceforth be responsible for the files related to the work; It is an area that he knows well and for whom his expertise will be used, "added Pascal Bérubé.
