Quebec- The member for Berthier and spokesman of the Parti Québécois in agriculture, food, SMEs, entrepreneurship and innovation, André Villeneuve, invites the Government to support his requested motion from the federal government to maintain integral of supply management. The motion, which will be debated tomorrow, reads as follows: 
"That in the context of the Trans-Pacific Partnership Negotiations, the National Assembly requires the federal government to maintain the supply management and that it does not make any concession with increased access to the market for dairy products. poultry and eggs, as demanded by agricultural producers. » 
The member wants the National Assembly to speak in a united voice to defend agricultural producers. "Producers mobilize in front of a federal government that ignores their interests. By this motion, we offer to the Minister of Agriculture, Pierre Paradis, as well as other political parties the possibility of making a common front so that the National Assembly gives its support to farmers, who claim the full maintenance of the management of offer. Any concession would have disastrous consequences for producers, "said André Villeneuve. 
In Quebec, there are more than 7000 farms whose production is under the management of the offer. The survival of many of them will be threatened if Canada has gave increased access to the market through the Trans-Pacific Partnership. 
"The thousands of Quebec farmers who will be affected if the federal government abdicates its responsibilities need the unanimous support of the members of the National Assembly. I invite parliamentarians to send a clear message to Ottawa: we should not do any concession on supply management, "concluded the member for Berthier.
