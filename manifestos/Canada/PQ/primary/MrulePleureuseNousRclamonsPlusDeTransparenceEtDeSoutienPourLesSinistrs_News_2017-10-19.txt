Quebec, on October 19, 2017 -The Member of Parliament for Rimouski, Harold Lebel, and the official habitation opposition spokesman, Catherine Fournier, ask the Government to make public the report of the Interministerial Committee established in 2016 in charge of Document the problem of mourning mélule, a fungus nicknamed "building cancer". They claim the same breath a plan to combat this scourge and a welfare fund for the victims. 
IN SHORT 
Crying Mélule is a fungus affecting a higher number of buildings in Quebec. Detected too late, it can result in the destruction of affected buildings, purely and simply. Caring allergies and other other respiratory problems, it also represents a real public health issue. 
"For three years, I have been with a couple with the mourning merciless in a municipality of my riding. Like many other Quebec families, he feels alone in the face of this situation, demuni, without means. All these people demand a rapid action of the government, a clear support that will help them cross this test, difficult both physically, moral and financial, "mentioned the hon. Member for Rimouski. 
"Owners faced with mourning merciless must often leave their home. Either temporarily, if a decontamination may be sufficient, or permanently, if they are obliged to demolish. And they must assume all the costs! Private insurance does not cover this type of claim, and public safety does not consider mourning merciless as a mass sinister. This situation therefore causes a lot of distress. Some people have nowhere to go. Others had to declare bankruptcy. We must help them, "Catherine insisted Fournier.
