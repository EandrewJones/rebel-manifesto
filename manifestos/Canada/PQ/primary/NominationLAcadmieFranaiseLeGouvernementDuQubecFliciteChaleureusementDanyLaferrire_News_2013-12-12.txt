Quebec- The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, warmly congratulates the author of Haitian origin, Dany Laferrière, for his election to the French Academy. He becomes the first Quebecer and the first Haitian to enter this institution created in 1635 by Cardinal de Richelieu. 
"This prestigious election comes once again highlight the incredible talent of Quebec artists and their radiation abroad. The work of Mr. Laferrière does honor the French language. I am convinced that he will bring to the French Academy an exceptional contribution and to make an original voice heard, "said the Prime Minister. 
"I feel a mixture of pride, admiration and recognition for what has just done Dany Laferrière. It is Quebec literature and Haitian literature that come with him at the French Academy. It is both his sensitivity, his passion for the imagination and understanding of the universality of the literature that will make it an outstanding academician. It's a big news for Quebec culture, "said Maka Kotto. 
Writer, journalist and columnist, Dany Laferrière grew up in the village of Petit-Goâve, Haiti. He immigrated to Quebec in 1976. Nine years later, his first novel, how to make love with a Negro without getting tired, made a successful entry into the Quebec literary landscape. He also made known and, above all, recognize in France and in La Francophonie. 
Mr. Laferrière received several career awards, including the Governor General's Award in the French Language Youth Literature category, for his album I'm crazy about Vava; The Medici Award and the Grand Prix of the Book of Montreal for the return of return. He was also a holy personality of the year 2009 the press / Radio-Canada and received the Blue Metropolis International Literary Grand Prix and an honorary doctorate from the University of Quebec in Rimouski. Last April, he also chaired, in Haiti, literary meetings between Haitian and Quebec writers.
