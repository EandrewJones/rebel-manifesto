Port-Daniel-Gascons- The Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, accompanied by the Minister Delegate to the Regions and Minister responsible for the Gaspé-Îles-de-la-Madeleine Region, Gaétan Lelièvre, and the hon. Member for Bonaventure, Sylvain Roy , confirmed the agreement in principle with the Ciment McInnis for the construction of a cement plant in Port-Daniel-Gascons, in Gaspésie. 
The Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of the Quebec Depot and Investment, Michael Sabia, President and CEO of Investissement Québec, Mario Albert, and the President of the Beaudier Group, Laurent Beaudoin, were also present at this ad . 
This project, estimated at $ 1 billion, will result in the creation of 2300 jobs during the construction phase, which will begin in the spring of 2014, and some 400 jobs will be linked to the implementation of the cement plant, scheduled for the spring of 2016. 
The new cement plant will have an annual capacity of 2.2 million tonnes of cement. The announced project also aims to exploit a limestone career nearby. This deposit represents a supplies of approximately 100 years. In addition, a maritime terminal will be landscaped in the region to export cement on the east coast of the United States, the main foreign market coveted by the company.
