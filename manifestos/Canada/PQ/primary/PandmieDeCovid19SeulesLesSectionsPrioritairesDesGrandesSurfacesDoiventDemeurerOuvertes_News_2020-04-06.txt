Quebec, on April 6, 2020 - In the collective interest, the member for Matane-Matapédia and parliamentary leader of the Parti Québécois, Pascal Bérubé, as well as the member for René-Lévesque and spokesperson for economy and finance, Martin Ouellet, asking the Government of Quebec to demand without delay the closure of the non-priority sections of the highly surface shops, that is to say of all that does not concern the grocery store, the pharmacy or the hardware store. 
IN SHORT 
According to Pascal Bérubé, the message of public health did not obviously rendered in too many high-area businesses. "Quebec is on break until May 4th; We must therefore all reduce our trips and activities to their strict minimum. Still, some highly surface shops allow their guests to shop shirts, jewelry, video game consoles ... This deplorable situation generates a superfluous traffic, putting unnecessary at risk for the health of employees and customers who have come to obtain essential goods. For our good to all, it must stop immediately, "he claimed. 
"At the beginning of the crisis, the decision to leave the businesses with a completely open surface could understand each other: we had to allow everyone to obtain what to start the confinement period and respect the instructions. But the time has now come to revise the list of priority services to adapt it to the reality of this crisis that extends, "added the parliamentary leader. 
Moreover, esteem Martin Ouellet, it is a form of unfair competition for SMEs that sell these same non-priority properties and who must remain closed. "Our entrepreneurs should not be punished more! In their interest, it's time to force supermarkets to close now the non-priority sections. The goods will remain available online; Consumers will not be deprived of it. It is a matter of justice and equity as well as public health, "summed up the hon. Member for René-Lévesque.
