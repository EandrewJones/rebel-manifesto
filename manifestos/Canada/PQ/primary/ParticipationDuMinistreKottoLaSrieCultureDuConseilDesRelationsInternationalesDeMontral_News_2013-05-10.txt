Quebec- The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, was the guest of the Montreal International Relations Council (CORIM) today, during an organized causation luncheon as part of the Culture series. 
Nearly 350 people attended the Conference entitled Culture A state affair. The minister has addressed various topics such as sovereignist governance and cultural implications, support for the creation, production and dissemination of Quebec culture, development and sustainable influence in Quebec and the International, the challenges of technological changes and strengthening the relationship between culture and education. 
"I thank the Corim for inviting me to address all of these influential actors in the business community and the metropolis' arts. We know, the areas of culture and communications are important development levers for Quebec and its influence on the international scene. That is why it is necessary to reaffirm our willingness to make culture a matter of state, "said Minister Kotto. 
Founded in 1985, the Corim is a private organization whose mission is to promote greater knowledge of international affairs and to create a closer collaboration between the various environments on international issues and partnerships.
