Montreal - The leader of the Parti Québécois, Pauline Marois, is committed to setting up a refundable tax credit to promote the teaching and learning of the arts. "We want to facilitate artistic practice among young people. We will support financially parents and we will help children flourish by the arts, "she explained. 
This refundable tax credit of 500 per child aged 5 to 16 will apply for any registration to an artistic practice for families whose annual income is less than $ 130,000. This measure will be beneficial, we hope for parents of about 250,000 young people from this age group and cost $ 20 million a year. 
"The Parti Québécois has always been very committed to culture. This allows us to dream and imagine new horizons. Culture is also one of the pillars of our economy. With this tax credit, we will encourage the development of our children as well as the outbreak of future talents, artists and creators of tomorrow. Among the young people aged 5 to 16 are the comedians, the musicians, circus artists, artistic creators and painters who will make us dream and shine Quebec culture around the world in 20 or 30 years, "concluded Pauline Marois.
