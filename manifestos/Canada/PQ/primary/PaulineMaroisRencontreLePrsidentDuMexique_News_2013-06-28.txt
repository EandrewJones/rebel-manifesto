Mexico-Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, met with Mexico City, the President of Mexico, Enrique Peña Nieto. The discussions focused on the means of increasing trade between Quebec and Mexico, as well as on various issues of a political, economic, cultural and social nature. 
At the out of a very warm 45-minute meeting with President Peña Nieto, the Premier said, "I have seen that Quebec is a major partner for Mexico and confirm the collaborative links and friendship that unite us for several decades. We are also considering the future of our exchanges with the same optimism, in fields of activity of common interest such as aeronautics, multimedia, culture, life sciences, energy and technologies some information. » 
Ms. Marois also delivered an important economic speech to nearly 350 Mexican and Quebec business people. It again emphasized the need to strengthen Quebec's economic relations with its Latin American partner under NAFTA. 
The Prime Minister continued its series of bilateral interviews with other important political actors, including the Secretary of External Relations, José Antonio Meade, and the Secretary to the Economy, Iidefonso G. Villarreal. 
For its part, the Minister Delegate to Industrial Policy and the Quebec Economic Development Bank, Élaine Zakaïb, met with several representatives of the Economic Environment of Mexico with Quebec International and Quebec Manufacturers and Exporters. 
Since the establishment of the Quebec Delegation in Mexico City, 1980, Quebec and Mexico have developed a dynamic and fruitful cooperation. To date, both partners have signed 17 cooperation agreements, including the most recent in the areas of higher education, research and innovation.
