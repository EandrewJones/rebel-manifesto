Quebec- The Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, expressed, on behalf of the Quebec people, his very great sadness as a result of the announcement of the death of the iconic figure of the fight against apartheid and former president of the South Africa, Nelson Mandela. 
"Today, the whole world pays tribute to this man who has devoted his life to his ideals of peace, equality and dignity. It is with a particular emotion that we join our thoughts to those of the South African people and that we transmit our most sincere condolences. As the whole of the international community, we are deeply saddened by the loss of this character larger than nature, "said Marois. 
"Some women and some outstanding men have this ability to orient their society towards better tomorrow. Animated by an insatiable thirst for justice, with a moral force, intelligence, a determination and exemplary courage, Nelson Mandela was one of those exceptional beings; His struggle has transformed the story of a whole continent, "continued the premier. 
Training Counsel, Nelson Mandela committed in 1948 in the fight against racial segregation in South Africa, while the apartheid regime took shape. Arrested in 1963, he was imprisoned for 27 years. In 1990, he was the apostle of reconciliation with the Government of his country before becoming the first black president of South Africa, in 1994. Nobel Peace Prize in 1993, he has made the fight against HIV / AIDS its last big fight.
