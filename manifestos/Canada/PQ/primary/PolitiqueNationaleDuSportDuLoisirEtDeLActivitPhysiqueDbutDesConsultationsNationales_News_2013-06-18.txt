Quebec- The Minister of Education, Recreation and Sport, Mrs. Marie Malavoy, launched this morning the first of the three national consultations announced in the wake of the Green Paper Title and the pleasure of moving: towards a national policy of the Sport, leisure and physical activity. For the occasion, it was accompanied by its parliamentary assistant and MP, Mr. Sylvain Pagé, who received the mandate to conduct national and regional meetings. 
"The views that will be shared during these consultations will bring valuable lighting for the development of our future national sport, leisure and physical activity. Let us together give the objective that this policy is a reflection of this society that we want to build, a company in action, which takes on hand and which goes at the end of its potential, "concluded Malavoy minister. 
Remember that the first two national meetings, which are held in Quebec City on June 18 and 20, respectively bear the development of sports practice, on the promotion of a physically active lifestyle and the development of recreational activities. During the third meeting, which will take place in Montreal on June 21, the partners will discuss the development of sports excellence. 
"We meet this week of the inescapable players of sport, leisure and physical activity that, strong of the expertise they have developed, will help us find the keys to give Quebeckers the taste of moving. I want to thank them for their presence at these consultations, which is eloquently demonstrating their commitment. This presence is the first step in the success of our project, "concluded Mr. Pagé.
