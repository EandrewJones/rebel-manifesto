Quebec- The member for René-Lévesque, Mr. Marjolain Dufour, is proud to give, on behalf of the Minister of Public Safety, Mr. Stéphane Bergeron, the payment of $ 225,000 to the village of Pointe-aux-Outardes. This amount represents the last payment to which the Village is entitled under the Memorandum of Understanding with the Ministry of Public Safety (MSP) for the realization of works aimed at preventing and mitigating the risk of erosion of the river. Saint-Laurent and for the displacement of main residences. 
"I want to emphasize the excellent collaboration of the village of Pointe-aux-Outardes, which made it possible to carry out this work and to help to act proactively in civil security," said Minister Bergeron. 
"The village of Pointe-aux-Outardes must deal with certain natural risks, including the erosion of the banks. This is why the planning and implementation of sustainable and efficient solutions are paramount to ensure a safer environment at the community, "said the member for René-Lévesque. 
According to the Memorandum of Understanding, the MSP undertakes to pay financial assistance equal to the eligible costs of the activities referred to in this Protocol, less a financial contribution set according to the demographic assessment. A first amount of $ 1,000,000 was paid to the Village of Pointe-aux-Outardes on April 15, 2010 for carrying out work on the prevention and mitigation of the risk of erosion of the St. Lawrence River and for the Moving main residences. 
It should be remembered that this Memorandum of Understanding is part of the work of the prevention of the prevention of the main natural risks, which has been designed to guide government and municipal actions in the erosion of the coast, landslides and floods. This framework is aimed at implementing integrated risk management and is based on a risk analysis, solutions analysis and implementation.
