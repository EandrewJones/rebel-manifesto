Quebec- Minister responsible for democratic institutions and citizen participation, Bernard Drainville, decided, for security reasons, to cancel his participation in a debate on the Charter of Quebec values, which was to hold this afternoon. Concordia University. The event organized by the student association of graduates and on the Charter of Québec values had to bring together Minister Drainville, the Liberal MP Kathleen Weil, and André Frappier of Quebec Solidaire. 
For the occasion, an invitation to demonstrate against the Charter has been launched on social networks. Organizers of this event have not dismissed "disrupt" the event or even preventing the outfit. Given this information, the Minister's Office wanted to ensure that Concordia University could ensure that the security of people attending the debate and the panelists. The university was unable to offer these security guarantees. In such circumstances, Bernard Drainville found it preferable to cancel his participation. 
"Since the beginning of the debate on the Charter of Quebec values, I have always said that the tone would be as important as the substance and that it would be necessary to debate in respect of the opinions of all. However, we see here a group of people who threaten to disrupt the debate to deprive ourselves of a democratic discussion. I sincerely regret this situation, "said Bernard Drainville. "I made the decision not to participate in the debate in order to avoid that unsightly incidents to tarnish the discussion around the Charter, which has so far unfolded essentially in peace, tolerance and the respect. »
