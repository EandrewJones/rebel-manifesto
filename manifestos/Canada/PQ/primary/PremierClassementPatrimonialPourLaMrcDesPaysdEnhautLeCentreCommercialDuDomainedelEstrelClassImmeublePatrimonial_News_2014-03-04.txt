Appeared in a context of multiplication of hotels and country houses in the Laurentians in 1936 and in 1937, this building is a heritage interest for its historical and architectural values. Indeed, it is one of the last witnesses of the prestigious resort whole that this area has been. In addition, the commercial center of the domain-de-l'Eserel is an early manifestation of modern architecture in Quebec. Naval references present in its architecture suggest the luxurious character of the ships and testify to the influence of the Streamline style of Art Deco in Quebec. 
"Not only is the mall of great value for the city of Sainte-Marguerite-du-Lac-Masson, but the environment in which it is equally exceptional. I look forward to this announcement that protects this emblematic building, erected in the Laurentians during the inter-wars, "adds the member for Bertrand, Claude Cousineau. 
The ranking applies to the outer envelope of the building and parts of the interior, including the Blue Room (former Cabaret restaurant) and the main stairwell. It should be noted that other parts of the building are cited by the city, the rectangular appendix of a floor located at the back, the cinema and the hall of the carousel. This ranking and this quote aim to guarantee its protection, knowledge and development as well as its transmission to future generations.
