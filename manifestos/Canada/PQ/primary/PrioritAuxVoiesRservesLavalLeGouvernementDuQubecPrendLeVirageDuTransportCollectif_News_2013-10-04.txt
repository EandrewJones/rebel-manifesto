"The announcement of the government goes exactly in the sense of the concerns of the citizens of Laval who participated in the public consultation on collective transport", Leo Bureau-Blouin 
Laval- The member for Laval-des-Rapides and Parliamentary Assistant to the Prime Minister (youth component), Leo Bureau-Blouin, welcomes the announcement of the Minister of Transport, Sylvain Gaudreault, concerning the establishment of 208 km of reserved lanes in Greater Montreal over the next three years representing an investment of more than $ 80 million. 
The Minister responds to several requests made by Mr. Bureau-Blouin as part of his report on public consultation on Laval collective transportation. Indeed, last March, the member launched a large public consultation that culminated by the filing of the said report on June 28th. It is therefore with satisfaction that Mr. Bureau-Blouin welcomes the many reserved lanes projects that will see the day by 2016 in Laval: 
"Laval citizens were clear during the public consultation I conducted this winter: it is necessary to make collective transport more attractive and more efficient. In this sense, the priority given to the routes reserved for Laval corresponds to the expectations of the population for the development of our transport infrastructures, "explains Mr. Bureau-Blouin.
