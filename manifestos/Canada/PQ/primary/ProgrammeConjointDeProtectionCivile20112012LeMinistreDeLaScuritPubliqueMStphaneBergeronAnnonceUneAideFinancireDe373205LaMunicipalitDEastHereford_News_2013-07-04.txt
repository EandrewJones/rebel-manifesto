Quebec- The Minister of Public Safety, Stéphane Bergeron, in conjunction with the Minister responsible for the Estrie Region, Mr. Réjean Hébert, announces a financial assistance of $ 3,732.05 to the Municipality of East Hereford under the Joint Civil Protection Program (CPPC) 2011-2012. 
"The Municipality of East Hereford benefits from this financial assistance for the project to develop an emergency plan, and thus facing possible claims more efficient," said Mr. Hébert. 
Thanks to this plan, stakeholders will be better equipped to limit risks. Also, this project will allow the coordination of available forces and the implementation of clear procedures. 
"We are proud to support municipalities that put in place measures to prevent a claim from or, where appropriate, to facilitate the return to the normal life of the victims affected by such circumstances. I want to emphasize the efforts made by the Municipality of East Hereford to ensure the safety of citizens of the region, "said Minister Bergeron. 
Recall that CPPC is a federal program for municipalities, regional county municipalities, metropolitan communities, and government departments and agencies. This program, under the responsibility of Public Security Canada, is administered in Quebec by the Ministry of Public Safety. It aims to encourage efforts to improve and increase emergency response capacity through civil security projects.
