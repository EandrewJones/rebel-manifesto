Quebec- Minister responsible for the Mauricie Region and the Center-du-Québec Region, Yves-François Blanchet, announces, on behalf of the Minister of Public Safety, Stéphane Bergeron, a financial assistance of 9 $ 617.83 at the City of Saint-Tite under the Joint Civil Protection Program (CPPC) 2011-2012. 
"The City of Saint-Tite benefits from this financial assistance for the project to develop a coordinating center, and thus facing possible claims more efficient," Blanchet said. 
This project will promptly communicate with local stakeholders and ministries from different government levels, such as civil security, environment and transport. Coordination of actions and interventions will be facilitated since physical and human resources will be gathered in one place. 
"We are proud to support municipalities that put in place measures to prevent a claim from or, where appropriate, to facilitate the return to the normal life of the victims affected by such circumstances. I want to emphasize the efforts made by the City of Saint-Tite to ensure the safety of citizens of the region, "said Minister Bergeron. 
Recall that CPPC is a federal program for municipalities, regional county municipalities, metropolitan communities, and government departments and agencies. This program, under the responsibility of Public Security Canada, is administered in Quebec by the Ministry of Public Safety. It aims to encourage efforts to improve and increase emergency response capacity through civil security projects.
