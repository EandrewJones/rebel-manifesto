Quebec- The Government Manager of the Laurentians and Labelle Member Region, Mr. Sylvain Pagé, said, on behalf of the Minister of Public Safety, Mr. Stéphane Bergeron, a financial assistance of $ 23,353.79 to the City of Boisbriand under the Joint Civil Protection Program (CPPC) 2011-2012. 
"The city of Boisbriand enjoys this financial assistance for the project to revise the municipal civil security plan, and thus facing possible claims more efficient," said Mr. Pagé. 
This project will allow stakeholders to be better equipped to intervene in an exceptional situation. 
"We are proud to support municipalities that put in place measures to prevent a claim from or, where appropriate, to facilitate the return to the normal life of the victims affected by such circumstances. I want to emphasize the efforts made by the City of Boisbriand to ensure the safety of citizens of the region, "said Minister Bergeron. 
Recall that CPPC is a federal program for municipalities, regional county municipalities, metropolitan communities, and government departments and agencies. This program, under the responsibility of Public Security Canada, is administered in Quebec by the Ministry of Public Safety. It aims to encourage efforts to improve and increase emergency response capacity through civil security projects.
