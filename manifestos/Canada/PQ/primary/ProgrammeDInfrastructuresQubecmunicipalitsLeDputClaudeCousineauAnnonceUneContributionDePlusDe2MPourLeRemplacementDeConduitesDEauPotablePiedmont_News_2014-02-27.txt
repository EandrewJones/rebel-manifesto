Piedmont- The member for Bertrand and Vice-President of the National Assembly, Mr. Claude Cousineau, on behalf of the Minister of Municipal Affairs, Regions and Occupation of the Territory, Minister of Transport and MP, Mr. Sylvain Gaudreault, Is happy to announce that the municipality of Piedmont will receive a financial contribution of $ 2,006,000 for the replacement of drinking water pipes. This assistance is granted as part of the Québec-Municipalities Infrastructure Program (PIQM). 
More specifically, the project aims to replace drinking water pipes under the Beausoleil, Belvedere, Corniche, Cape Town, and Pigoons. At the end of this work, drinking water pipes for nearly two kilometers and a half will be renewed. 
"I am very pleased with the financial support granted to the municipality of Piedmont. Our government recognizes the need to renew and develop drinking water, wastewater and stormwater infrastructure in communities across Quebec. That's why, in partnership with municipalities, we want to make sure that citizens benefit from quality sustainable public services for current and future generations, "said Cousineau deputy. 
"It is with great pleasure that I learned that the Ministry of Municipal Affairs, Regions and Occupancy, has responded to our grant application for the establishment of an aqueduct network in The Mont-Belvédère sector, because currently this sector is served by high density polyethylene pipes of very small diameters and located back lots. The Mayor of Piedmont, Clement Cardin said. 
The realization of this project requires maximum government financial assistance of $ 2,006,000 under the PIQM 1.5 component. With a total envelope of $ 462 million, this new component not only ensures the sustainability of the infrastructure, but also to enhance the quality of life of the population and to create many jobs in all regions of the region. Quebec. 
The support of the Government of Quebec to this project is part of the 2013-2023 Quebec Infrastructure Plan (PQI) made public in April 2013. It should be remembered that this ten-year plan will eventually result in investments of 8.1 billion dollars for the municipal infrastructure component. It will have a major economic impact throughout Quebec, with nearly 60,000 jobs created or maintained on average per year. 
The PQI also marks a turn in the way of investing and managing public infrastructure. This new way of doing things will make it possible to evaluate the needs, according to the priorities of Quebecers, to measure the costs associated with them and to make informed choices.
