Beaupré- The Prime Minister of Quebec and MP from Charlevoix-Côte-de-Beaupré, Pauline Marois, has given a sum of $ 39,150 at the youth house the enigma of Beaupré. This amount comes from the sale of property seized by the various police services of Quebec and property seized by the courts. 
"The prevention of crime is a priority for the Government of Quebec, and it is with pride that I put on this amount to the youth house the enigma. This money will allow stakeholders to continue their mission, including the TAG - street work project whose goal is to listen, to inform, to raise awareness and support people who live certain difficulties or who are in social rupture, so To promote their care, "said the member for Charlevoix-Côte-de-Beaupré. 
The young house The enigma of Beaupré is an organization whose commitment and actions towards the community are now recognized. This financial assistance will serve to offer an animated place to young people aged 11 to 17 to promote their autonomy and provide them with the tools to lead their lives. 
Since 1999, a proportion of 25% of the net product of the property obtained illegally, and become the property of the state following police operations, is discount each year to community organizations in Quebec. This program provides financial assistance to those who work with young people for the realization of a project in crime prevention. 
"I want to remember that the Quebec government was the first, and long the only one, to redistribute some of the amounts from the resale of the property acquired during criminal activities. Thus, organizations working with victims of crime and those dedicated to the prevention of crime can take advantage of it, "concluded the Prime Minister.
