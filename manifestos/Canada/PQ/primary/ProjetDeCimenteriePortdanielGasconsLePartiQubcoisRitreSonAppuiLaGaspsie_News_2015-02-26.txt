Quebec- The hon. Member for Bonaventure, Sylvain Roy, said that the Quebec party will support Bill 37, which subjects the Port-Daniel-Gascons Cement and Marine Terminal project to the authorization regime provided for in Article 22 of Laloi on the quality of the environment. 
"We are very proud to see the cement plant project take shape. It is our government that, with the partners and the Gaspé population, had relaunched it; We believed, Prime Minister Pauline Marois in mind, the economic and social development of Gaspésie. The establishment of a cement plant in Port-Daniel-Gascons will create sustainable jobs and diversify the economy of the region. In this sense, we will support Bill 37, "said Sylvain Roy. 
In addition, the hon. Member for Bonaventure wants the McInnis Cement Company to file specific commitments on the conversion of cement plant to fuels to lower environmental impact. "We support the Environmental Monitoring Committee and the environmental mediation process set up jointly by the Quebec Center for the Law of the Environment and Cement McInnis; One can hope to advance the cement plant to the mitigation of its environmental footprint, "added Sylvain Roy.
