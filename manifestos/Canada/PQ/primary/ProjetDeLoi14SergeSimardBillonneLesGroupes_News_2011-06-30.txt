Québec-on the refusal of the Minister Delegate to Natural Resources and Wildlife, Serge Simard, to hear the groups who want to testify in Parliamentary Committee on the Program 14, An Act to amend the Mining Act, the Parti Québécois Question about the real desire of the minister to reach consensus on exploration and mining in Quebec. 
"As legislators, we must make sure we have the best possible bill. In this sense, the refusal to hear groups that have expressed their interest is flavored, said the official opposition spokesman Scott McKay. The Liberal government and Minister Simard must stop gagging civil society members who want to participate in debates. The Union of Agricultural Farmers, Manufacturers and Exporters of Quebec, the Regional Conference of the Elected of Abitibi-Témiscamingue, Sept-Uranium Islands, the Confederation of National Trade Unions and Agnico-Eagle Ltdé Mines all addressed a request in order to To be heard and the minister has all ignored them. Groups always have relevant additions and comments to bring to bills, it is the people in the field and it is imperative that we hear them, "added the member for the Assumption. 
"In Bill 14, the Minister added articles from amendments by the Quebecois party at the deceased by Bill 79, whereas at the time of the detailed study of the latter, the Government voted against these amendments ! The minister should avoid repeating these mistakes and hear what groups have to say about Bill 14, which is far from unanimous, "concluded Scott McKay.
