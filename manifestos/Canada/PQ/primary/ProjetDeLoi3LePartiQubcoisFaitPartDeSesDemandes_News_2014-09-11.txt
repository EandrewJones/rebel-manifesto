Quebec- MP for Taschereau and Parliamentary Leader of the Official Opposition, Agnès Maltese, and the member for Sanguinet and Spokesperson for Pension and Pension Places, Alain Therrien, submitted requests to the Minister of Municipal Affairs and of the occupation of the territory, Pierre Moreau, in anticipation of the detailed study of Bill 3, law promoting the financial health and the sustainability of the defined benefit pension plans in the municipal sector. 
"We will make suggestions for amendments to the bill, in the best interests of workers, retirees, taxpayers and municipalities. The unique solution for all regimes and automation are not acceptable. We believe in the benefits of negotiations and respect for signed contracts, and it is in the sense we will make proposals to the minister, "said Alain Therien. 
At the end of the parliamentary committee, here are the main findings of the official opposition: 
Minister Moreau must commit 
Agnès Malteis asks Minister Moreau to make a clear commitment before the adoption of the principle of the bill: to file, before the start of the detailed study, actuarial assessments of the pension plans as at December 31, 2013. It also requests To the Minister to forward to the official opposition the main amendments he intends to testify to the Commission, always before the detailed study of the bill. 
"It's a matter of respect for all parties concerned. The up-to-date numbers will allow us to do a rigorous job. Faced with such a significant problem, it is essential to have the most accurate portrait of the situation. The same goes for the amendments, "concluded the parliamentary leader of the official opposition.
