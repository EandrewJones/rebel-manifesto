Quebec- On the occasion of the 26th National Suicide Prevention Week, the Quebec Party Spokesperson in social services, youth protection, home care and prevention, Jean-François Lisé, expresses his Concern about the consequences of liberal austerity on support offered to suicidal persons. 
"The effects of liberal austerity are worrying. Suicide prevention stakeholders draw the alarm: 15% of calls received at Help number 1 866 Calling do not find an answer, "said Jean? François Lisée. 
"Since they've been in power, the Liberals cut $ 23.7 million in prevention and public health. In addition, there are many austerity measures directly affecting the most vulnerable people in our society. Needs are increasing, and organisms are to be sufficient for the task. They have more than ever needed government support. And when it comes to keeping someone alive, we will all agree that these services should not be saved on candlelights, "continued the spokesperson. 
"In Quebec, suicide causes the loss of three persons a day, three people too much. Thanks to the mobilization of the company, we have managed to make significant gains. However, rates remain very high, especially in people aged 35 to 49. It is imperative to continue prevention efforts. All means must be put in place so that every Quebecker knows where to find help, and that it is insured to receive when it needs it, "concluded Jean-François Lisée.
