Bromont- The Minister Delegate to Industrial Policy and the Quebec Economic Development Bank, Élaine Zakaïb, participated in the inauguration of the GE Aviation Robotics Center of Excellence in Bromont. 
This center, which will create new optimized robotization techniques, will also serve to design robotic vision and inspection systems that will be used in all GE aviation factories around the world. 
"This new Center of Excellence allows the GE Aviation Bromont factory to maintain and strengthen its competitive position in the General Electric Group and in world markets," mentioned Élain Zakaïb. "This major project also contributes to the dynamism and economic vitality of the Montérérégie region while consolidating and creating highly specialized jobs in Quebec. » 
The GE Aviation BROMONT plant specializes in the hot and cold forging of aircraft reactor compressor blades. It currently has more than 700 employees, including 30 are already working in the robot center. In addition, 60 other employees will join them. 
"Our bromont aviation facilities are globally productive and competitive thanks to the talent, creativity and skills of our employee teams," said Elyse Allan, President and Chief Executive Officer of GE Canada. "The impressive aeronautical cluster in Quebec and the strong academic programs that support it make Bromont the ideal location for our new global research and development center in robotics, automation and instrumentation. » 
Remember that the Government of Quebec granted financial assistance of nearly $ 8.4 million at the GE Aviation Bromont plant for the establishment of two centers of excellence in research, including that in robotics.
