Sherbrooke- The Minister of Veterans Affairs and Minister of La Francophonie, the Honorable Steven Blaney, the Minister of Higher Education, Research, Science and Technology, Mr. Pierre Duchesne, and the rector of the Université de Sherbrooke, the Pre Luce Samoisette, were gathered in order to inaugurate the new Applied Cancer Research Pavilion (PRAC) from the University of Sherbrooke. They were accompanied for the occasion of the Minister of Health and Social Services and Minister responsible for the Estrie Region, Dr. Réjean Hébert, from the member for Sherbrooke, Mr. Serge Cardin, and the Dean of the Faculty of Medicine and the health sciences of the University of Sherbrooke, Pr Pierre Cossette. 
"Our government has invested in innovation and infrastructure to improve the quality of life for Canadians and lay the foundations for economic prosperity. Thanks to the investments our government in the Applied Cancer Research Flag, researchers at the University of Sherbrooke are now equipped with the necessary tools to pursue a world-class research, "said the Honorable Steven Blaney on behalf of Minister of Industry, the Honorable Christian Paradis. 
The pavilion was achieved through total financial assistance of $ 31.4 million granted under the Knowledge Infrastructure Program. An amount of $ 22.3 million comes from the Government of Quebec, while Industry Canada contributed $ 9.1 million. 
"At the dawn of the deposit of the National Research and Innovation Policy, the government can already rely on a health research network that stands out. It is therefore essential for Quebec that our universities are home to state-of-the-art search infrastructure and this flag illustrates its brilliant way. I am delighted with the collaboration between the institutions and the pooling of resources that are essential to optimize the development of research and to maximize its effects, "said Minister Pierre Duchesne.
