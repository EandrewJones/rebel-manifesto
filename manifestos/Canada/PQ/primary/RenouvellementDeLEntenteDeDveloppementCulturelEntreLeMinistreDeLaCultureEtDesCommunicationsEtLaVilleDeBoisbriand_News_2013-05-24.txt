Boisbriand- On behalf of the Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, the member for Labelle and Head of the Laurentian Region, Sylvain Pagé, as well as the Mayor of the City of Boisbriand, Marlene Cordato, announced the renewal of the Cultural Development Agreement between the Ministry of Culture and Communications and the City of Boisbriand. 
"I am pleased to confirm the continuation of the partnership between the Government of Quebec and the city of Boisbriand. This agreement will make possible the realization of many carrier cultural projects, and its implementation will provide the whole population with the opportunity to learn about the arts and culture, while promoting interaction with creators Minister Kotto said. 
"Cultural development agreements allow local and regional environments to build close links with the community and promote broader access to citizens to culture. I salute the city of Boisbriand, which places culture at the heart of the quality of life of its population, "said MP Sylvain Pagé. 
"For three years, the Municipal Council has regard to culture as an element of social and economic development. Contribute to culture is to offer citizens and an additional opportunity to appreciate their city in a new light. I thank the Ministry of Culture and Communications to participate in the cultural effervescence we have known for the last few years. The government's financial contribution will allow us to diversify our actions in terms of cultural development. Our current position on the cultural scene will be consolidated, "said Mayor Marlene Cordato. 
The $ 30,000 financial assistance from the Ministry of Culture and Communications is granted over two years under its programAide to partnership initiatives. The city is committed to investing the equivalent amount during the same period. The renewal of the biennial agreement will have the effect of improving and consolidating the regional cultural offer.
