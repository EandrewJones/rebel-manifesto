Quebec- The member for Lac-Saint-Jean and spokesman for the Parti Party in terms of education, research and school perseverance, Alexandre Cloutier, asks the Minister of Education to hear the cry of the heart launched by Thousands of families and the Quebec association of speech therapists and audiologists (AQOA), Friday, the National Assembly, and put an end to the cuts in the services to students in difficulty. 
The founder of the group of services for our different children, Geneviève Lapointe, the spokesperson for parents of children with special needs, Brigitte Dubé, and the outgoing president of the AQOA, Marie-Claude Rousseau, came to testify to The impact of cuts on children in difficulty, Friday, the National Assembly. 
"Students with disabilities or in difficulty of adaptation or learning represent one in five children in Quebec. These are the first victims of austerity. The testimonies of parents exhausted, exhausted, flock by hundreds. Today, speaking on behalf of thousands of families, two brave mothers have come to ask the government to reverse. The Minister of Education can not remain insensitive, "said Alexandre Cloutier. 
According to a consultation with the members of the AQOA from 19 to 21 September 2015, nearly 40% of school speech therapists indicate that this year, speech language cups affect their school board. "The government saber services that were already too rare. In doing so, he directly compromises the success of students struggling with language disorders, "deplored the member for Lac-Saint-Jean. 
Interpellation 
The Parti Québécois has also argued the Minister of Education for two hours at the Blue Show, Friday, about the Liberal government's drastic cuts in student services. "Parents, teachers, education professionals, support staff, even school departments call for a change in the government. The minister must come out of the denial and hear the environmental concerns, "concluded Alexander Cloutier.
