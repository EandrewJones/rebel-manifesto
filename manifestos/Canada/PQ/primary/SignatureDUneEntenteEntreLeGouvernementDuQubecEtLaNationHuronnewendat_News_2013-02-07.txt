Quebec- The Quebec Prime Minister, Ms. Pauline Marois, and the Great Head of the Huron Wendat Nation, Mr. Konrad Sioui, today proceeded to renew the signing of the Aboriginal Initiatives Fund Agreement II (FIA Ii). The ceremony took place in the presence of representatives of the Huron-Wendat community, including the Deputy Chief, Mr. Jean Vincent, and the leaders MM. René Gros-Louis and Richard Picard Jr. 
The Premier was very happy with the renewal of this agreement. "My government has always wanted to maintain a harmonious bilateral relationship and constructive partnerships with the Huron-Wendat Nation, and it is in this spirit that we will continue this collaboration," she said. 
As part of the FIA II Economic Development component, $ 1.3 million over five years is reserved for the Wendake community. This fund provides financial support for diverse projects offering opportunities for benefits for indigenous communities. It aims to support them in their economic, social and community development. 
"The development of indigenous communities is a priority for the Government of Quebec. FIA II's financial support will provide tools for creating jobs, Aboriginal training and cultural influence, "said Aboriginal Affairs Minister Elizabeth Larouche. 
The Grand Chef Konrad Sioui said he was very satisfied with the meeting and the agreement that will allow members of his community to develop stimulating projects. "On behalf of the Huron-Wendat Nation, I am honored to meet the Quebec Prime Minister. I welcome its opening as to the establishment of bilateral relations between its Government and my nation, in the spirit and intent of our National Treaty Huron-Wendat. The Prime Minister also stated at the Montreal Statement on the Tritures and Resources held in Montreal on December 3, his intention to establish nation-nation relations, and I can only rejoice at this vision. »
