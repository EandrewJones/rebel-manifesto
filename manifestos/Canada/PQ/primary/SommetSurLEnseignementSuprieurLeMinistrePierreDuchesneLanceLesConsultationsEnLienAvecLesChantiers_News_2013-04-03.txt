Quebec- Just over one month after the Higher Education Summit, the Minister of Higher Education, Research, Science and Technology, Mr. Pierre Duchesne, officially launches consultations surrounding The five projects created at the end of the summit. 
"Already, the work of the construction sites on the financial assistance to the studies and on the National Council of Universities are well initiated and the others will begin this week. By the end of the week, I would have had the opportunity to chat with all Presidents and presidents of construction, "said Minister Duchesne. 
As part of their work, site presidents will consult all the partners of the Summit on Higher Education who wish. In addition, like the preparatory approach to the higher education summit, the work will be accessible on the web. A new section for more information on each of these projects is available on the Department's website at www.messst.gouv.qc.ca / construction sites. 
Those interested in submitting suggestions or contributions can do so now by accessing this section. Each site has an email box that meets the President or the Chairman and to transmit any document related to the mandate.  Let us remember that at the end of the higher education summit, the Quebec government has opened five work sites. 
These projects testify to our desire to bring major reforms into higher education. A tight schedule was given to construction chairpersons to promote the effectiveness of the work because the results are expected not only by the partners of higher education, but by all Quebeckers and Quebeckers. We hold our commitments and we act promptly, "concluded Minister Duchesne.
