Quebec - The Quebec Prime Minister Pauline Marois, entrusts his parliamentary assistant for youth issues, the member for Laval-des-Rapides, Leo Bureau-Blouin, the mandate to make a regional tour aimed at raising awareness, mobilizing and To consult Quebec youth on the issues that concern it today, as well as those that will be the challenges of tomorrow. 
"In the spring, we have experienced an unprecedented intergenerational conflict to be drawn from a number of lessons," said the Prime Minister during the study of the appropriations of the Ministry of the Executive Board, youth component. It is our duty to better inform citizens to promote public discussions and constructive consultations. In addition, Ms. Marois took the opportunity to announce that the government extends a year the 2009-2014 Youth Action Strategy. 
The tour of Mr. Bureau-Blouin will be organized by the Youth Secretariat, in collaboration with the Quebec Regional Youth Forums and their consultation table. The deputies of Saint-Jean and Terrebonne, Dave Turcotte and Mathieu Traversy, will also take part in these unique meetings with young people from all regions of Quebec. New information technologies will also be used to support this initiative. 
"I enthusiastically accept the mandate that the Prime Minister entrusts to me and I wish to take this opportunity to invite young people to project themselves into the future and to imagine Quebec from 2030. We will discuss the demographic issues that will be at the forehead. heart of the challenges of Quebec society in the coming years. Through this tour, we want young people to voice the next government plan for youth, "said Leo Bureau-Blouin. 
"I hope that all Quebec's youth partners engage actively in this approach we will initiate soon," the Prime Minister concluded.
