Kuujjuaq- Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, took advantage of his visit to Nunavik, on 12 and 13 September, to discuss local elected officials related to telecommunications. It said itself determined to negotiate a financial partnership with the main actors involved, the federal government and the Kativik Regional Administration (ARK), to ensure effective access to telecommunications networks. "This is an essential service for Nunavimmiut. In the digital age, access to an Internet service is essential, "said Pauline Marois. 
Thus, the Prime Minister mandated the President of the Treasury Board, Stéphane Bédard, to study the telecommunications solution for proposals made public by the ARK on 12 September. These proposals could take over the current agreement totaling $ 14.9 million over $ 7.4 million by Industry Canada, $ 3 million from the Department of Natural Resources, 2.5 M $ by the ark and, finally, $ 2 million by Telesat. A new financial partnership will have to be studied before the 2016 deadline. 
The Premier also mandated the Minister Delegate to Canadian Intergovernmental Affairs, Canadian Francophonie and Sovereign Governance, Alexander Cloutier, to undertake discussions with the federal government on the issue of telecommunications in Nunavik. 
"Access to an adequate telecommunications network adapted to the 21st century needs is vital for Nunavimmiut. The government's commitment today is encouraging and we look forward to starting the discussions on this. The 2016 target must be achieved, "said Kativik Regional Administration Chair, Maggie Emudluk. 
Remember that over the last 10 years, the ARK has made significant efforts to develop a reliable Internet network for Nunavik organizations and residents. She founded the company Tamaani Internet, which offers wireless internet access to the 14 northern villages.
