Saint-Zenon-du-Lac-Humqui- on the occasion of a tour of members of the Government of Quebec in Bas-Saint-Laurent and Gaspésie, the Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, announced the Award of a $ 179,900 grant for the Saint-Zenon-du-Lac-Humqui Library, located in the former village school. Pascal Bérubé, Minister Delegate to Tourism and Minister responsible for the Bas-Saint-Laurent Region, and Reginald Duguay, Mayor of Saint-Zenon-du-Lac-Humqui, were also present at the event. 
"Thanks to this financial support, improvements will be made to make the Library of Saint-Zenon-Du-Humqui a place of the most unifying and innovative for the region. The new library will become a suitable place to intensify cultural and literary exchanges between citizens and, moreover, will ensure the maintenance of this essential local service to the vitality of the municipality, "said Minister Kotto. 
From left to right: Maka Kotto, Minister of Culture and Communications, Reginald Duguay, Mayor of Saint-Zenon-du-Lac-Humqui, Pascal Bérubé, Minister Delegate for Tourism and Minister responsible for the Bas-Saint-Laurent Region 
"It is by investing in local services that the sustainability of local communities. I am particularly proud to see a project like the Bas-Saint-Laurent, which provides the population with an additional Community anchoring, "continued Minister Bérubé. 
This grant was granted under the Department of Culture and Communications Capital Assistance Program. It will, among other things, make the library accessible to people with reduced mobility and in accordance with the construction code, in addition to modernizing the interior equipment.
