New York, New York (United States) -The MP for La Prairie, François Rebello, pleaded this morning, in front of more than 200 American railway leaders, for the development of an effective link between Montreal and New York and more Specifically for the establishment of a night train between the two metropolises. 
"The development of a fast night train is more than ever a future project that will further strengthen our economic and commercial exchanges," said François Rebello. The current journey time trip time is more than 11 hours between Montreal and New York, which is not conducive to tourism exchanges, while a night train could connect both cities in less than 7 hours. A lower cost than the aircraft, "he added. 
The member took the floor this morning in New York as part of a conference on the development of transport in the Americas, in particular of the former congress member, James Oberstar, and the Senator of the State of New York, Malcolm Smith. He stressed the importance of investing rapidly in improving the railway service between Quebec and the United States. 
The member for the Prairie went to New York at the invitation of the US High Speed Rail Association and took part, as a speaker, at the High Speed Rail Conference, held from November 6th to 8th, 2011. The US High Speed Rail Association is an organization whose mandate is to promote the fast train in the United States.
