In response to the decision of the Director of Criminal and Criminal Prosecutions (PPCP) to file charges at Mr. Gilbert Rozon in a single file and to abandon 13 others, the member for Joliette and Spokesperson for the Second Opposition Group on Justice, Véronique Hivon, Press The Minister of Justice, Sonia Lebel, to set up a working group that would include legal and psychosocial experts, elected officials of all political formations, lawyers of the Defense as well as representatives of the judiciary and PCPP to evaluate all possible solutions in order to respond to the deep questioning which arises, the problem of trust and the inadequacy between the justice system and the reality of victims of sexual assault . 
IN SHORT 
· Victims feel abandoned by the justice system; There is an inadequacy between their reality and the functioning of the system. 
· More than ever, there is a need to find solutions to improve the processing of complaints on sexual assault. 
· Véronique Hivon challenges Minister Lebel for a working group to be set up. 
· The idea of a specialized court on sexual and conjugal violence must be studied. 
"We must act to rebuild trust and offer answers to the feeling of misunderstanding and impunity that lives in the victims. It is our responsibility, as representatives of the population, to tell us and to act in the wake of the #Moiausi movement. We must also respond to the courage demonstrated by all those who spoke publicly for things to change. I invite the minister to bring together a working group to find solutions for victims, "said Véronique Hivon game entry. 
"According to Calacs, centers for helping sexual assault, only 5% of sexual assaults are denounced to police services. And only 3 complaints out of 1000 will ultimately lead to a conviction. In this context, the minister can not just say that the system works, that we have to keep trusted and invite victims to denounce. There is visibly a problem somewhere, "said the member for Joliette. 
"At the Québécois Party, we have been proposing since last year the establishment of a specialized tribunal in the Quebec Court so that the specific reality of victims of sexual or conjugal violence is taken into account, that the rules of practice can be adapted and that the specialization of all concerned stakeholders is acquired. This Tribunal could also better take into account the needs of victims about processes and awards, for example, a more significant place for the principles of restorative justice, "added the spokesperson. 
The #Moiausi movement led to an unprecedented wave of denunciations of sexual crimes. "As an elected, I repeat that we have the responsibility to act and be a vector of change to put an end to the culture of silence and the feeling of shame that gnaws too many Quebec victims. And, above all, to restore confidence in our justice system. I tend his hand to the minister so that we worked non-partisan in this sense, in the best interests of the victims, "said Véronique Hivon.
