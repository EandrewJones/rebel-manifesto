Longueuil- The Minister of Education, Recreation and Sport and Minister responsible for Montérégie, Mrs Marie Malavoy, on behalf of the Minister of Transport and Minister of Municipal Affairs, Regions and the Occupation of the Territory, Mr. Sylvain Gaudreault, is pleased to note that the investments of the city of Longueuil and the Metropolitan Transport Agency (AMT) on the Roland-Therrien Boulevard, in Longueuil, give results. 
"Collective transport has always been a priority commitment to our government and I have a priority in my election campaign. In this autumn return where the trips are at their maximum, I am particularly pleased to note that not only the new bus service with a reserved route on the Roland-Therrien boulevard is now a reality, but it is also effective and Beneficial for all users, "Ms. Malavoy said. 
The new express service on the Roland-Therrien boulevard was set up on August 26. He is the first step towards the introduction of a fast network within the agglomeration. In the long term, when the project will be completed in 2014, the Longueuil Transportation Network (RTL) will have 5.2 km from the additional reserved lane. 
In addition, the AMT contributes $ 2 million for the realization of reserved routes on the same corridor. "This concrete measure is part of the sustainable mobility principles supported by the Government, including the introduction over the coming months of a Quebec sustainable mobility policy," concluded Mr. Gaudreault.
