Quebec- The Minister of Municipal Affairs, Regions and the Occupation of the Territory and Minister of Transport, Mr. Sylvain Gaudreault, has tabled today to the National Assembly a bill to complement the reform of municipal political funding with Law No. 26. 
"The government continues to act on integrity by depositing a new bill to counter illegal funding and encourage democratic exercise. If it is adopted, it will promote the independence of political parties and independent candidates with respect to private contributors by increased public funding while allowing them to have sufficient resources. In the end, it is public confidence in elected officials and political financing that will be strengthened by this reform, "said Minister Gaudreault. 
In concrete terms, the new measures: 
The new measures complement those introduced by Bill 26, which aimed at introducing a temporary municipal political funding regime in the context of the 2013 municipal elections. This bill and Bill 2, on funding. provincial policy, have been adopted unanimously by the National Assembly. 
Remember that the measures proposed in the bill have been the subject of public consultations. The consultation report is available on the Ministry's website atwww.mamrot.gouv.qc.ca.
