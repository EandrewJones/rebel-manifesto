SINT-LOUIS-DU-HA! Ha! - Minister Delegate to Tourism, Minister Responsible for the Bas-Saint-Laurent Region and Member of Parliament for Matane-Matapédia, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, on behalf of the Minister of Municipal Affairs, Regions and the Occupation of the Territory and Minister Transportation, Mr. Sylvain Gaudreault, announces financial assistance from $ 48,908 to the Témiscouata Aster Observatory Corporation for the upgrading of the Observatory and the development of a new exhibition. 
More specifically, the project is to buy equipment and signaling panels, prepare a marketing plan and hiring an extra person. This project is estimated at $ 97,816. 
"I am very pleased with the support of the Quebec government to the realization of this project. With this financial assistance, the Aster Observatory will be able to contribute to the bonus of the Bas-Saint-Laurent Regional Tourist Offer. The consolidation of the activities of the Observatory also part of the priorities selected by the MRC de Témiscouata in its new 2013-2016 economic development plan, "said Minister Pascal Bérubé. 
Remember that Atter is a scientific extension and recreation station and an interpretive center that is aimed at both school clients and local and international tourism. Aster continues a dual mandate of interpretation and scientific animation. 
Financial assistance comes from the Cyclical Development Fund of the Ministry of Municipal Affairs, Regions and the Occupation of the Territory. It is added to the $ 85,000 government assistance that had been confirmed at the Corporation of the Témiscouata-Aster Observatory on April 8, 2013 for the restoration of its building. This assistance came from the Collective Entrepreneurship (PIEC) infrastructure program.
