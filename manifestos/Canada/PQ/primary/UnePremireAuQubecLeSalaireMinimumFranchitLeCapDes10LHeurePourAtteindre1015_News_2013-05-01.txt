Quebec- The Quebec Prime Minister Pauline Marois, and the Minister of Labor, Minister of Employment and Social Solidarity and Minister responsible for the Status of Women, Agnès Maltese, benefit from the International Day of Workers to announce that the wages Minimum crosses today, for the first time in Quebec, the $ 10 per hour. It goes from $ 9.90 to $ 10.15, an increase of $ 0.25 per hour. 
"Employment always remains the best solution to fight against poverty and ensure the economic security of people. This 2.53% increase in the minimum wage is greater than the increase of 1.84% of the cost of living provided by the Ministry of Finance and the Economy. This increase will improve the situation of low-income workers, while preserving the jobs and competitiveness of our economy, "said the Prime Minister. 
The minimum wage rate payable to employees will be increased by $ 8.55 to $ 8.75 per hour. As of today, the minimum wage payable to workers in some sectors of the garment industry will be increased from $ 990 to $ 10.15. In the agricultural sector, the minimum wage will increase from $ 2.91 to $ 2.98 per kilo for raspberry gatherers and $ 0.77 to $ 0.79 per kilo for strawberry pickers. 
"On this International Workers' Day, the announced increase in the minimum wage confirms the importance of work to support. So I welcome all these workers and all those workers for whom their jobs are a source of pride and dignity, "said Agnès Maltais. 
All information relating to the new minimum wage rates and at certain working conditions, such as meals and accommodation, is published on the Lacommission website of labor standards.
