Quebec - Minister Delegate to Tourism, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, participated today at the first meeting of the Illegal Tourism Accommodation Advisory Committee in Quebec. At the same time, the Minister entrusts an external consultant the mandate to establish a diagnosis related to this problem. 
"Tourisme Québec sets up a structured and concerted approach to better understand the phenomenon of illegal tourism accommodation, a first in the history of Quebec. This issue, which concerns us, will be analyzed in close collaboration with the various partners related to the tourism industry, "said Minister Bérubé. 
At the same time, the realization of the mandate entrusted to the external analyst, Mr. Jean-Claude Cloutier, a consulting economist, will fuel the work of the Committee and will allow a better analysis of the situation both economically and legal. 
"The Committee has already started its work and held its first meeting today. This demonstrates the willingness of Tourisme Québec to act as a leader and to mobilize industry-related industry partners, "concluded Minister Bérubé. 
The Advisory Committee, chaired by Tourisme Québec, is composed of the following representatives: the Quebec Revenue Agency, the Quebec City Hotel Association, the Greater Montreal Hotel Association, the Quebec Hotel Association , ATR Associated Quebec, Camping Québec, the Corporation of the Quebec Industry, the Federation of Founders of Quebec and the Ministry of Municipal Affairs, Regions, and the Occupation of the Territory.
