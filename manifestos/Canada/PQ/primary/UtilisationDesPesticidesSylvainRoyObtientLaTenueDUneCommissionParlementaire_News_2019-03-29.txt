Quebec, March 28, 2019 -The MP for Bonaventure and Spokesperson for the Third Opposition Group on Agriculture and Food, Sylvain Roy, was very proud of the fact that members of the Commission. Agriculture, Fisheries, Energy and Natural Resources (Capern) has given themselves to a parliamentary committee on the use of pesticides in agriculture in Quebec, unanimously. 
IN SHORT 
The hon. Member for Bonaventure is pleased that CAPERN has seized this mandate. "We can no longer pretend that the file of the use of pesticides in agriculture is not worrying. With this parliamentary committee, we will finally have the time just about the possible impacts of chemicals on our diet, health and our environment. It's a nice victory of the Parti Québécois! He rejoiced. 
Sylvain Roy is reassured: the fact that the government has agreed to move forward with the holding of such a parliamentary exercise is rather good sign. "The survival of healthy agriculture is at stake right now. Like me, the organizations, the farmers themselves, consumers and research centers are satisfied with this decision, which will allow us to go to the merits of things. The government seems sensitive to this issue; It's encouraging, "he said. 
"We want to encourage Quebec families to buy local; They therefore have the right to know what exactly, in their plate. More and more Quebeckers are more likely to claim a turn, a transition to more environmentally friendly agriculture and, ricochet, public health. It is time for light to be done on all this question, "added the spokesman. 
Sylvain Roy is very pleased to be at the origin of this mandate of initiative. "I am of the opinion that at the end of this parliamentary committee, Quebec will be able to position itself as a leader in healthy agriculture. It is, in any case, the goal we need to aim, "he concluded.
