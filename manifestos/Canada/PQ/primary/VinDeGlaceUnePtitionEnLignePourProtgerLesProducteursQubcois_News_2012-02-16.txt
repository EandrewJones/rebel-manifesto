Québec- "The consultation launched by the Harper government with the aim of making regulatory changes to the labeling of wine that may have terrible consequences for Quebec ice wine producers generate a lot of reactions. Indeed, producers on the ground mobilize and it is a pleasure for me to bear an electronic petition asking the elected officials of the National Assembly to unite to defend the producers, "said the hon. Member for Kamouraska-Témiscouata and Official opposition spokesman for agriculture and food, André Simard. 
The petition explains the specificities of Quebec production recognized by the International Organization of Vine and Wine and emphasizes the prosperity of Quebec ice wines around the world. 
The Canadian Food Inspection Agency, in UncommuniqueéPublished in January, announced that the name "ice wine" could only belong to the wines produced with grapes frozen on the vine. 
"This consultation is an illustration of more than the Conservative government's misunderstanding with Quebec peculiarities. It is out of the question that we let him do without saying anything, "added André Simard. 
"As we have repeated it for several months, we wish, to the Parti Québécois, support the emergence of the largest number of local products and Quebec specificity," concluded André Simard, inviting the people to visit the website of the National Assembly to sign massively grazing.
