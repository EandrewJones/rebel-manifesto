Quebec - The government of Quebec would like to reiterate that it will support the City of Lac-Mégantic in all the stages to come, including the continuation of the clean-up work following the train accident of July 6.
Remember that the service providers hired by the Montreal, Maine and Atlantic (MMA) railway company have been advised by the City of Lac-Mégantic that the latter had the necessary powers to ensure that they will be paid if MMA was in breach of his obligations.
A formal notice has also been sent to MMA by the City to remind it of its responsibilities. The government hopes that MMA will act as an exemplary corporate citizen and comply with the demands of the City of Lac-Mégantic.
The government believes that the population of Lac-Mégantic, already severely affected, does not have to assume the consequences of any breaches. &quot;If the MMA company failed to meet its obligations, the City of Lac-Mégantic could ask the Quebec government for the reimbursement of the sums it has paid to the MMA suppliers, which will be eligible for a 100% reimbursement under the program. specific financial assistance adopted by the Quebec government on July 10, ”said the Minister of Public Security, Stéphane Bergeron.
Details of the specific financial assistance program can be found at www.securitepublique.gouv.qc.ca.
