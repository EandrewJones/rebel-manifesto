Quebec - The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, and the Minister of Employment and Social Solidarity, Minister of Labor, Minister responsible for the Status of Women and Minister responsible for the Capitale-Nationale region, Agnès Maltais, announced an agreement in principle of $ 201,700 for the restoration of the Maison Augustine-Plamondon, located in Saint-Raymond de Portneuf.
Cited under the Cultural Heritage Act, since August 1, 2011, the Maison Augustine-Plamondon was built between 1907 and 1912, by the explorer and surveyor Joseph Bureau, grandfather of Augustine Plamondon, who was the first music teacher of the famous lyricist Luc Plamondon. The latter acquired the house in 2010 and donated it to the Plamondon Foundation, in order to support the realization of the project to restore and bring the house up to standard, which will then become a place of creation, cultural dissemination and training. for artists.
“At the heart of the municipality and the history of Saint-Raymond, the Maison Augustine-Plamondon was the birthplace of Luc Plamondon, one of the most famous lyricists in Quebec and throughout the Francophonie. The agreement in principle announced today will ultimately enable this house to perpetuate its vocation as a place of creation, dissemination and training. It will thus become a cultural crossroads for the whole region, ”Minister Kotto said.
“The Portneuf region is rich in culture and artistic life. The preservation of its heritage is of national importance. All of Quebec will benefit since Luc Plamondon plans to deposit his personal archives there, ”continued Ms. Maltais.
It should be noted that this project is supported under component 2 of the Quebec Cultural Heritage Fund. The Municipality of Saint-Raymond is also financially involved in the project, and the Foundation has also set up a vast grassroots fundraising campaign to complete the financial package necessary for the realization of the project.
