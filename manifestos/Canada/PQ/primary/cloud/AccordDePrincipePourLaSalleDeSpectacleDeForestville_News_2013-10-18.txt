Forestville - The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, accompanied by the MNA for René-Lévesque and responsible for the Côte-Nord region, Marjolain Dufour, announced an agreement in principle of $ 1,657,000 for the project. renovation of the auditorium of the Rivières school.
“This structuring project will help revitalize the arts and entertainment community in Forestville. I am proud to conclude today the agreement in principle which demonstrates in a tangible way the importance that the Government of Quebec gives to access to quality cultural facilities to our fellow citizens across the country. national territory ”, declared Minister Kotto.
&quot;This boost from our government will ensure the continuity of an essential place of cultural dissemination that has brought happiness to two generations of Nord-Côtières and Nord-Côtiers,&quot; added MP Marjolain Dufour.
Currently, the project involves the complete renovation of the auditorium of the Polyvalente des Rivières and the purchase of equipment to ensure its vocation of dissemination of arts and culture.
The Department&#39;s contribution is part of the 2013-2023 Quebec Infrastructure Plan, made public in April. It should be remembered that this ten-year plan will ultimately result in investments of $ 2 billion for the cultural component. It will have a major economic impact throughout Quebec, with nearly 60,000 jobs created or maintained, on average, per year. This new way of doing things will make it possible to prospectively assess the needs, based on the priorities of Quebecers, to properly measure the associated costs and thus to make informed choices. The City of Forestville and the Commission scolaire de l&#39;Estuaire are also contributing to the financing of the project.
