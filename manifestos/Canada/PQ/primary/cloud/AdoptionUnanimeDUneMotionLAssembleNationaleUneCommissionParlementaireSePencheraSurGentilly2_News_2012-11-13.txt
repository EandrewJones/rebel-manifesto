Quebec - The chief government whip and responsible to the Council of Ministers of the Center-du-Québec and Mauricie regions, Yves-François Blanchet (Johnson), joins his voice to those of MPs Luc Trudel (Saint-Maurice) and Noëlla Champagne (Champlain ) to welcome the unanimous adoption of a motion presented by the Minister of Natural Resources and Member of Parliament for Vachon, Martine Ouellet, which entrusts the Committee on Agriculture, Fisheries, Energy and Natural Resources with the mandate to study the impacts of the decommissioning of the Gentilly-2 nuclear power plant and of the economic diversification plan for the Center-du-Québec and Mauricie regions.
The deputies from the Center-du-Québec and Mauricie regions united their voices in saluting the study mandate which will be exercised on January 29 and 30, 2013. “I salute the happy outcome which will allow the population to d &#39;&#39; have all the information you need. It is a constructive approach in which the collaboration of all stakeholders will be leveraged, ”said Yves-François Blanchet, Member of Parliament for Johnson.
While analyzing the impacts of the decommissioning, the parliamentary committee will also have a mandate to study the economic diversification plan announced by the Government of Quebec. &quot;The mandate given to the parliamentary committee will make it possible to respond to the concerns of our world and to look to the future through an economic diversification plan&quot;, explains Noëlla Champagne, Member of Parliament for Champlain,
More than ten speakers will be heard during the hearings of the parliamentary committee which will have to report to the National Assembly no later than February 21, 2013. &quot;This announcement, which responds to a request from the community, confirms the government&#39;s concern to listen to the regions of Quebec,&quot; concludes the member for Saint-Maurice Luc Trudel.
The wording of the motion reads as follows:
&quot;That the National Assembly entrust the Committee on Agriculture, Fisheries, Energy and Natural Resources with the mandate to study the impacts related to the decommissioning of the Gentilly-2 nuclear power plant as well as the diversification plan economic for the Center-du-Québec and Mauricie sectors;
That to this end, the latter hold special consultations in the National Assembly on January 29 and 30, 2013;
That it hear Hydro-Québec management on its report on the Gentilly-2 nuclear power plant refurbishment project for a maximum period of thirty minutes;
That following this presentation, parliamentarians be able to discuss with Hydro-Québec management for a period of three hours;
That it then hear from the cities of Bécancour and Trois-Rivières, the Director of the Industrial and Port Park of Bécancour, the representatives of the Chamber of Commerce and Industry of Cœur-du-Québec, the representatives of the Chamber of Commerce and Industries of Trois-Rivières, the Regional Conference of Elected Officials of Center-du-Québec, the representatives of the workers&#39; unions at the plant, the Strategic Action Committee and Sortons le Québec du nuclear;
That the maximum duration of the presentation for each of these speakers be 15 minutes and that the discussion with the members of the Commission be no longer than 45 minutes;
That a 12-minute period be provided for opening and closing remarks;
That the Committee report to the Assembly no later than February 21, 2013;
That the Minister of Natural Resources be a member of the said commission;
Finally, that this motion become an order of the National Assembly of Quebec. ”
