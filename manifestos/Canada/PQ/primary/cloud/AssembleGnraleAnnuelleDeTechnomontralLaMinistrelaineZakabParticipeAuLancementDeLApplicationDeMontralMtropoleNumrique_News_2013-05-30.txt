Outremont - The Minister for Industrial Policy and the Banque de développement économique du Québec, Ms. Élaine Zakaïb, presented the new application of Montréal Métropole Numérique (MMN) at the annual general meeting marking the 5th anniversary of the TechnoMontréal cluster.
“Montréal Métropole Numérique is a visionary project that eloquently testifies to TechnoMontréal&#39;s promising role,” said Ms. Zakaïb. “For five years now, this industrial cluster has mobilized key players in the information and communications technology sector of greater Montreal around innovative and structuring projects for Quebec. &quot;
Montréal Métropole Numérique aims to propel Montréal to the rank of major “smart cities” such as Tokyo, Berlin, Paris, New York and London. The result of concerted action between businesses, universities, governments and citizens, MMN will provide the city with state-of-the-art digital infrastructures, services and functions. The result will be a series of applied innovations to meet the major challenges of the metropolis, such as the efficiency of transport and health services, citizen participation, education and access to public data.
“In line with the strategic sustainable development approaches advocated by the Government of Quebec, Montréal Métropole Numérique will, for example, allow the establishment of a Web platform facilitating the establishment of partnerships between companies in the technology sector. information and communications, thus making them more competitive on a global scale, ”concluded Minister Delegate Zakaïb.
TechnoMontréal is a non-profit organization that brings together all stakeholders in the information and communications technology sector of greater Montreal around common objectives and concerted actions in order to optimize the competitiveness and growth of their businesses. companies.
