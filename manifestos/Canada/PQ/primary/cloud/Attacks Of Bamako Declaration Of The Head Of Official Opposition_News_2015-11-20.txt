Quebec - Following the tragic events that unfolded in Bamako this morning, the leader of the official opposition, Pierre Karl Péladeau, and the Parti Québécois spokesperson for international relations, Carole Poirier, made a point of &#39;address to the Malian people.
“On behalf of all elected members of the Parti Québécois, I would like to express our deep solidarity with Mali, while Bamako is facing the horror of terrorist attacks. We share the pain and sorrow of the Malians, especially those of the families and relatives of the victims, in front of these barbaric and despicable acts that we strongly condemn. Ending terrorism must be a priority for the entire international community. This senseless violence must stop. We offer Mali our support in the face of this tragedy, ”said Pierre Karl Péladeau.
“I am deeply moved by the events that took place this morning in Bamako. Mali is struck by unspeakable acts of terrorism. My thoughts are with the families of the victims and the Malian people, to whom I offer my sincere condolences. I also have a special thought for the Quebecers present at the scene of the tragedy, in particular for Mr. Maxime Carrier-Légaré, employee of the National Assembly of Quebec, who, fortunately, came out safe and sound, ”said Carole Poirier concluded.
