Gatineau– The Minister of Health and Social Services and Minister responsible for Seniors, Dr. Réjean Hébert, announces a budget allocation of $ 500,000 to the Center de santé et de services sociaux (CSSS) de la Vallée-de-la-Gatineau , which will enable energy efficiency measures to be carried out in three of its facilities, namely the Maniwaki Hospital and the Maniwaki and Gracefield residential centers.
“This budget allocation reiterates our government&#39;s firm desire to pursue the responsible development approach that it has undertaken for Quebec. We demand that Quebec health establishments deploy sustained efforts on a daily basis to provide the population with high-quality care and services at the best possible cost. Thanks to these energy efficiency measures, the establishment will be able to reduce its energy bill at the same time as its greenhouse gas emissions, thus leading to both economic and environmental gains, ”the Minister said. Réjean Hébert.
The work planned for the establishment&#39;s three facilities is estimated at $ 4,994,092 and should generate a reduction in annual operating costs of just over $ 240,000. This anticipated cost reduction will finance the remaining project costs.
“This announcement is undoubtedly in perfect harmony with the vision of sustainable development that we defend, for the benefit of all Quebecers. It is therefore with great joy that we today support the efforts of the CSSS de la Vallée-de-la-Gatineau in its quest for better overall efficiency, ”said the Minister of Public Security. and Minister responsible for the Outaouais region, Stéphane Bergeron.
