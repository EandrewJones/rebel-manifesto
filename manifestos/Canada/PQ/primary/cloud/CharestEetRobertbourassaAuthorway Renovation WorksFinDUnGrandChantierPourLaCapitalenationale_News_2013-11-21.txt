Quebec - The Minister of Transport and Minister of Municipal Affairs, Regions and Land Occupancy, Mr. Sylvain Gaudreault, today announced the imminent end of major redevelopment work on the Charest and Robert-Bourassa highways, as well as the commissioning of new lanes reserved for public transport.
The Minister underlined that this redevelopment project, the most important in the Capitale-Nationale region of the last 25 years, was implemented in order to improve the fluidity and safety of this strategic axis in Quebec, in addition to &#39;&#39; be completed on time and on budget.

As for the reserved lanes of Robert-Bourassa, they will be able to accommodate 500 buses daily, including 325 during peak periods. This will allow residents to consider public transit as an efficient option to get to Sainte-Foy, an area where there are many commercial and office buildings as well as educational institutions, and which has experienced incredible growth in recent years. .

On this occasion, Mr. Gaudreault was accompanied by the Minister of Labor, Employment and Social Solidarity and Minister responsible for the Capitale-Nationale region and the Chaudière-Appalaches region, Ms. Agnès Maltais, and the president of the Réseau de transport de la Capitale, Mr. Rémy Normand.
