Quebec - The most recent employment data, released this morning by Statistics Canada, show that the unemployment rate in Quebec fell by 0.3 percentage point in August to settle below 8% (7, 9%). It should also be noted that some 21,200 new full-time jobs were created in Quebec last month.
“Although the situation remains stable, we must be optimistic since since the start of the year, employment has seen good growth. The Quebec labor market is showing solidity since the jobs created in Quebec are full-time. In fact, from January to August 2013, employment increased by 62,200, including 52,300 full-time, compared to the same period in 2012 ”, indicated the Minister of Employment and Social Solidarity, Minister of Labor and Minister responsible for the Status of Women, Ms. Agnès Maltais.
Note that in 2012-2013 and 2013-2014, the Quebec government is investing more than $ 22 billion in infrastructure, a level never reached before. It should be remembered that the GDP growth forecast is 1.3% in 2013, and that to date, it has reached 1.4% compared to 2012. Furthermore, the employment situation is higher than what has been achieved. was budgeted for in November 2012, and professional prospects are favorable for the coming years, in particular with 140,000 jobs to be filled annually on average in Quebec.
In addition, the labor market in the metropolitan region has experienced a significant improvement over the past year, which has helped boost the Quebec economy. In fact, over the past twelve months, 28,200 jobs have been created in Montreal, mostly in the service sector.
The Ministère de l&#39;Emploi et de la Solidarité sociale, through Emploi-Québec, acts as a producer, user and distributor of national and regional labor market data. In particular, it produces the Bulletin sur le marché du travail au Québec, which reports monthly on changes in employment, the active population and unemployment in addition to listing information by region, sector of activity and profession, as well as comparisons with Canada and Ontario.
