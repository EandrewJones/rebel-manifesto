Quebec - The Member of Parliament for Saint-Jérôme and Leader of the Official Opposition, Pierre Karl Péladeau, as well as the Parti Québécois spokesperson for culture and communications, Véronique Hivon, offered their most sincere condolences to Ms. Rita Lafontaine, who passed away yesterday in Montreal.
“Today, Quebec is in mourning for one of the public&#39;s most beloved cultural ambassadors. Through television, cinema and theater, this generous actress will have made us experience countless emotions. On behalf of the elected officials and the great family of the Parti Québécois, I offer all my sympathy to the spouse, family members and many friends of Mrs. Rita Lafontaine, ”declared the leader of the official opposition.
“It was without reserve that she invested herself in the roles entrusted to her, creating from scratch characters that have deeply touched Quebecers. On stage or on the screen, she transmitted her passion and, over the years, several awards have come to testify to her immense talent. Rita Lafontaine is survived by generations of actors for whom she was an inspiration and a model, ”added Véronique Hivon.
In addition to having played in fifteen plays by Michel Tremblay, of whom she was a great accomplice, Rita Lafontaine has interpreted several outstanding roles in plays, films and flagship shows, notably in Cormoran, Le Retour, The Great Seduction and Grandpa.
“We will long remember his simplicity, his humor, his gentleness. Thank you very much, Madame Lafontaine, ”concluded Pierre Karl Péladeau.
