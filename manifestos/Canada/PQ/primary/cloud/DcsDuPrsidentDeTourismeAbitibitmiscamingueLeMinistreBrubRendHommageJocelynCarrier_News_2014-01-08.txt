Quebec - The Minister for Tourism, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, offers his sincere condolences to the relatives of Mr. Jocelyn Carrier, who died at the beginning of the year and whose funeral will take place in the coming days.
Mr. Carrier, well known to players in the tourism industry, was notably president for several years of ATR associés du Québec (ATRAQ), an organization bringing together all of the regional tourism associations. He had held the position of President of Tourisme Abitibi-Témiscamingue for 15 years and still sat on the board of directors of the Fondation québécoise de la succession en tourisme.
“The death of Mr. Carrier represents a great loss for the tourism industry. For years, this man worked tirelessly to have Abitibi-Témiscamingue recognized as a strong and attractive tourist destination in addition to all the work he did at ATRAQ for the benefit of all regions of the country. Quebec. Mr. Carrier has contributed to the development of our tourism industry and it is with great sadness that I learned of the news of his death. I also offer my condolences to his family, friends and colleagues at Tourisme Abitibi-Témiscamingue, ”Minister Bérubé said.
Mr. Carrier received the medal from the National Assembly in 2011, thus underlining his great contribution to the development of tourism in Quebec.
