Montreal– The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, accompanied by the Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food, François Gendron, the Minister of Finance and the Economy, Nicolas Marceau, and the Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Société des alcools du Québec (SAQ), Sylvain Simard, announced that, in the wake of the Food Sovereignty Policy, the government is committing to the deployment of a marketing and in Quebec wines, in collaboration with the SAQ.
“Our government&#39;s objective is to improve access to Quebec wines while promoting the sustainable and structured development of the wine industry. It is now ready to enter a new phase of its development. And today we are proud to support our producers by giving them the place and the visibility they deserve. We are thus meeting the expectations of winegrowers and consumers by henceforth offering, in SAQ branches, a prominent place for Quebec wines, ”indicated the Premier.
This marketing plan stems from the Support Program for the local alcohol sector, which has just been renewed for a period of one year. The current year is intended to be transitional and prepares for the new phase which will run over three years, from 2014 to 2017. This follows numerous consultations with the main players concerned as well as a strategic reflection on the sector. alcohol from Quebec. The new program will include certain bonuses that will promote the obtaining of a certification or a reserved designation. For the wine sector, this improvement amounts to $ 4.3 million for the next three years.
