Sherbrooke– Following the national council of the Parti Québécois, the leader of the Parti Québécois, Pierre Karl Péladeau, insisted on making the following clarification in order to avoid any misinterpretation.
&quot;The dialogue that the Parti Québécois wishes to maintain with the First Nations and the Inuit nation, after many achievements, notably made by René Lévesque, and the agreements reached, such as the Paix des Braves and the James Bay convention, will continue. in openness and common enrichment. Through this nation-to-nation dialogue, we are confident of reaching new mutually beneficial arrangements for all the nations concerned while respecting the integrity of the territory, ”said Pierre Karl Péladeau.
