Montreal - The leader of the Parti Québécois, Pauline Marois, is pleased to announce the candidacy of Diane De Courcy, president of the Commission scolaire de Montréal (CSDM), in the riding of Crémazie. “Diane De Courcy is an exceptional candidate for the Parti Québécois. Not only is she a formidable politician, she is also a Quebecker deeply attached to her country and to her youth, ”said Pauline Marois.
President of the CSDM and of its executive committee since 1998, she has also been president of the Movement for a Modern and Open School for over 15 years. A citizen of Ahuntsic, she knows the riding of Crémazie like the back of her hand and has represented it as school commissioner since 1994.
“The Parti Québécois and the riding of Crémazie have a long and beautiful history and I am proud to succeed women and men like Jean Campeau, Guy Tardif and Manon Blanchet. I also want to continue this tradition of involvement and service for the people of Ahuntsic and I am convinced that Pauline Marois and the entire Parti Québécois team will be in the best position to move Quebec forward in the field. &#39;interest of all, without exception, ”said Diane De Courcy.
“We have a competent team of women and men from all walks of life who want to contribute to the advancement of Quebec. We have ideas and a program that will rally the population and we have the most beautiful social projects. This electoral campaign is the perfect opportunity for Quebec to get back on track, ”concluded Pauline Marois, recalling that the Parti Québécois is the largest political formation in Quebec with its 90,000 members.
