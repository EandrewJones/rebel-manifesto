Montreal - On the eve of International Women&#39;s Day, the Premier of Quebec and leader of the Parti Québécois, Pauline Marois, announces that exceptional women are joining the Parti Québécois team, including Djemila Benhabib, candidate in Mille- He is.
“I am proud that Djemila Benhabib is part of my team. A woman of her word and conviction, she ardently defends the principle of equality between women and men. Djemila can attest to the importance of a secular state, an essential condition for making Quebec a more welcoming society, ”said Pauline Marois.
Originally from Algeria, Djemila Benhabib teaches at the University of Quebec in Trois-Rivières. Author of three essays, including My Life as a Counter-Coran, she is often invited as a speaker or columnist. She recently received the 2014 Humanist Prize and was a finalist for the Simone-de-Beauvoir Prize in 2013.
“Equality between women and men, the secularism of our institutions and the affirmation of the Quebec identity are values that are dear to me. The government has affirmed that it respects these values and puts forward its desire to ensure better living together. The Prime Minister has indeed shown that she is determined to have a charter of secularism adopted, ”declared Djemila Benhabib.
