Quebec - The Committee on Health and Social Services will begin special consultations in the National Assembly tomorrow on Bill n? 52, entitled Act respecting end-of-life care. The Minister for Social Services and Youth Protection and Minister responsible for the Dying with Dignity file, Ms. Véronique Hivon, as well as her fellow members of the parliamentary committee will hear from nearly sixty groups and experts who will speak on the issue. bill over the next four weeks.
“I am happy to continue the dialogue that began almost four years ago with the establishment of the Special Commission on the Question of Dying with Dignity. The bill, which proposes a global and integrated vision of end-of-life care, is in line with the unanimous recommendations of the Special Commission, which are the result of extensive consultation work. Despite this vast exercise, it seemed opportune to hold new consultations which could shed light on the principles as well as the precise provisions of the bill, ”declared Véronique Hivon.
Citizens are also invited to express their opinion and to comment on the bill using a form available online in the “Express your opinion” section of the National Assembly&#39;s website at www.assnat. qc.ca. For more information on the content of the bill, citizens are invited to consult the website www.mourirdansladignite.gouv.qc.ca.
“I am confident that the exchanges and discussions on the bill concerning end-of-life care will be conducted with openness and respect in order to provide the best answers to the demands of Quebec society. Remember that the goal of this process is to provide Quebec with a law that meets the needs of people at the end of their life, while respecting the complexity of their situation, with a constant concern for solidarity, respect and compassion ”, concluded the Minister.
