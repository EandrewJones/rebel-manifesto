Saint-Sauveur - As part of the Economic Policy: Priority Employment, the Member for Bertrand and Vice-President of the National Assembly, Mr. Claude Cousineau, as well as the Member for Labelle and government official for the Laurentides region, Mr. Sylvain Pagé, announced today, on behalf of the Minister of Education, Recreation and Sports, Ms. Marie Malavoy, financial assistance of $ 3,264,540.24 for the construction of the Vallée de Saint aquatic complex. -Saviour.
The aquatic complex project includes the construction of a semi-Olympic swimming pool (25 meters, 6 lanes, 2 springboards of 1 meter and 1 of 3 meters), a small swimming pool with water games and beach, changing rooms as well as meeting rooms.
“This project is of particular importance since it responds to a significant lack of sports infrastructure in the region. Its goal is to promote the health of young and old by giving them access to a most modern aquatic complex. I am therefore delighted to announce this important grant which will directly contribute to the well-being of the population of Saint-Sauveur and the surrounding area, ”declared Mr. Cousineau, Member of Parliament for Bertrand.
This project was analyzed and selected according to the criteria of the Support Program for Sports and Recreational Facilities - Phase II of the Fund for the Development of Sport and Physical Activity, which provides maximum financial assistance equivalent to half of the eligible costs.
“Across Quebec, 84 projects for the construction, renovation, development and upgrading of sports and recreational facilities will materialize thanks to investments totaling $ 130 million. These sums come from a $ 50 million envelope granted under the second component of the Sports and Recreational Infrastructure Support Program - Phase II of the Ministère de l&#39;Éducation, du Loisir et du Sport. To this envelope is added additional assistance of $ 80 million to support the implementation of new projects now, ”explained Mr. Pagé, government official for the Laurentides region.
It should be noted that these investments are planned within the framework of the Québec government&#39;s economic policy, Priority employment, recently presented by the Premier and by the Minister of Finance and the Economy, Nicolas Marceau. This policy aims in particular to create jobs and wealth throughout Quebec, in the short and medium term.
Of the approximately $ 565 million in public infrastructure announced, in addition to investments intended for the modernization of sports and recreational infrastructures, some $ 401 million is devoted to making educational infrastructures in Quebec healthier environments for learning. The completion of these projects should create around 3,500 jobs.
“For the past year, we have been stepping up our actions to improve our public infrastructure. We are doing even more today, determined to increase the level of physical activity and improve the physical condition of Quebeckers. By investing massively in our sports and recreational infrastructures, we stimulate economic development while contributing to the health, well-being and quality of life of people. In my view, these are strong and responsible actions, ”Minister Malavoy said.
