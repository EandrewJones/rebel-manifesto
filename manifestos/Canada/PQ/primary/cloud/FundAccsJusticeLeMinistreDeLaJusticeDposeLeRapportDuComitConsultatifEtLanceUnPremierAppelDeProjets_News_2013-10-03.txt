Quebec - The Minister of Justice and Attorney General of Quebec, Mr. Bertrand St-Arnaud, today tabled in the National Assembly the report of the Advisory Committee on Access to Justice.
Provided for in the Law establishing the Access to Justice Fund, this committee is notably responsible for advising the Minister of Justice on the orientations and priorities to be adopted for the financing of activities or projects centered on access to justice.
“I thank the members of the Committee for their work. I agree with their recommendations to give priority support to projects aiming at access to free or low-cost legal services for our most vulnerable fellow citizens, as well as projects encouraging the use of alternative methods of prevention and resolution of disputes ” , said the Minister of Justice.
The Advisory Committee on Accessibility to Justice is chaired by Maître Nancy Chamberland, representative of the Chambre des notaires du Québec. It is made up of Ms. Geneviève Baril, representative of the citizens; master Fernand Côté, representative of the community sector; of Maître Geneviève Fortin, representative of the Barreau du Québec; and master Pierre-Claude Lafond, representative of the university community. Ms. Sabrina Marino, from the Department of Justice, acts as secretary of the Committee.
The report can be viewed on the website of the Ministère de la Justice du Québec: www.justice.gouv.qc.ca.
In the wake of the tabling of the Advisory Committee&#39;s report, Minister Bertrand St-Arnaud also officially launched the first call for projects. For the 2013-2014 fiscal year, a budget of $ 500,000 will be devoted to the funding of projects or activities corresponding to the priorities established by the Advisory Committee.
The deadline for submitting a project is November 22, 2013. All information is available online: www.justice.gouv.qc.ca.
