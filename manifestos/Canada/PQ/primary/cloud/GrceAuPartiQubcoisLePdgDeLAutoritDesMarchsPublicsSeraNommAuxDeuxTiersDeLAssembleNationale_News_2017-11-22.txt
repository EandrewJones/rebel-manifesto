Québec, November 22, 2017 - Official Opposition spokesperson for the Conseil du trésor, Nicole Léger, is delighted that the Liberal government accepts its proposal to appoint the CEO of the Autorité des marchés publics (AMP) with the approval of two-thirds of the deputies elected to the National Assembly. As for the spokesperson for public security, Pascal Bérubé, he would like the Minister of Public Security, Martin Coiteux, to take the example of his colleague Robert Poëti and accept that the head of the Permanent Anti-Corruption Unit (UPAC ) or he, too, appointed by the elect.
IN SHORT
“From the start of the study of Bill 108, An Act to promote the monitoring of contracts by public bodies and to establish the Autorité des marchés publics, I insisted on the Minister that the CEO be appointed by a vote. two-thirds of the National Assembly, and I tabled amendments to this effect. I am delighted that Minister Robert Poëti has agreed with our arguments so that the CEO of the AMP, who will be the great supervisor of public contracts, is finally appointed by all elected officials ”, declared Nicole Léger.
However, the Liberal government still refuses to have the anti-corruption commissioner, who heads UPAC, appointed by the National Assembly. &quot;For several years, the Parti Québécois has been asking that the boss of the UPAC be appointed by a vote of at least two-thirds of the deputies, in order to prevent the one who investigates the party in power from being appointed by this same party. . I ask Minister Coiteux: take your colleague&#39;s example, be open and accept our proposal: the UPAC boss must be chosen by at least two-thirds of the deputies, ”added Pascal Bérubé.
