Montreal - The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, today officially launched the renovation work on the former motherhouse of the Sisters of Providence. The transformation of this building, built between 1884 and 1888, will soon make it possible to offer 94 affordable housing units to seniors with a slight loss of autonomy and to people who have experienced homelessness, thanks to the AccèsLogis Québec program. from the Société d&#39;habitation du Québec (SHQ), as well as a 62-room accommodation resource for the elderly with loss of autonomy. This multifunctional project, which also provides for the development of office spaces and the Social Economy House, as part of the Collective Entrepreneurship Infrastructure Program of the Ministry of Municipal Affairs, Regions and Occupation of territory, will require an investment of more than $ 35.8 million from the government of Quebec, the City of Montreal and community partners.
