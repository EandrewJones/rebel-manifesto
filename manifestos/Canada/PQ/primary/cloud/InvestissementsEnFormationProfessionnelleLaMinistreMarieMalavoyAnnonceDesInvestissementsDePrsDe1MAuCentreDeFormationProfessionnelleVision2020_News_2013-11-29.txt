Quebec - The Minister of Education, Recreation and Sports, Ms. Marie Malavoy, accompanied by the Minister of Sustainable Development, Environment, Wildlife and Parks and Minister responsible for the Center-du-Québec region, Mr. Yves-François Blanchet, announced today that investments of $ 1,000,000 would be made in the vocational training center (CFP) Vision 20 20, of the Commission scolaire des Bois-Francs.
The CFP Vision 20 20 will thus be able to carry out the expansion and upgrading work required to comply with the requirements of the Personal Assistance in Health Establishment (5316), Home Assistance (5317) and Health study programs. , assistance and nursing (5325), three programs offering good employment prospects. This work should be completed in spring 2015.
“We are making these investments for young people in Quebec. It is to our advantage that vocational training attracts them more. We also need to get more adults to explore the many possibilities it offers. Vocational training is not only essential to the development of our economy, but it allows the young people who follow it to achieve fulfillment in fields that are close to their hearts, ”said Minister Malavoy.
Government investments totaling more than $ 24.3 million were announced in September 2013 to improve the supply of vocational training. In addition to the project announced today, various other projects aiming at the expansion and redevelopment of existing vocational training centers as well as the updating and implementation of new study programs will be implemented in several regions of Quebec.
“Our region is blessed with government investments in the vocational training sector: this year, nearly $ 9 million will be invested to improve the study programs and the facilities they require. This clearly shows that education is a real instrument of development for the regions of Quebec. I am very proud to be associated with these government decisions, ”concluded Minister Blanchet.
