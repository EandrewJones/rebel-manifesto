Quebec - The Minister of Education, Recreation and Sports, Ms. Marie Malavoy, took advantage of her visit to the Center-du-Québec region to inaugurate the new vocational training center (CFP) Le Trécarré, of the Commission school in Bois-Francs. The Minister of Sustainable Development, Environment, Wildlife and Parks and Minister responsible for the Center-du-Québec region, Mr. Yves-François Blanchet, also took part in the event.
“I have come to visit you so that we can together highlight the culmination of a project that significantly improves the vocational training offer in the Center-du-Québec region. This kind of investment is necessary if we want to maintain the high quality standards of vocational training, ”said Minister Malavoy.
The construction of the new CFP required government investments of $ 3,255,139. This project was made necessary following the obtaining, by the School Board, of the authorization to offer on a permanent basis the Plastering study program (5286). “The students in plastering will study in a setting that meets their training needs. They thus receive an additional guarantee in their approach to obtain quality training, ”noted Minister Malavoy.
“As Minister responsible for the Center-du-Québec region, I encourage young people and adults in the region to acquire qualifications. It is the surest way to achieve personal and professional goals, in addition to making a concrete contribution to the prosperity and well-being of one&#39;s community, ”concluded Minister Blanchet.
Government investments totaling more than $ 24.3 million were announced in September 2013 to improve the supply of vocational training. These projects, which target the expansion and redevelopment of existing vocational training centers as well as the updating and implementation of new study programs, will be implemented in several regions of Quebec.
