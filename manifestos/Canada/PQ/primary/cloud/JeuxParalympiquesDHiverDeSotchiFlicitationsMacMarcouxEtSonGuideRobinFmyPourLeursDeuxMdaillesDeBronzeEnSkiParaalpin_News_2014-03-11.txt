Quebec - The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, and the Minister of Education, Recreation and Sport, Marie Malavoy, highlight the exceptional performance of Mac Marcoux and his guide, the Quebecer Robin Fémy, who won at Sochi, two bronze medals at the Paralympic Winter Games: one in the para-alpine ski downhill and the other in the super-G.
“These medals, symbols of sporting excellence, are a tribute to their complicity and their courage. The confidence that unites these two athletes is matched only by their audacity in the face of the challenge they have taken on together. I send them my sincere congratulations, ”said the Prime Minister.
&quot;MM. Marcoux and Fémy gave us an impressive performance. Thanks to their sustained efforts and the pooling of their talents, the dream of these two athletes has come true. In this sense, they are models of perseverance for our young people, ”declared Minister Malavoy.
Robin Fémy is one of the athletes who benefit from the support of the Government of Quebec. Several programs and measures of the Ministère de l&#39;Éducation, du Loisir et du Sport, in particular the Support Program for the Development of Sports Excellence and the tax credit measure for high-level athletes, aim to promote the practice of sport. elite, to develop a sporting culture in Quebec and to encourage the next generation.
