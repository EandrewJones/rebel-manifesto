Quebec - The Minister of Justice and Attorney General, Mr. Bertrand St-Arnaud, wishes to inform the population that the Rendez-vous avec la justice will take place from May 13 to 17 in Quebec courthouses. On the theme of &quot;justice in the service of citizens&quot;, activities will be held with the collaboration of actors and partners in the justice sector in most regions of Quebec.
“This is a great opportunity for the population to become more familiar with the Quebec legal system. People will be able to discover that our justice system facilitates the exercise of citizens&#39; rights, that it is trustworthy, ”said the Minister.
In this way, students in grades 5 and 6 of elementary school will experience the ambience of a courtroom by participating in mock trial and mediation sessions. They will enrich their knowledge of the legal professions by meeting different actors of the judiciary: judges, lawyers, court clerks, court bailiffs, etc. Students, adults and seniors can also take part in the activities offered in the various courthouses. Among other things, they will have the opportunity to visit palaces, attend a conference on de facto spouses or attend a workshop on parenting after separation.
&quot;I invite the population to take full advantage of these Rendezvous with Justice which, let us remember, once again testify to the actions of the Government of Quebec for ever more accessible and efficient justice, justice for all&quot; , concluded the Minister of Justice.
All information on the full program of activities is available on the Rendezvous with Justice microsite at [www.rendezvousjustice.ca0 (http://www.rendezvousjustice.ca). In addition, the microsite covers several topics, including bullying, legal professions, common-law partners, parenthood after break-up, small claims mediation, protection regimes and many more.
