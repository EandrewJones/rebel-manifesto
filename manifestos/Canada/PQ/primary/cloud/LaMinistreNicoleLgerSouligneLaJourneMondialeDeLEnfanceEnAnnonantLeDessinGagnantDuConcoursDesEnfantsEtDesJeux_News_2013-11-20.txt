Quebec - The Minister of Families, Ms. Nicole Léger, today unveiled the name of the winner of the Children and Games drawing competition, organized by the Ministère de la Famille as part of World Children&#39;s Day . It is the work of the young Flavie Bourbeau, four years old, from Quebec. As a prize, Flavie and her group received educational toys and craft materials. The winner&#39;s design will also be featured on the Minister&#39;s holiday card for the entire holiday season.
This year, Minister Nicole Léger wished to mark World Children&#39;s Day with a drawing competition highlighting the importance of play in early childhood. Children from all daycare services in Quebec were invited to participate in the Children and Games contest by illustrating their favorite game for the creation of a poster. More than 4000 drawings have been sent to the Ministry of the Family. To learn more about the importance of play in early childhood, go to www..mfa.gouv.qc.ca/des-enfants-et-des-jeux.
“All children need to play, because it is by playing that children develop, learn and arouse their curiosity. The Children and Games drawing competition aims to remind people that play is a fundamental right during early childhood. This is why it is important to promote it on a daily basis and to give young children time to experience a variety of play experiences, ”said Minister Nicole Léger.
Remember that World Children&#39;s Day, celebrated on November 20, is the anniversary of the adoption of the Declaration of the Rights of the Child, in 1959, and of the signing, in 1989, of the International Convention on the Rights of the Child. children by 191 countries. As a signatory to the Convention, Quebec is committed to ensuring the overall well-being of children and respect for their rights at all levels.
