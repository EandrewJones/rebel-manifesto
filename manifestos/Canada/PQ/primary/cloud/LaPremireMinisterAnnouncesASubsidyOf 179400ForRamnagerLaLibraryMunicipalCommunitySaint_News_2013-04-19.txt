Saint-Siméon– The Premier of Quebec and Member of Parliament for Charlevoix – Côte-de-Beaupré, Pauline Marois, today announced financial assistance of $ 179,400 to the Municipality of Saint-Siméon for the redevelopment and upgrading from his library.
“I am very happy for the Municipality of Saint-Siméon, which will provide itself with a high-quality library service. This sum will allow the necessary work to be carried out so that the library is equipped with modern equipment and the space is reorganized. Thus, the library will be able to exercise its mandate more effectively and respond even better to the expectations of citizens, ”said Premier Marois.
The municipal library of Saint-Siméon was founded in 1975. In December 2009, it was relocated to the old rectory, owned by the Municipality. Redevelopment, maintenance and upgrading work is now necessary so that the library meets the needs of the population.
The Municipality&#39;s project consists of reviewing the space of the library. The more spacious premises will not only make it easier to access the library, but also organize festive and informative events, and offer consultation and animation spaces.
The total cost of the project amounts to $ 397,078 and breaks down as follows: $ 179,400 comes from the Ministry of Culture and Communications, under its Capital Assistance program, and $ 217,678 is allocated by the Municipality of Saint -Simeon.
