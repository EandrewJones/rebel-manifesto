Edinburgh - Premier of Quebec Pauline Marois today met Prime Minister Alex Salmond in the Scottish Parliament.
Prime Minister Marois was in Scotland following her participation in the World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland. The meeting allowed the two heads of government to discuss the strengthening of economic collaboration.
At the end of their meeting, the two prime ministers made this statement:
“Quebec and Scotland both have modern and vibrant economies that rely on key sectors such as financial services, energy, life sciences and tourism. Our two governments are also counting on the development of foreign trade and the attraction of foreign investment as levers of economic growth. &quot;
“Since Quebec and Scotland have set themselves ambitious targets in the fight against climate change, the energy and environment sectors, in particular, appear to be most promising for strengthening our economic collaboration. Quebec aims to reduce its greenhouse gas emissions by 25% by 2020, while Scotland wants to reduce them by 80% by 2050, below the 1990 level. an opportunity to discuss our respective best practices in order to meet these commitments. Scotland also has recognized expertise in wind and tidal energy, two sectors of interest to Quebec. &quot;
“In addition to exploring the various avenues of economic and commercial collaboration, we will continue to encourage the multiplication of cultural links. For example, many Quebec artists have performed in recent years at the prestigious Edinburgh International Arts Festival. &quot;
The two prime ministers also discussed their respective political situations and concluded that Quebecers and Scots will decide their own destiny.
