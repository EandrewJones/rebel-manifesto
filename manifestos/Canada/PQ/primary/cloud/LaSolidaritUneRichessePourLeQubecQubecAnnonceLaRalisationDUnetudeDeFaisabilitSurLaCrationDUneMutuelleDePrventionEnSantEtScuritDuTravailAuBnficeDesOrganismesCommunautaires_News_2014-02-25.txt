Quebec - The Minister of Employment and Social Solidarity, Minister of Labor and Minister responsible for the Status of Women, Ms. Agnès Maltais, announces an investment of $ 50,000 aimed at carrying out a feasibility study on the creation of an occupational health and safety prevention mutual benefiting community organizations.
“Community organizations are faced with several challenges, including that of recruiting and maintaining their staff. In order to better support them, the Quebec government intends to help them improve the working conditions of employees in the community sector, ”said Minister Maltais.
The work carried out by the Government of Quebec prior to this investment of $ 50,000 revealed that the majority of employers in the community sector do not have common resources aimed at reducing the risks and costs associated with work accidents. where the relevance of financing a feasibility study on the creation of a mutual prevention in occupational health and safety.
The coordination of the realization of the feasibility study was entrusted to the Collective of integration enterprises of Quebec, a group whose mission is to support the social and professional integration of people in search of a better future. Work is scheduled to start in 2014-2015.
This measure is in line with the government orientations on which the pillar “Solidarity: a wealth for Quebec” is based, one of the four pillars of government action, along with integrity, prosperity and identity. These guidelines, made public on October 30 by Premier Pauline Marois, will guide the government&#39;s action in implementing measures aimed at improving the well-being of the most vulnerable individuals and families. The government thus wants to give new impetus to solidarity, a value inseparable from collective wealth.
