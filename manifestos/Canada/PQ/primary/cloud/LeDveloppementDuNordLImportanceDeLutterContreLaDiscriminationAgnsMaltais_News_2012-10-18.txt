Quebec - The Minister responsible for the Status of Women, Ms. Agnès Maltais, is pleased with the initiative of the Council on the Status of Women which, in its opinion published today, identifies the benefits and opportunities, but also several important issues related to to the realities and needs of women in the development of northern territories. The Council wishes to see women, especially those from Aboriginal communities, participate more in consultations and decision-making on northern development issues, an objective shared by the Minister.
“It is clear that a thorough analysis of this advice must be done so that we, as a government and as a society, can act on this call from the Council. I therefore invite departments, organizations, businesses and stakeholders from all walks of life to examine ways of contributing to the implementation of these recommendations, ”said Agnès Maltais.
“Quebec&#39;s economic development must benefit everyone and avoid accentuating inequalities. Discrimination on construction sites must end and our government is currently studying avenues to curb this phenomenon, ”the Minister concluded.
Finally, like the Council, Ms. Maltais is worried about the increase in sexual violence in the northern territories and considers it important to do everything possible to counter this phenomenon.
It should be remembered that this discussion paper, divided into four chapters, looks at various themes related to the situation of women, in particular labor needs, traditionally male occupations, training, housing and the place of women. in the construction industry and in instances.
