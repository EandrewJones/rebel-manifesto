Quebec - The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, and the Minister responsible for the Capitale-Nationale and Chaudière-Appalaches regions, Minister of Labor, Employment and Social Solidarity and Minister responsible for Status of Women, Agnès Maltais, are pleased to announce the allocation of financial assistance of $ 206,700 to the École de joaillerie de Québec and the École nationale de lutherie de Québec for the purchase of specialized equipment.
“The Government of Quebec recognizes the importance of providing our fine craft training schools with favorable conditions to ensure their sustainability. Thus, through their creative talents and skills, the students who attend them contribute to the maintenance and development of a know-how that sets us apart, ”said Minister Maka Kotto.
“The École de joaillerie and the École de lutherie are two tangible proofs of the cultural dynamism and artistic innovation capacity of the Capitale-Nationale. Through their professionalism, the staff of the two schools allows the development of niches of excellence and trains a creative and dynamic succession, ”Minister Agnès Maltais said.
The financial contribution from the Ministère de la Culture et des Communications, of $ 206,700, is paid under the Capital Assistance program. It will enable the two institutions to acquire cutting-edge equipment.
These workshop-schools provide technical education in fine crafts in partnership with the Cégep Limoilou in Quebec. They are supported in operation by the Society for the Development of Cultural Enterprises (SODEC), as part of its assistance program for schools-workshops in crafts. This program is aimed at 14 accredited workshop-schools as part of the National Art Trades Training Plan. The annual subsidy granted by SODEC is used for the development of research, dissemination and support activities for the organization&#39;s professional environment.
