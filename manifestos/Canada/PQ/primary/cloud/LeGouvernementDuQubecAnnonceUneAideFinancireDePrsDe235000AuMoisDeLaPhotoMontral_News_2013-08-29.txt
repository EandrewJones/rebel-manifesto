Montreal - The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, and the Minister of International Relations, La Francophonie and Foreign Trade and Minister responsible for the Montreal region, Jean-François Lisée, announce the allocation of aid funding of $ 234,600 in support of the Photo Month in Montreal 2013, which will take place from September 5 to October 5, under the theme Drone: the automated image.
“The biennial Le Mois de la Photo offers the public a daring program made up of original works by artists from here and elsewhere. Thanks to the free exhibitions presented in 14 venues in Montreal, the event is a great showcase for the discovery of this contemporary art which has been able to adapt by marrying traditional techniques with new technologies, ”Minister Kotto said.
“The Month of Photography in Montreal offers Montrealers and visitors an ideal opportunity to appreciate photographic works that are exclusive or that are presented for the first time in the country. This exhibition is available in several Montreal venues, making the metropolis a veritable territory of exploration. I am proud to support this eminently creative and innovative cultural event, ”added Minister Lisée.
The contribution from the Government of Quebec is broken down as follows: $ 160,000 comes from the Support for Operation - National and International Events program for digital arts, visual arts, film and video, fine crafts and architectural research from the Conseil des arts et des lettres du Québec, $ 60,000 from the Metropolitan Initiative and Outreach Fund, administered by the Secretariat for the Metropolitan Region, and $ 14,600 from the Project Assistance program of the Ministère de la Culture and Communications.
