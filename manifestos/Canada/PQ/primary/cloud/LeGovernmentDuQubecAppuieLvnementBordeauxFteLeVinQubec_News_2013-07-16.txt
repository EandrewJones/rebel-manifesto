Quebec - The Minister of Labor, Employment and Social Solidarity, Minister responsible for the Status of Women and Minister responsible for the Capitale-Nationale and Chaudière-Appalaches regions, Ms. Agnès Maltais, and the Minister for Tourism , Mr. Pascal Bérubé, are pleased to announce the allocation of financial assistance of $ 80,000 to support the Bordeaux Fête le Vin à Québec event which will take place from August 29 to September 1.
For its second year, Bordeaux celebrates wine in Quebec City offers a variety of activities that will allow all lovers of wine and gastronomy to discover the finesse of Bordeaux wines and taste products with a regional flavor. Participants will therefore be able to browse the various pavilions erected for this occasion, enjoy the gourmet kiosks, participate in training workshops, and finally, attend a wide variety of shows. In short, there will be something for everyone.
“Once again this year, the population and visitors to Quebec City will have the pleasure of discovering this event which highlights the richness of Quebec&#39;s terroir as well as the dynamism of our producers. It is with great pride that the Government of Quebec is therefore associated with this popular gathering which, for many, constitutes a must-see during the summer season, ”said Minister Maltais.
“Bordeaux celebrates wine in Quebec is an exceptional event that diversifies and enriches the region&#39;s tourist offer. This event mobilizes large crowds who have come to enjoy the products of Bordeaux and our region, while taking advantage of Québec&#39;s many tourist attractions, ”said Minister Bérubé.
Government assistance breaks down as follows: $ 60,000 comes from the Tourism Industry Development Program - Financial assistance for festivals and tourist events, from Tourisme Québec, and the Secrétariat à la Capitale-Nationale contributes an amount of $ 20,000.
