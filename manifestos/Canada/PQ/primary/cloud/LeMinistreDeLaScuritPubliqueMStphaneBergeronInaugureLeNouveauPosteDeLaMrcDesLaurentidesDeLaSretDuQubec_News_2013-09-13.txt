Quebec - The Minister of Public Security, Stéphane Bergeron, proceeded today, along with the Member for Bertrand and Vice-President of the National Assembly, Claude Cousineau, as well as the Director General of the Sûreté du Québec, Mr. Mario Laprise, at the inauguration of the new Sûreté du Québec post in the MRC des Laurentides.
Located at 501, rue Léonard, in Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts, the new station consolidates the position of the Sûreté du Québec in the MRC and offers police officers a work environment at the cutting edge of technology.
“By fighting day after day, both to prevent and to contain and reduce crime on the territory, police officers contribute, with the expertise and dedication that we know to them, to ensuring a safe living environment for all population of the MRC des Laurentides. I am convinced that this new position of the Sûreté du Québec is already having positive effects on police interventions, for the benefit of the population of the region ”declared Minister Bergeron.
Built at a cost of $ 6.1 million and respecting the environment, the MRC des Laurentides substation is in the process of obtaining LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) certification. &quot;This new building represents the desire of the Sûreté du Québec to offer high quality services to the population it serves, but also to contribute to improving the quality of life of citizens, as well as of its employees. , through sustainable development, ”said Mr. Mario Laprise.
The new building houses 53 police officers and 6 civilian members, combining their expertise to provide police coverage adapted to the needs of the population. It will bring together under one roof the administration, the gendarmerie, criminal investigations as well as detention and storage facilities.
