Quebec - On the occasion of the weekly vigil held in front of the National Assembly, the member for Chicoutimi and leader of the official opposition, Stéphane Bédard, the member for Hochelaga-Maisonneuve and spokesperson for international relations, Carole Poirier, the deputy for Taschereau and parliamentary leader, Agnès Maltais, as well as several other members of the Parti Québécois have expressed their support for blogger Raif Badawi.
“Every week since the start of this mobilization, the Parti Québécois has been present, with the citizens, to demand the release of Raif Badawi and to support his children and his wife, Ensaf Haidar. One year after his sentence to 10 years in prison and 1000 lashes, it is a sad anniversary that we mark today. The premiers of Quebec and Canada must intervene urgently and concretely so that Raif Badawi is released and reunites with his family. The wait has been going on for a year; it is unacceptable! », Declared Stéphane Bédard.
“All of Quebec is mobilized for Raif. Vigils, letters, marches, support from the Barreau du Québec and Avocats sans frontières, resolutions from several municipal councils and the National Assembly… all civil means were used to demand the blogger&#39;s release. Prime Ministers Philippe Couillard and Stephen Harper, as well as Quebec&#39;s Minister of International Relations, Christine St-Pierre, and her federal counterpart in Foreign Affairs, Rob Nicholson, must now get involved personally and deploy all possible diplomatic levers. so that Raif Badawi can quickly find his wife and children in Sherbrooke, ”added Carole Poirier.
