Quebec - Following the announcement of his death, the member for Chicoutimi and leader of the official opposition, Stéphane Bédard, wishes to pay tribute to the Archbishop Emeritus of the Diocese of Montreal, Cardinal Jean-Claude Turcotte.
“Quebecers and Montrealers will remember a devoted man who created lasting ties with people and organizations in his community. Concerned about the fate of the most disadvantaged, he first exercised his ministry within the working class, from which he came and from which he has always remained very close. I offer my most sincere condolences to his family, his friends, as well as his colleagues at the Archdiocese of Montreal, ”said Stéphane Bédard.
Among the accomplishments of Cardinal Turcotte, let us mention his active participation in the organization of the visit of Pope John Paul II to Quebec in 1984. He also held many managerial positions within the Catholic Church, where he was respected and appreciated by his peers.
Born in Montreal, Jean-Claude Turcotte was ordained priest in 1959. In 1990, he was appointed archbishop of the diocese of Montreal; he became cardinal in 1994.
