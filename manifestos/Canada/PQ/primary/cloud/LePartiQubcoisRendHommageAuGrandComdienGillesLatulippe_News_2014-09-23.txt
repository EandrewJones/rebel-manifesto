Quebec - It is with sadness that the member for Chicoutimi and leader of the official opposition, Stéphane Bédard, and the member for Joliette and spokesperson for culture and communications, Véronique Hivon, learned of the death of Mr. Gilles Latulippe. He passed away today at the Montreal General Hospital. He was 77 years old.
“Gilles Latulippe is a true monument of theater, comedy and culture here. Quebec king of burlesque and variety, he left his mark at the start of the cabaret era. With his associates, including Olivier Guimond, Claude Blanchard, Suzanne Lapointe and Fernand Gignac, to name just a few, he bequeaths an immense legacy to Quebec culture. On behalf of the parliamentary wing of the Parti Québécois, I offer my most sincere condolences to the family and loved ones of this great Quebecer, ”declared Stéphane Bédard.
Véronique Hivon recalled, for her part, the large number of television shows in which he participated. Symphorien, Poivre et Sel, Les Brillant, Les Démons du midi, among others, touched the hearts of Quebecers at various times of our television. Mr. Latulippe was also the father of the illustrious Théâtre des Variétés in Montreal; in 2004, the hall was renamed in his honor.
“There is no doubt that Gilles Latulippe will forever be remembered by thousands of Quebecers, for whom he was such a familiar figure. Through him there invariably arrived joy and gaiety; her memory will always be heartwarming. Obviously, he had unconditional love for his audience and his playing partners, who made him feel good; As evidenced by her long and successful career, ”concluded the Member for Joliette.
