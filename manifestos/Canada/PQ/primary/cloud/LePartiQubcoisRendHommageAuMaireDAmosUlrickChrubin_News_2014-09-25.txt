Quebec - It is with sadness that the member for Chicoutimi and leader of the official opposition, Stéphane Bédard, and the member for Abitibi-Ouest, François Gendron, learned of the death of the mayor of Amos, Ulrick Chérubin, which occurred today. &#39;hui at the Amos hospital center.
“On behalf of the parliamentary wing of the Parti Québécois, I offer my sincere condolences to the family and loved ones of Mr. Chérubin. My thoughts are also with the entire community of Amos and Abitibi-Témiscamingue, which is losing a worthy representative of the region. Through his unparalleled kindness and his great dedication to his fellow citizens, Mayor Chérubin left his mark on Quebec as a whole, ”declared Stéphane Bédard.
Ulrick Chérubin has been mayor of Amos since 2002. He was re-elected on November 3 with more than 72% of the vote. Thanks to its commitment, the city of Amos has experienced a particularly dynamic period of development in recent years. In particular, the quality and diversity of the activities surrounding the celebration of the centenary of Amos, which takes place this year, were praised by the entire population.
The deputy for Abitibi-Ouest, François Gendron, wished to testify to the great loss that the city of Amos is experiencing. “The Amoss and Abitibi community is losing a giant. Ulrick was a fighter; he cared about the development of his city. He was a man of stature, of immense generosity, anxious to be close to his fellow citizens. Above all, he wanted to carry out and put forward his projects, for the benefit of the population, ”he said.
