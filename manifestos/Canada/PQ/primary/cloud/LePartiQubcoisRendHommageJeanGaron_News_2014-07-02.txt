Quebec City, July 2, 2014 - It is with sadness that the leader of the official opposition, Stéphane Bédard, learned of the death of Mr. Jean Garon, on Tuesday, July 1, at the Hôtel? Dieu in Lévis. He was 76 years old.
“On behalf of the great family of the Parti Québécois and on my behalf, I offer my condolences to the family and loved ones of Jean Garon. A great communicator, his contribution to the emergence of the sovereignist movement, in particular, is important. He leaves an immense legacy to the agricultural world and to Quebec politics, ”declared Stéphane Bédard.
Lawyer and economist, Jean Garon was Member of Parliament for the riding of Lévis from 1976 to 1998. Minister of Agriculture in the first government of René Lévesque, he held this post until 1985. He became Minister of Education from 1994 to 1996. Subsequently, he was mayor of Lévis from 1998 to 2005.
Jean Garon was at the origin of many reforms for the agricultural community, in particular the Law on the protection of the territory and agricultural activities, commonly called the law on agricultural zoning. This essential law ensured, among other things, the preservation of agricultural land, highly coveted at the time. It also allowed the sustainability and development of sustainable agriculture in Quebec.
Abitibi-West MP François Gendron also wished to recall the outstanding contribution of his former fellow MP and Minister.
“Jean Garon was a tireless worker, who accomplished great things for Quebec and its agriculture. He has always worked with great dedication for the farmers and fishermen of Quebec. It brought our agriculture out of the doldrums and allowed it to enter modernity, ”he said.
