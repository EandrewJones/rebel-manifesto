Quebec - The member for Chicoutimi and leader of the official opposition, Stéphane Bédard, pays tribute to the former member of the Bloc Québécois, Maurice Dumas, who died Sunday at the age of 87.
“On behalf of my fellow MPs, I extend my sincere condolences to the family and loved ones of Maurice Dumas. We have lost a remarkable man, passionate, and committed to the sovereignty of Quebec. Very attached to the French language and to the roots of Quebec culture, we will remember him as a proud person who was committed to celebrating the difference in Quebec, ”said Stéphane Bédard.
Maurice Dumas was among the first cohort of Bloc Québécois MPs in the House of Commons. He represented the citizens of the federal riding of Argenteuil – Papineau from 1993 to 2000.
