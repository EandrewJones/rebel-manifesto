Quebec - It is with sadness that the member for Chicoutimi and leader of the official opposition, Stéphane Bédard, learned of the death of Mr. Marcel Masse on August 25. He was 78 years old.
“On behalf of my fellow members of the Parti Québécois, I offer my condolences to the family, relatives and many friends of Marcel Masse. Throughout his public life, he has been a great defender of Quebec society, of its culture and of the French language, here and across the world, particularly in France, with which he has maintained special ties ”, said Stéphane Bédard.
After having held numerous ministerial positions in Quebec, from 1966 to 1970, Marcel Masse did the same at the federal level, from 1984 to 1993. He then fulfilled several political and public functions, notably the presidency of the Montreal Regional Commission and the vice-president. -Presidency of the National Commission on the Future of Quebec in 1995. He also chaired the Council of the French Language and the Commission of Cultural Property of Quebec, and was Delegate General of Quebec in Paris from January 1, 1996 to June 2, 1997.
