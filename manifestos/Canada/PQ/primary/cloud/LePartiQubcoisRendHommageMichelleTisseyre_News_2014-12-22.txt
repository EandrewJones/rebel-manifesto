Québec– The MNA for Joliette and official opposition critic for culture and communications, Véronique Hivon, as well as the MNA for Hochelaga-Maisonneuve and spokesperson for the status of women, Carole Poirier, wish to pay tribute to the great journalist and host Michelle Tisseyre, who died yesterday.
“On behalf of all my colleagues in the parliamentary wing of the Parti Québécois, I offer my sincere condolences to the family and loved ones of Michelle Tisseyre. A great lady of radio and television, she was one of the most prominent personalities in Quebec for nearly 25 years. Quebec will remember a journalist and a host of many talents, all in simplicity and driven by the desire to provide rigorous and quality information, ”declared Véronique Hivon.
Michelle Tisseyre was the first woman to host a newscast, the Radiojournal, in 1941. She was also the helm of the first television talk show, Rendez-vous avec Michelle, and co-hosted the first public affairs program. , Today.
“Michelle Tisseyre paved the way for generations of female journalists. In particular, on radio and television, it will have contributed to making Quebecers better understand the great changes of the Quiet Revolution and their consequences. She was a true pioneer of Quebec journalism, ”concluded Carole Poirier.
