Quebec - The MNA for Hochelaga-Maisonneuve and Parti Québécois spokesperson on the status of women, Carole Poirier, wished to underline the social and feminist commitment of Solange Fernet Gervais, who died recently.
“Ms. Fernet Gervais has dedicated her life to the advancement of society, rural affairs and women&#39;s rights. Businesswoman, co-founder and co-owner of Ferme Denijoy, she has also been involved, as a volunteer, in various sectors of activity. She is a founding member of 10 organizations, including the Women&#39;s Association for Education and Social Action (AFEAS) and the Normandy Volunteer Action Center, of which she was Executive Director for nine years. On behalf of the Parti Québécois, I would like to offer my sincere condolences to the family and loved ones of Solange Fernet Gervais, ”said Carole Poirier.
“While raising her seven children, Ms. Fernet Gervais was involved in the development of her community and her region, the Mauricie. She is an example of commitment and work-family balance for the well-being of her fellow citizens. In addition, she has served on numerous boards of directors; moreover, she was the first woman to sit on that of the daily Le Devoir. For all these years of work, thank you, Solange Fernet Gervais, ”concluded the member for the Parti Québécois.
