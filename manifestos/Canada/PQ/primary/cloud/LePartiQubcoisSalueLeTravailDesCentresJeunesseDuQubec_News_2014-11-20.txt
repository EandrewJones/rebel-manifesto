Quebec - Jean-François Lisée, the member for Rosemont and official opposition spokesperson for social services, youth protection, home care and prevention, underlines Youth Centers Week 2014, which takes place takes place from November 16 to 22.
“This is an opportunity to recognize the work of more than 14,000 women and men who have been united around a single goal for more than 20 years: that of supporting and helping young people in difficulty and their families. I would like to salute their remarkable commitment and express my gratitude to them for all the energy they devote to these thousands of young people who, each year, attend youth centers in search of human and professional support. Thanks to this support, they have the opportunity to flourish and realize their dreams, ”said Jean-François Lisée.
The deputy also expressed the concern of many researchers, experts, managers and professionals in the field of youth protection about the impacts of Bill 10. “The reform of Minister Gaétan Barrette proposes to merge youth centers with all other health establishments to create new mega-infrastructures. Thus, we are preparing to dismantle Quebec&#39;s youth protection model, which is nevertheless effective and envied beyond our borders. Youth centers have essential expertise and offer a range of services to some of the most vulnerable young people in our society. It is therefore essential that this Quebec success be preserved and promoted, ”concluded Jean-François Lisée.
