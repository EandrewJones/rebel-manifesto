Quebec - At the invitation of the Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, the Secretary General of the International Organization of La Francophonie (OIF), Mr. Abdou Diouf, will be on an official visit to Quebec on September 18 and 19. The Secretary General will deliver an address to the National Assembly on Wednesday, September 18, which is an exceptional parliamentary event and a privilege that honors Quebec. “This man with a remarkable career has managed, thanks to his moral authority, his talent and his vision, to increase the political influence of the OIF and the international influence of the French language. In this regard, Mr. Diouf is an unwavering ally of Quebec, ”said the Premier.
“Mr. Diouf has always made human rights one of his great struggles. Both as President of Senegal and Secretary General of the OIF, his contribution to this chapter will make history, ”added Pauline Marois.
In the morning, the Secretary General will meet with the Prime Minister to take stock of current international issues and the future of La Francophonie. He will then have a working session with the Minister of International Relations, La Francophonie and Foreign Trade, Jean-François Lisée. Mr. Diouf will also meet with the staff of the Institut de la Francophonie pour le développement durable (IFDD), a subsidiary body of the OIF headquartered in Quebec.
The Secretary General will take part, on Thursday, September 19, in a lunch-conference in front of the Council of International Relations of Montreal (CORIM). He will complete his stay with a series of exchanges with young people, organized by Les Offices jeunesse internationales du Québec, whose themes will touch on culture, the promotion of French, entrepreneurship and civic engagement.
