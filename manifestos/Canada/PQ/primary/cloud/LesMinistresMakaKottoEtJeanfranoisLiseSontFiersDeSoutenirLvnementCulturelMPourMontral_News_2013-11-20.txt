Montreal - The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, and the Minister of International Relations, La Francophonie and Foreign Trade and Minister responsible for the Montreal region, Jean-François Lisée, announce financial assistance totaling $ 87,000 , as part of the launch of M for Montreal, which will take place from November 20 to 23, 2013.
The Government of Quebec thus contributes to the development of emerging artists on the international scene and highlights the cultural vitality of Quebec, which is distinguished by its creativity and musical avant-garde. &quot;M for Montreal is an exceptional showcase for our new talents and can serve as a gateway for them to develop their careers on an international scale,&quot; said Minister Kotto.
M for Montreal, in addition to being a crossroads for meetings and exchanges between industry representatives and artists, is a real springboard for our musical succession. “M for Montreal is a key moment for Quebec&#39;s musical culture and for its international influence. The original mission and avant-garde programming of this event confirm the creativity and reputation of our cultural metropolis internationally, ”added Jean-François Lisée.
The Society for the Development of Cultural Enterprises (SODEC) grants a grant of $ 35,000 to M for Montreal, to which is added $ 25,000 from the Assistance for International Projects program of the Ministère de la Culture et des Communications and 27 $ 000 from the Metropolitan Area Secretariat&#39;s Initiative and Outreach Fund.
At the same time, the 3rd edition of Mundial Montreal will be held, which celebrates the richness of world music. Organized by the founder of M for Montreal, the event benefits from a grant of $ 25,000 from SODEC.
