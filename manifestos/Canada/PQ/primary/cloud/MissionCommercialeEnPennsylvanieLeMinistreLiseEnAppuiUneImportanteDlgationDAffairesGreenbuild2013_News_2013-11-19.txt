Quebec - The Minister of International Relations, La Francophonie and Foreign Trade (MRIFCE), Jean-François Lisée, will be in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, from November 20 to 22, to participate in an economic mission as part of Greenbuild 2013 , dedicated to green construction. The Minister, accompanied by some twenty Quebec companies, will take advantage of the event to promote Quebec&#39;s expertise in sustainable building.
“Quebec has the know-how and the technology to be a world leader in eco-construction. By adopting a Foreign Trade Development Plan, the Government of Quebec is committed to helping Quebec businesses that stand out in sectors of excellence, particularly in sustainable building, in their export efforts. I am delighted to see Quebec industry representatives promoting our excellence in the United States, one of our priority markets, ”said the Minister.
This visit to Pennsylvania will also be an opportunity for Mr. Lisée to meet important economic and political players in the region in order to strengthen trade and explore new avenues of collaboration with this state. Note that Pennsylvania is Quebec&#39;s 5th largest trading partner in the United States, with trade totaling more than $ 3.5 billion in 2012.
Greenbuild is the world&#39;s largest green building trade fair. It brings together more than 1,000 exhibitors and 30,000 visitors from more than 90 countries participate each year. The Quebec showcase of eco-construction companies is organized by Export Quebec, a unit of MRIFCE, and the General Delegation of Quebec in New York, in partnership with Développement PME Chaudière-Appalaches, the Chamber of Commerce of Metropolitan Montreal, the Quebec section of the Council of the sustainable building of Canada, the Société d&#39;habitation du Québec and the ERAI / Expansion Québec office in Philadelphia.
