Port-Daniel – Gascons– The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, accompanied by the Minister for the Regions and Minister responsible for the Gaspésie – Îles-de-la-Madeleine region, Gaétan Lelièvre, and the Member for Bonaventure, Sylvain Roy , confirmed the agreement in principle with the company Ciment McInnis for the construction of a cement plant in Port-Daniel – Gascons, in the Gaspé.
The president and chief executive officer of the Caisse de dépôt et placement du Québec, Michael Sabia, the president and chief executive officer of Investissement Québec, Mario Albert, and the president of Groupe Beaudier, Laurent Beaudoin, were also present during this announcement. .
This project, estimated at $ 1 billion, will result in the creation of 2,300 jobs during the construction phase, which will begin in spring 2014, and some 400 jobs will be linked to the commissioning of the cement plant, scheduled for spring 2016.
The new cement plant will have an annual capacity of 2.2 million tonnes of cement. The announced project also aims to operate a limestone quarry located nearby. This deposit represents a supply lasting approximately 100 years. In addition, a marine terminal will be built in the region to export cement to the east coast of the United States, the main foreign market coveted by the company.
