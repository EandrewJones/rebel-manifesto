Quebec? The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, accompanied by the Minister of Labor, Minister of Employment and Social Solidarity and Minister responsible for the Status of Women, Agnès Maltais, today announced the appointment of a special mediator, Normand Gauthier, in order to find a solution to the labor dispute that continues in the construction industry. The mandate of the latter will be to deploy, at the institutional - commercial and industrial table, all the necessary efforts to intensify the dynamics of the negotiations and to bring the management party and the union party towards the conclusion of an agreement before the end of the negotiation. week.
&quot;Seasoned mediator, Mr. Gauthier will use all his know-how to try to bring the parties together at this crucial stage of the negotiations,&quot; declared the Prime Minister.
Normand Gauthier intervened in many cases, including that of the labor dispute at the Commission de la construction du Québec in 2012. He also worked at the Ministry of Labor for 22 years, notably as a mediator-conciliator. He ended his career as Deputy Minister there in 2001, before becoming President of the Essential Services Council until 2007.
“I would like to thank Normand Gauthier for having accepted this challenge, which affects a key sector of our economy, in each region of Quebec. I formally ask the parties to put aside their differences in order to quickly reach a compromise, for the benefit of the entire population, ”concluded the Minister of Labor, Agnès Maltais.
The Prime Minister also said she was satisfied with the agreement reached in the civil engineering and roads sector, which ends the strike for more than 41,000 construction workers, who will be back to work on Tuesday. This agreement in principle shows that a negotiated settlement is possible.
