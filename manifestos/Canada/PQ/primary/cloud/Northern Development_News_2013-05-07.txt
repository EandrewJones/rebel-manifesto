Chibougamau– The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, and the Minister of Natural Resources, Martine Ouellet, today announced, in Chibougamau, the main government orientations in the area of northern development. On this occasion, they were accompanied by the Minister for Canadian Intergovernmental Affairs, the Canadian Francophonie and Sovereignty Governance and Minister responsible for the Nord-du-Québec region, Alexandre Cloutier, the Minister of Transport and Minister of Municipal Affairs, of Regions and Land Occupancy, Sylvain Gaudreault, of the Minister for Native Affairs, Élizabeth Larouche, and of the Parliamentary Assistant to the Minister of Natural Resources and Member of Parliament for Ungava, Luc Ferland.
“We want to develop the North in a responsible manner in order to maximize the benefits for local communities and for all Quebecers. To do this, we are announcing that the new Northern Development Fund will allow us to invest more than $ 800 million in public infrastructure over the next five years. We will propose a new framework for funding infrastructure related to northern projects, we will put forward concrete and innovative solutions to ensure the well-being and development of communities and, finally, we will ensure harmonious development that respects the environment. &#39;environment. The next northern development strategy will be based on solid foundations, ”said Prime Minister Pauline Marois.
