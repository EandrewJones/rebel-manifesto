Montreal - The leader of the Parti Québécois, Pauline Marois, pledges to implement a refundable tax credit to promote the teaching and learning of the arts. “We want to facilitate artistic practice among young people. We will support parents financially and we will help children thrive through the arts, ”she explained.
This refundable tax credit of $ 500 per child aged 5 to 16 will apply for any registration in an artistic practice for families with an annual income of less than $ 130,000. This will hopefully benefit the parents of about 250,000 young people in this age group and cost $ 20 million per year.
“The Parti Québécois has always been very committed to culture. This is what allows us to dream and imagine new horizons. Culture is also one of the pillars of our economy. With this tax credit, we will encourage the development of our children as well as the blossoming of future talents, artists and creators of tomorrow. Among today&#39;s young people aged 5 to 16 are the actors, musicians, circus artists, artistic creators and painters who will make us dream and make Quebec culture shine around the world in 20 or 30 years. years, ”concluded Pauline Marois.
