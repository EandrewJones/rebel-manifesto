Quebec - The Leader of the Official Opposition and Member of Parliament for Saint-Jérôme, Pierre Karl Péladeau, announces changes to the parliamentary wing of the Parti Québécois team.
The member for Chicoutimi, Stéphane Bédard, becomes House leader of the official opposition and critic for the economy, entrepreneurship, SMEs and exports. The deputy for Taschereau, Agnès Maltais, is appointed deputy leader, while retaining the responsibilities of spokesperson for the Capitale-Nationale region as well as in matters of secularism, reform of democratic institutions and electoral law. .
“These two deputies are seasoned parliamentarians, well aware of the debates in the National Assembly. They represent an asset and a strength for the Parti Québécois team. I would also like to thank Stéphane Bédard for the excellent work he did as leader of the official opposition. He led our wonderful team of MPs with flying colors. My thanks also go to Agnès Maltais, for her fine work as parliamentary leader. She will continue to play an important role within this team, ”said Pierre Karl Péladeau.
The leader of the official opposition also paid tribute to the work of the member for Verchères, Stéphane Bergeron. “As deputy leader, Stéphane was a centerpiece of the parliamentary work carried out by our team. I thank him very warmly. I am also very happy since he will continue his effective work as spokesperson vis-à-vis the government, particularly in matters of Canadian intergovernmental affairs, ”he concluded.
