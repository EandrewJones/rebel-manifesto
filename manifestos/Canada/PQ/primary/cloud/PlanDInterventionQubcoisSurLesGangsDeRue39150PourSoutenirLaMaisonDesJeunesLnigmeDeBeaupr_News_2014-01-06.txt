Quebec - As part of the Quebec intervention plan on street gangs, the Premier of Quebec and Member of Parliament for Charlevoix-Côte-de-Beaupré, Pauline Marois, and the Minister of Public Security, Stéphane Bergeron, announce the granting of in the sum of $ 39,150 to the Maison des jeunes l&#39;Énigme de Beaupré.
“Through their commitment and targeted actions, the workers in this organization help prevent and counter the phenomenon of street gangs. It is therefore with pride that we support them concretely in their efforts. The mission of the Maison des jeunes l&#39;Énigme de Beaupré is a fine example of an initiative that the Government of Quebec wishes to encourage; the organization must continue this essential work, ”said the Premier.
&quot;I am happy to financially support this organization&#39;s project, which aims to offer young people in the community various services adapted to their needs, in order to prevent crime and youth joining street gangs,&quot; added the Minister of Public Security.
This program ensures that the pressure on street gangs is maintained and offers young people perspectives other than the world of delinquency and criminality. It makes it possible to pursue actions aimed at reducing the crime associated with street gangs, deterring young people at risk from joining them and helping members of street gangs to leave this universe.
