Senneterre - As part of a tour of Quebec which stops today in Abitibi-Témiscamingue, the Minister for the Regions and Minister responsible for the Gaspésie-Îles-de-la-Madeleine region, Mr. Gaétan Lelièvre, accompanied by the Deputy Premier, Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food, Minister responsible for the Abitibi-Témiscamingue region and Member of Parliament for the riding of Abitibi-Ouest, Mr. François Gendron, and the Minister for Native Affairs and Member of Parliament for the Abitibi-Est constituency, Ms. Élizabeth Larouche, confirmed the granting of nearly $ 6 million to rural RCMs in the Abitibi-Témiscamingue region and to the City of Rouyn-Noranda for the implementation of the National Rurality Policy. Following a commitment by the Premier of Quebec, Ms. Pauline Marois, to improve rural pacts, the National Policy on Rurality was renewed for a period of ten years with an envelope of $ 470 million. Created in 2001 by the Parti Québécois, the National Policy on Rurality provides unprecedented support to rural areas and regions of Quebec.
