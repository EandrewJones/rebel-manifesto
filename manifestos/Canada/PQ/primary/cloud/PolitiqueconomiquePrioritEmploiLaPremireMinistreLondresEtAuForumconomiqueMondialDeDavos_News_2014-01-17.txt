Quebec - The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, will carry out an economic mission to the United Kingdom and Switzerland, from January 20 to 24. After a visit to London where she will deliver, in the financial heart of the British capital, a speech on investment opportunities in Quebec, the Prime Minister will participate in the 44th annual meeting of the World Economic Forum in Davos.
&quot;This mission will be a unique opportunity to promote Quebec, to present the Priorité emploi economic policy, launched last October, and to meet key players in the financial, economic and political fields, whether heads of government, investors or business leaders who have specific interests for Quebec, ”declared the Premier.
Unmissable event for economic and political decision-makers, this 44th edition of the World Economic Forum in Davos revolves around the theme The Reshaping of the World: Consequences for Society, Politics and Business.
In addition to participating in high-level economic meetings, the Premier will take part in two workshops, including one on energy alternatives in the transport sector, a subject of interest to Quebec, which has just been launch its transport electrification strategy. The second workshop will focus on ways to better adapt training to the job market, particularly for young people.
“The incentives that we have put in place within the framework of the Economic Policy, combined with the know-how, the creativity and the immense capacity for innovation of Quebecers, make Quebec a particularly favorable place for investment. This is the message we want to deliver in order to ensure our growth and prosperity, ”concluded the Premier.
