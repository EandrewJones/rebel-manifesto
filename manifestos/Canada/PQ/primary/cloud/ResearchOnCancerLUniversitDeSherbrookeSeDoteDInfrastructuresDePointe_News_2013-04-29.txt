Sherbrooke– The Minister of Veterans Affairs and Minister for La Francophonie, the Honorable Steven Blaney, the Minister of Higher Education, Research, Science and Technology, Mr. Pierre Duchesne, and the President of the Université de Sherbrooke, Prof. Luce Samoisette, gathered to inaugurate the new Pavilion for Applied Cancer Research (PRAC) at the Université de Sherbrooke. They were accompanied for the occasion by the Minister of Health and Social Services and Minister responsible for the Estrie region, Dr. Réjean Hébert, the Member of Parliament for Sherbrooke, Mr. Serge Cardin, and the Dean of the Faculty of Medicine. and health sciences from the University of Sherbrooke, Prof. Pierre Cossette.
“Our government has invested in innovation and infrastructure to improve the quality of life for Canadians and to lay the foundations for economic prosperity. Thanks to the investments that our government has made in the Pavilion for Applied Research on Cancer, researchers at the University of Sherbrooke are now equipped with the necessary tools to pursue world-class research, ”said the Honorable Steven Blaney on behalf of from the Minister of Industry, the Honorable Christian Paradis.
The pavilion was made possible with a total financial assistance of $ 31.4 million granted under the Knowledge Infrastructure Program. A sum of $ 22.3 million comes from the Government of Quebec, while Industry Canada has contributed $ 9.1 million.
“At the dawn of the tabling of the National Research and Innovation Policy, the government can already rely on a health research network that stands out. It is therefore essential for Quebec that our universities equip themselves with cutting-edge research infrastructures and this pavilion brilliantly illustrates this. I am delighted with the collaboration between the institutions and the pooling of resources which are essential to optimize the development of research and to maximize its effects, ”declared Minister Pierre Duchesne.
