Quebec - At the end of the working session following the hearings of the parliamentary committee on the relevance and impacts of regulating the sale price to the public of new printed and digital books, the Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto is committed to proposing measures to the 12,000 Quebeckers working in this industry within a reasonable period of time.
“The situation of independent bookstores is worrying and I am very sensitive to it. The Parti Québécois had undertaken to hold a parliamentary commission on this issue. I am very proud to have honored this commitment within the first year of our mandate ”, declared the Minister.
At the end of the parliamentary commission, Minister Kotto makes six main observations:
“I am satisfied to see the work accomplished over the past few weeks. This democratic exercise made it possible to better understand the ins and outs of regulating the sale price of new books, while opening up more reflection on the reading and consumption habits of Quebeckers, creation and production. culture. I will provide concrete and viable solutions in order to maintain a network of independent bookstores throughout Quebec and it will not take me five years to do so, ”concluded Mr. Kotto.
