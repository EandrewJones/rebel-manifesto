Quebec - The Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, announces the allocation of financial assistance of $ 179,000 to the Municipality of Saint-Gédéon-de-Beauce, for the development of a library in the former convent of the Sisters of Charity of Saint-Louis.
&quot;I congratulate the leaders of the Municipality for having had the audacity and the creativity to convert this magnificent building, in the heart of Saint-Gédéon-de-Beauce, into a multifunctional center which will house a library, a place for the dissemination of culture and communications. This initiative will give the entire population an additional attachment to their community and to their environment, ”said Maka Kotto.
The former convent of the Sisters of Charity of Saint-Louis, acquired by the Municipality in the summer of 2013, will be recycled into a multifunctional center that will house a CLSC, municipal offices and a new library. The latter, affiliated with the BIBLIO Network, will offer the population of Saint-Gédéon-de-Beauce and the surrounding area a place and equipment allowing them access to books and other cultural documents, entertainment activities and services. affecting information technology.
The total cost of the project is estimated at $ 199,000 and is divided between the Department ($ 179,000, or 90% of the total amount), the Corporation de développement de Saint-Gédéon-de-Beauce ($ 5,000) and the Municipality. of Saint-Gédéon-de-Beauce ($ 15,000).
The Department&#39;s contribution comes from the Capital Assistance program and is part of the 2013-2023 Quebec Infrastructure Plan.
