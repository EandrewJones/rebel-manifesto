Quebec - The leader of the official opposition, Stéphane Bédard, confirms that the member for Saint-Jérôme, Pierre Karl Péladeau, will not be able to participate at the start of the work of the National Assembly due to a bicycle accident that occurred this Sunday , while driving on a road in the Estrie region.
“On behalf of all my fellow members of the Parti Québécois, I wish Pierre Karl Péladeau a speedy recovery. We hope to see him soon by our side in the National Assembly but, until then, that he take all the time necessary to recover his strength, ”said Stéphane Bédard.
Pierre Karl Péladeau is currently hospitalized at the Center hospitalier universitaire de Sherbrooke, suffering from several fractures. For the moment, no date of leave is envisaged.
“I would like to tell my colleagues that I was anticipating with great emotion the start of parliamentary work. I wish my fellow members of the Parti Québécois, as well as all the members of the National Assembly, a very good return to the school year. Unfortunately, this bicycle accident will keep me bedridden for a while, ”said Pierre Karl Péladeau.
“That said, I remain very involved in my role as MP for Saint-Jérôme, thanks to daily communication with the people of my riding. I will therefore continue to take good care of their files, as well as those of the parliamentary agenda. Finally, I would like to thank the members of the personnel of the Center hospitalier universitaire de Sherbrooke for the quality of the care given to me as well as for the good humor they spread around them, ”added the deputy.
