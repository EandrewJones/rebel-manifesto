Quebec - The leader of the official opposition, Pierre Karl Péladeau, and the Parti Québécois spokesperson for the status of women, Carole Poirier, applaud the holding of the Women&#39;s Summit and commit to implementing 10 commitments aimed at the parity in politics and within decision-making apparatus.
“Each time we move towards a more egalitarian society, all of Quebec evolves. The reverse is also true. When women&#39;s rights decline, it is our ideal of a more just and freer state, of a society that falls back. The Parti Québécois has already taken several actions to fight discrimination and promote equality. We will continue, as a parliamentary wing and as a political party, to commit ourselves to equality between women and men ”, declared Pierre Karl Péladeau.
“Equality between women and men is not an individual issue, but rather a collective objective for all Quebecers. Solidarity with the feminist movement is a necessity that should be supported by both women and men, ”added Carole Poirier.
