Quebec City - On the occasion of the 100th anniversary of the death of Louis Hémon, the Minister of Culture and Communications, Maka Kotto, made his appointment as a historical figure under the Cultural Heritage Act.
&quot;Emblematic writer, Louis Hémon is the author of the best-selling Quebec novel of his time. Maria Chapdelaine: a story of French Canada, this masterpiece, still popular today, has greatly contributed to the influence and dissemination of our culture internationally. I am proud to honor the memory of this talented writer from Quebec by adoption, ”said Minister Kotto.
Louis Hémon, author and journalist born in France, landed in Quebec in 1911. A year later, he settled in the Lac-Saint-Jean region, where he began writing his novel, recognized as the most important of the local school, Maria Chapdelaine: story of French Canada. The work is inspired by the life of the settlers living around Lac Saint-Jean. Following the author&#39;s death, the novel was published in Montreal and was a great success. This Quebec book has been printed in several million copies, has been the subject of 150 editions in more than 20 languages, has inspired three film adaptations and a play.
Louis Hémon is today designated as a historical figure, because he distinguished himself in Quebec and is considered an essential author of Quebec literature.
Additional information about Louis Hémon as well as all the heritage elements listed in the Cultural Heritage Register are available on the Quebec Cultural Heritage Directory (RPCQ) website, at www.patrimoine-culturel.gouv.qc.ca. This dissemination tool from the Ministry of Culture and Communications makes it possible to discover the richness and diversity of Quebec&#39;s cultural heritage by making it accessible to all.
