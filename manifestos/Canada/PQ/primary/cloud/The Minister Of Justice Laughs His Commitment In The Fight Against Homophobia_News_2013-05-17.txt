Quebec - The Minister of Justice and Minister responsible for the fight against homophobia, Mr. Bertrand St-Arnaud, today marked the International Day against Homophobia, also called, in several European countries, the International Day against homophobia and transphobia.
This thematic day was launched in Quebec in 2003, at the initiative of the Fondation Émergence. Today it is celebrated in nearly sixty countries around the world. The International Day Against Homophobia is a unifying initiative that allows all stakeholders to unite their voices against discrimination based on sexual orientation.
“For eight months, I have been participating in this fight against homophobia as responsible minister. I would like to thank all the organizations that are involved in this fight to put an end to prejudices. I make a point of working with them to build a Quebec that is more open to difference, a Quebec for all, ”said Minister Bertrand St-Arnaud.
Since last September, the government&#39;s commitment has notably resulted in an increase in financial support of more than $ 600,000 to some twenty organizations promoting and defending the rights of persons of sexual minorities and an advertising campaign. national awareness campaign to fight homophobia.
Let us recall, moreover, that the Minister of Justice tabled Bill 35, Law amending the Civil Code in matters of civil status, inheritance and publication of rights last April. This bill proposes to remove certain obstacles that transgender and transgender people face within the government apparatus.
“On this day, I also want to reiterate the government&#39;s full commitment to combat all forms of discrimination based on sexual orientation and gender identity. Bill 35 is another important step in the full and complete recognition of these people, ”concluded the Minister.
