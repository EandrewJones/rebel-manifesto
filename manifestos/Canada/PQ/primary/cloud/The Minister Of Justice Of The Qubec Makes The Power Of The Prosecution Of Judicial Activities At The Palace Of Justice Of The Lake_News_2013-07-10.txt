Quebec - Like the rest of the Quebec population, the authorities of the Quebec Department of Justice were deeply touched by the tragedy that occurred in Lac-Mégantic and wish to express their sincere condolences to those affected.
Since Monday, the Ministry&#39;s teams have been hard at work to ensure the continuation of judicial activities. Indeed, although the building housing the courthouse of Lac-Mégantic, rue Frontenac, was not destroyed, the sector where it is located is still subject to an evacuation notice. Consequently, relocation solutions are being studied so that the hearings on Tuesday July 16, 2013 can take place.
In the event of a relocation, the lawyers concerned will be informed as soon as possible and they will be able to follow up with their clients.
Moreover, knowing that the offices of several lawyers and notaries in Lac-Mégantic and those of legal aid were destroyed during the fire, the Ministry of Justice is currently working with the Barreau du Québec, the Chambre des notaires du Québec. Québec and the legal aid office in order to agree on the support that can be provided to them, in particular for the reconstruction of files. Links have also been made with courthouses, to prevent default judgments from being rendered in the files of the affected offices.
For more information on judicial activities, citizens can contact the usual telephone number of the Lac-Mégantic courthouse: 819 583-1268.
For all information relating to their files, affected citizens, lawyers and notaries are invited to contact the Syndic of the Barreau du Québec at 1 800 361-8495, extension 3438, or the Service du 1 800 notaire of the Chambre des notaires du Québec. Quebec at number 1 800 668-2473.
