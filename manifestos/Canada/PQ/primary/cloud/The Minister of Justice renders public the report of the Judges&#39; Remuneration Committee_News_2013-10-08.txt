Quebec - The Minister of Justice and Attorney General of Quebec, Mr. Bertrand St-Arnaud, today tabled the report of the Judicial Remuneration Committee in the National Assembly.
The Judicial Remuneration Committee, established under the Courts of Justice Act, was set up on April 1, 2013. Chaired by Mr. Michel Clair, it is made up of the Honorable Claire L&#39;Heureux-Dubé, of the Honorable Pierre-A. Michaud, the Honorable André Forget and Madame Julie Gosselin.
The mandate of the Committee was to assess whether the salary, pension plan and other social benefits of judges of the Court of Quebec and presiding justices of the peace are adequate. He was to do the same for the salary, other social benefits and, where applicable, the pension plan of municipal judges.
The Courts of Justice Act provides that the National Assembly may, by reasoned resolution, approve, modify or reject, in whole or in part, the recommendations made by the Committee in its report. This resolution must be adopted no later than the 30th sitting day following the tabling of the report.
The report of the Judicial Remuneration Committee can be consulted on the website of the Ministère de la Justice: www.justice.gouv.qc.ca.
