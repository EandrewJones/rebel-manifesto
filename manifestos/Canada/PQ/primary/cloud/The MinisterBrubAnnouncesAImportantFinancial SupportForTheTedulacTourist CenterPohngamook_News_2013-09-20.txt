Quebec - The Minister responsible for the Bas-Saint-Laurent region and Minister responsible for Tourism, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, announces significant financial support within the framework of phase I of the project to modernize the facilities of the Center tourisme de la tête- du-lac Pohénégamook.
The project of this phase 1, at a cost of $ 1.05 million, consists of the construction of a building at the western entrance to the beach which will serve as a reception station, rest area and services. tourism for the benefit of swimmers and campers. Phase II of the modernization project, which aims to build a new 160-site campground, is planned for a later date.
On the one hand, $ 100,000 is granted under the Strategic Assistance for Tourism Projects component of the Tourism Industry Development Program and $ 20,000 under the Regional Tourism Partnership Agreement of the Bas-Saint-Laurent (EPRT), for which Tourisme Bas-Saint-Laurent contributes $ 9,000, Tourisme Québec $ 9,000 and $ 2,000 comes from the CRÉ du Bas-Saint-Laurent.
“I am proud to support this project, which has received a lot of support from the community. For more than 20 years, the Tête-du-Lac Pohénégamook Tourist Center has undoubtedly been a major attraction for the municipality. It enjoys notoriety and popularity with an average of 18,000 visitors per year. Completion of the project represents added value to consolidate this pole with all the existing attractions including the Lac-Témiscouata National Park. It will make the region more competitive on the tourist scene in Quebec, ”said Minister Bérubé.
Finally, it should be noted that the Tourist Center of Tête-du-Lac Pohénégamook, founded in 1986, is a non-profit organization whose mission is to promote and develop the tourist site located at the head of Lake Pohénégamook, positioned at the northern entrance to the municipality.
