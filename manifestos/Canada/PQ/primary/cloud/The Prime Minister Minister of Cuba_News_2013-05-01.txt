Quebec - The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, underlines the essential contribution of Quebec workers to the development and influence of society.
“When it comes to innovation, creativity and entrepreneurship, Quebeckers are there. At all times, these are people who have put their hearts to work, as the expression goes. May 1st is an opportunity to remind ourselves that no economic, social or cultural development would be possible without everyone&#39;s contribution. We are all links in a large chain, ”said Pauline Marois.
To mark the occasion, and as tradition dictates, the Premier met with the heads of the three major labor federations: the president of the Confederation of National Trade Unions, Jacques Létourneau, the president of the Centrale des unions du Québec. , Louise Chabot, and the president of the Federation of Quebec Workers, Michel Arsenault. They discussed priority issues such as public finances, natural resources, labor-sponsored funds, Bill C 377 as well as the National Employment Insurance Review Commission, set up following the changes. brought to the plan by the federal government.
“The workforce is Quebec&#39;s first resource and by far its most precious. Workers are the guarantors of our prosperity. We must ensure that the rights and interests of all are protected, and find appropriate solutions to the challenges facing us, ”concluded the Premier of Quebec.
