Quebec - The MNA for René-Lévesque, Mr. Marjolain Dufour, is proud to present, on behalf of the Minister of Public Security, Mr. Stéphane Bergeron, the payment of $ 225,000 to the Village of Pointe-aux-Outardes. This amount represents the last payment to which the Village is entitled under the memorandum of understanding signed with the Ministry of Public Security (MSP) for the carrying out of work aimed at preventing and mitigating the risk of erosion of the river coast. Saint-Laurent and for moving primary residences.
“I would like to underline the excellent collaboration of the Village of Pointe-aux-Outardes, which made it possible to carry out this work and to contribute to taking proactive action in civil protection,” declared Minister Bergeron.
“The Village of Pointe-aux-Outardes has to deal with certain natural risks, in particular that of bank erosion. This is why planning and implementing sustainable and efficient solutions are essential to ensure a safer living environment for the community, ”added the MNA for René-Lévesque.
According to the memorandum of understanding, the MSP undertakes to pay financial assistance equal to the eligible costs of the activities referred to in this memorandum, less a financial contribution fixed according to the demographic assessment. An initial amount of $ 1,000,000 was paid to the Village of Pointe-aux-Outardes on April 15, 2010 for the carrying out of work aimed at preventing and mitigating the risk of erosion of the coastline of the St.Lawrence River and for the relocation of main residences.
It should be remembered that this memorandum of understanding is part of the work of the Framework for the Prevention of Main Natural Risks, which was designed to guide government and municipal actions in terms of coastal erosion, landslides and floods. This framework aims to implement integrated risk management and is based on an approach based on risk analysis, analysis of solutions and their implementation.
