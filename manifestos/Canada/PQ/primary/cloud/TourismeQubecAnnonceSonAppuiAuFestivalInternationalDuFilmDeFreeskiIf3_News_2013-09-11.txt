Quebec - The Minister for Tourism, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, is pleased to announce the granting of financial assistance of $ 10,000 to the International Freeski-iF3 Film Festival, which will take place from September 11 to 15, 2013.
For a 7th year, the Festival brings together independent film producers, professional athletes and festival-goers from more than 20 countries. For five days, the event offers an exceptional program including ski film premieres, concerts, remarkable photographs, documentaries, webisodes and many other interactive activities.
The International Freeski Film Festival - iF3 promotes all aspects of this global sport, while producing several editions of its Festival in various countries. It honors and celebrates producers, photographers, international athletes and all those who contribute to the constant advancement of the ski industry and the export of its culture on different levels. This year, the Festival won the Tourisme Montréal award for the best event with a budget of less than $ 300,000.
“Over the years, this sporting celebration has become a must for both thrill seekers and freeski enthusiasts. This event is a tourist attraction that sets Montreal apart internationally as a cultural and creative city. Thanks to this meeting with the seventh art, Quebec is positioning itself as an avant-garde winter destination, ”declared Minister Bérubé.
The financial assistance granted by Tourisme Québec comes from the Tourism Industry Development Program - financial assistance for festivals and tourist events.
