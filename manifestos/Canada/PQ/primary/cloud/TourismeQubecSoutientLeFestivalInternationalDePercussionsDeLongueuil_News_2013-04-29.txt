Quebec - The Minister for Tourism, Mr. Pascal Bérubé, is pleased to announce the allocation of government financial assistance of $ 50,000 to support the holding of the 12th Festival international de percussions de Longueuil, which will take place from 9 to 14 next july.
This cultural event, which occupies a musical niche in the festivals and events circuit, has for mission again this year to make its audience discover rhythms from the world. For the first time in the history of the event, an entire continent will be in the spotlight for the 2013 edition, that of Oceania. Thus, participants will have the opportunity to discover Australia, New Zealand, Bali and many other countries since artists from abroad and also from Quebec will be on the program.
“It is a source of pride to have such a great tourist offer in Quebec. With a variety of festivals and events, each more original than the next, such as the Longueuil International Percussion Festival, residents and visitors alike can enjoy a unique experience that is accessible to all, ”Minister Bérubé said.
The financial assistance granted by Tourisme Québec comes from the Support Program for Tourism Development and Promotion - Financial Assistance for Festivals and Tourism Events component.
By funding festivals and events, the Government of Quebec supports the implementation of initiatives that contribute to the economic development of the Montérégie region. It thus promotes the renewal of the tourist offer with a view to continuous and sustainable improvement.
