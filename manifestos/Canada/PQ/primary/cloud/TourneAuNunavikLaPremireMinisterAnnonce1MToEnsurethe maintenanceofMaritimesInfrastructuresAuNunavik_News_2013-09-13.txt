Kuujjuaq - While in Nunavik on September 12 and 13, the Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, announced financial assistance of $ 1,050,000 over three years to ensure the maintenance of maritime infrastructure in northern villages .
“Maritime service activities are essential. Food supplies by boat are the most economical way to deliver non-perishable food and other essentials to Nunavimmiut. The maintenance of marine infrastructures is essential to preserve the investments of $ 90 million made by the two levels of government, ”she declared.
To ensure the sustainability of these maritime infrastructures, the Premier is pleased to announce a contribution of $ 200,000 for the year 2013, and is committing to the tune of $ 425,000, per year, in 2014 and in 2015 In addition, it promises to continue funding the Quebec government for the years after 2015, provided that the federal government does its part.
To further advance this file, she mandated Alexandre Cloutier, Minister responsible for Canadian Intergovernmental Affairs, the Canadian Francophonie and Sovereignty Governance and Minister responsible for the Nord-du-Québec region, to undertake, in collaboration with the Kativik Regional Government, discussions with the federal government to arrive at a shared and sustainable financial package, as provided for in the Sanarrutik Agreement.
&quot;The participation of the two levels of government is necessary to ensure the financing of our maritime infrastructures, essential to the practice of our traditional activities and to the supply of our communities&quot;, declared the president of the Kativik Regional Government, Maggie Emudluk.
“We have invested $ 45 million for the construction of marine infrastructure and additional sums for maintenance. We will continue to see it, but we hope to come to an agreement with the federal government in order to keep these maritime infrastructures in good condition, ”concluded the Premier of Quebec.
