Quebec - The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, flies to Nunavik where she will make important announcements concerning the development and enhancement of the northern territory. During her stay, Ms. Marois will continue her dialogue on social, economic and community issues in the region, with the main local and regional elected representatives, namely the President of the Kativik Regional Government (KRG), Ms. Maggie Emudluk, and the President of the Makivik Corporation, Mr. Jobie Tukkiapik.
“Our presence in Nunavik will mark the fulfillment of my government&#39;s commitment to make the Aboriginal issue a government priority. A lot of ground has been covered in recent months, and we intend to continue these nation-to-nation exchanges with the Inuit, in order to improve their quality of life. My government is committed to listening to the Inuit and working in partnership, in order to promote the development of the nation and the territory in a harmonious manner. We will work together to secure the future of future generations, ”said the Premier.
For this mission, Ms. Marois will be accompanied by the Minister of Sustainable Development, Environment, Wildlife and Parks, Yves-François Blanchet, as well as the Minister responsible for Canadian Intergovernmental Affairs, the Canadian Francophonie and Sovereignist Governance. , Alexandre Cloutier.
&quot;My visit to Nunavik is intended to be the bearer of decisions and initiatives that will ensure the best progress of the files deemed a priority by the community,&quot; concluded the Premier.
