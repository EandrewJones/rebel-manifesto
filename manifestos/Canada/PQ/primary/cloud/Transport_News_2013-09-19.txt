Longueuil– The Minister of Education, Recreation and Sport and Minister responsible for Montérégie, Mrs. Marie Malavoy, on behalf of the Minister of Transport and Minister of Municipal Affairs, Regions and Land Occupancy, Mr. Sylvain Gaudreault, is pleased to see that the investments of the city of Longueuil and the Agence métropolitaine de transport (AMT) on boulevard Roland-Therrien, in Longueuil, are yielding results.
“Public transit has always been a priority commitment of our government and I made it a priority during my election campaign. In this fall return where travel is at its peak, I am particularly happy to note that not only the new bus service with a reserved lane on Roland-Therrien Boulevard is now a reality, but that it is also efficient and effective. beneficial for all users, ”said Ms. Malavoy.
The new express service on Boulevard Roland-Therrien was put in place on August 26. It is intended to be the first step towards the establishment of a rapid network within the agglomeration. Ultimately, when the project is completed in 2014, the Réseau de transport de Longueuil (RTL) will have 5.2 km of additional reserved lane.
In addition, the AMT is contributing $ 2 million for the construction of reserved lanes on this same corridor. &quot;This concrete measure is in line with the principles of sustainable mobility supported by the government, in particular through the implementation over the next few months of a Québec policy on sustainable mobility,&quot; concluded Mr. Gaudreault.
