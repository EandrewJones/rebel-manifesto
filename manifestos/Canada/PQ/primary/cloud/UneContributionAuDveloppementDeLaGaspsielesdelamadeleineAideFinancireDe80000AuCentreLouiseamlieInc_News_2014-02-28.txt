Sainte-Anne-des-Monts– The Minister for the Regions, Minister responsible for the Gaspésie-Îles-de-la-Madeleine region and Member for the riding of Gaspé, Mr. Gaétan Lelièvre, announces, as part of the Fund for assistance for regional initiatives, financial assistance of $ 80,000 to the Center Louise-Amélie inc. for carrying out renovation work aimed at improving the quality of the services offered to the people it accommodates.
This help and shelter for women victims of violence and their children is no longer sufficient on demand, because it has displayed an occupancy rate of over 100% for more than 10 years. The project therefore aims to carry out renovation work that will better accommodate customers, who find themselves in a vulnerable situation. Thus, three rooms, including one for people with reduced mobility, will be added, the common room reserved for adolescents will be enlarged, functional spaces for caregivers will be set up and a room for the storage of the personal effects of the people accommodated will be set up.
“Our government is committed to providing adequate services to all Quebecers. It is therefore with pride that we support this structuring project that will meet the basic needs of women and children in the region who find themselves in a situation of distress while offering them the support and comfort necessary to get through this. trying period in their lives, ”said Minister Lelièvre.
The Regional Initiatives Assistance Fund is part of the support for the government intervention strategy for the development of the Gaspésie-Îles-de-la-Madeleine region and is a major lever to promote, in particular, the achievement of projects stemming from the region&#39;s development priorities. A maximum annual envelope of $ 6 million is provided for this fund for a period of five years. The program is in effect until March 31, 2018.
