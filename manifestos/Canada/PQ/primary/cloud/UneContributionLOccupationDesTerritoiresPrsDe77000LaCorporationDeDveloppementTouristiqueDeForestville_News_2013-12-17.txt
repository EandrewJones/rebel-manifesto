Quebec - The MNA for René-Lévesque and responsible for the Côte-Nord region, Mr. Marjolain Dufour, on behalf of the Minister of Municipal Affairs, Regions and Land Occupancy and Minister of Transport, Mr. Sylvain Gaudreault, and from the Minister for Tourism, Pascal Bérubé, announces financial assistance of $ 76,757 to the Forestville Tourism Development Corporation for the purchase of a groomer used for the maintenance of cross-country ski trails.
The Plein d&#39;Air club is a cross-country ski center located in Forestville serving the territory of the MRC de La Haute-Côte-Nord. He participates in the organization of the Boréal Loppet, an international competition which is the flagship winter tourist attraction of the entire region.
The acquisition of a snow groomer could reduce the working time of volunteers and significantly improve the quality of the slopes.
“It is necessary for the sustainability of the Plein d&#39;Air club and the Boréal Loppet to acquire a maintenance vehicle specially designed for cross-country skiing, comparable to what is used in other ski centers. That is why my government is providing financial support for this promising project for the Côte-Nord region, ”said MP Dufour.
Financial assistance comes from the Economic Development Fund of the Ministry of Municipal Affairs, Regions and Land Occupancy ($ 46,757) and the 2012-2015 Manicouagan Regional Tourism Partnership Agreement of the Ministry of Tourism ( $ 30,000).
