Montreal– Richard Langlais, a businessman firmly rooted in the Verdun community, will wear the colors of the Parti Québécois for the by-elections in Verdun, the poll of which will take place on December 5th.
“I have been involved in the Verdun community for over 20 years. By becoming the representative in the National Assembly of Quebec, it is part of the continuity of my commitments to the residents of the constituency. I look forward to the next few weeks to meet people in the field and discuss the development of our sector with them. Declared Richard Langlais, Parti Québécois candidate.
“Richard is a local entrepreneur well known to the entire Verdun community. It is by presenting candidates who are firmly rooted in the local reality of the counties that the Parti Québécois demonstrates its ability to listen to the concerns of all regions of Quebec and of the population. I am convinced that Richard will be an invaluable Member of Parliament for the citizens of the entire constituency, ”added Jean-François Lisée, leader of the Parti Québécois.
Throughout the campaign, Mr. Langlais will be on the ground to meet Verdunites and present his commitments to health, education, housing and public services. Voters will be able to see it at door-to-door, public events or thematic meetings at the electoral premises.
