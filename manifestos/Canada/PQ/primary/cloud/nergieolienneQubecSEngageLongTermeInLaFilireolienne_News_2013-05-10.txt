Matane– The Premier of Quebec, Pauline Marois, accompanied by the Minister of Natural Resources, Martine Ouellet, the Minister for the Regions and Minister responsible for the Gaspésie-Îles-de-la-Madeleine region, Gaétan Lelièvre, and the Minister responsible for Tourism and Minister responsible for the Bas-Saint-Laurent region, Pascal Bérubé, announces that the government is continuing to develop the wind power sector in Quebec with the allocation of 800 MW for new projects.
“The government believes in the future of the wind power industry in Quebec. This block will generate nearly $ 2 billion in investments. It will also help maintain several hundred manufacturing jobs in Gaspésie-Îles-de-la-Madeleine and Bas-Saint-Laurent, for several years, ”said the Premier.
This 800 MW block is divided into four components: a first 150 MW for a project of the Mi&#39;gmawei Mawiomi group, i.e. the three communities of Listuguj, Gesgapegiag and Gespeg, a 300 MW block awarded by call for tenders for projects in the Gaspé and Bas-Saint-Laurent regions, 200 MW for Hydro-Québec Production and, finally, 150 MW allocated by call for tenders for projects throughout Québec. The calls for tenders will target projects initiated by local communities or cooperatives, in partnership with private developers.
