Montreal - The leader of the Parti Québécois, Pauline Marois, has formalized the candidacy of Frédéric St-Jean for the partial election in the riding of Lafontaine. "Frederic is a brilliant young man who is part of the succession in politics. Integrates and honest, Frédéric will bring the proposals and positions of the Parti Québécois with dynamism and franchise during this election. » 
"If we are today in partial election, it is because the Liberal member of the riding has been caught in a series of scandals. In particular, favoritism was spoken in the granting of day care permits and the use of the credit card of a private enterprise when it operated. Seeing the lack of consideration for the ethics and democracy of Jean Charest's party, the Liberal member's behavior should not remain unpunished. I will use the forum that is offered to remind the ethical drifts of the Liberals. Corruption must stop, "said Frédéric St-Jean. 
"The patronage, the collusion, the disturbing links with the oil industry, the shale gas, the daycare scandal, the health tax, the traveler island, the Turcot exchanger, the CHUM, the dinners with members of the crime Organized, the Liberal organizers arrested as part of the UPAC survey, the examples multiply. This government has a delinquent behavior and its attitude in the file of tuition fees led Quebec in a real social crisis. You have to put an end to all that. It lasted enough and I introduce myself in Lafontaine to help reduce integrity into politics, "he added. 
Frédéric St-Jean, who is Technical Advisor to Quebec's Housing Corporation, explained that only the Parti Québécois proposes a democratic reform that will ensure that Québec's political manners in particular by lowering the limit of donations to political parties at 100 dollars and creating fixed date elections. 
Remember that the Parti Québécois is the greatest political force with its 90,000 members.
