Shawinigan - Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, accompanied by the Minister Delegate to Industrial Policy and the Quebec Economic Development Bank, Élaine Zakaïb, today announced the creation, in the long term, of a hundred Jobs in the company Addenergy of Shawinigan. 
This project, which represents an investment of nearly $ 21 million over five years, will benefit from a $ 5 million loan from the Quebec government for the implementation of a charging station assembly plant for electric vehicles. 
"Lastracy of transport electrification, which is part of our economic, is one of the major initiatives that my government has launched to create jobs and support the economy. We want to quickly make a larger place to electric vehicles, but also make Quebec a model to follow in the field of transport electrification. The products and softwares offered by addenergy are innovative and a remarkable quality, exactly the type of success Quebec that must be promoted around the world, "said Pauline Marois. 
With this new factory, addenergy wants to integrate in the same place all the assembly operations of its terminals as well as the management of its inventories. Thus, the company will improve its ability to meet the needs of its new customers, in order to pierce the North American and global markets, and contribute to the increase in our exports. 
"Today's announcement sends a strong signal about the future of the electricity industry in Quebec. With its factory project in Shawinigan, addENERGY creates employment in the region and well demonstrates the ability of Quebec entrepreneurs to embark on plans for the future. With the financial support of our government, addenergy will strengthen its position as a Quebec leader in the manufacture of refill terminals in a full effervescence market, "says Zakaïb underlined.
