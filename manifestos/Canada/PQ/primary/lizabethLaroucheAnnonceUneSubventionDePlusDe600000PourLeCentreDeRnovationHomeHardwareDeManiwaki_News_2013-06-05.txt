"This investment should consolidate nearly about twenty jobs in the Algonquin Kitigan Zibi community, in addition to making the business more competitive in its niche and boosting regional economic development," said the minister. LAROUCH. 
Thanks to this grant, the owner of the renovation center will bring a series of significant improvements to his business. In addition to updating its truck fleet and its computer network, it will build a garden space to offer new products taken by the clientele. In addition, the entire store display will be reviewed to equip its product line with better visibility. 
"The demand for hardware and building materials is constantly growing. It comes from indigenous communities, but also allocheted residents of neighboring regions. I hope that this investment will respond to demand and can have a leverage in the field of building construction, "said the minister. 
The financial assistance granted to the renovation center comes from the Aboriginal Aboriginal Affairs Fund. The objective of this fund is to support projects that can have significant benefits on Quebec's Aboriginal Nations Economic, Social or Community Development.
