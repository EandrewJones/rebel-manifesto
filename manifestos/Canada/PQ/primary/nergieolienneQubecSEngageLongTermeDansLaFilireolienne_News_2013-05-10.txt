Matane-Prime Minister of Quebec, Pauline Marois, accompanied by the Minister of Natural Resources, Martine Ouellet, Minister Delegate to the Regions and Minister responsible for the Gaspé-Islands-de-la-Madeleine Region, Gaétan Lelièvre, and the Minister Delegate to Tourism and Minister responsible for the Bas-Saint-Laurent Region, Pascal Bérubé, announces that the government is pursuing the development of the wind industry in Quebec with the allocation of 800 MW for new projects. 
"The government believes in the future of the wind industry in Quebec. This block will generate nearly $ 2 billion in investments. It will also maintain several hundred manufacturing jobs in Gaspésie-Îles-de-la-Madeleine and Bas-Saint-Laurent, for several years, "said the Prime Minister. 
This 800 MW block is divided into four components: a first 150 MW for a Mi'gmawei Mawiomi group, the three communities of Listuguj, Gesgapegiag and Gespeg, a block of 300 MW granted by call for tenders for projects for projects. In the areas of the Gaspésie and Bas-Saint-Laurent, 200 MW for Hydro-Québec Production and, finally, 150 MW awarded by tender for projects throughout Quebec. Calls for tenders will target projects initiated by local communities or cooperatives, in partnership with private developers.
