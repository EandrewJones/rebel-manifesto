Quebec- The Minister of Health and Social Services, Minister responsible for Elders and Minister responsible for the Estrie Region, Mr. Réjean Hébert, on behalf of the Minister of Natural Resources, Mrs. Martine Ouellet, announces the governmental authorization For the completion of the Community Wind Project granite in the Estrie region. 
With a total power of 24.6 MW, this wind farm will have 12 wind turbines with a power of 2.05 MW each and provide clean energy to 3,600 homes in the region. The development of this park, estimated at $ 60 million, should generate 70 jobs in the construction phase and will be commissioned in December 2014. 
"As Minister responsible for the Estrie Region, I am delighted by the project announced today. While respecting the environment and the quality of life of the residents, this project will contribute to boosting the economic development of the Granite MRC, which was greatly affected by the tragedy of July 6th. This project is all the more prominer for the region that the majority of municipalities that this MRC are stakeholders, "said Rejean Hébert. 
The Community Wind Project Granite comes from the third call for tenders of 500 MW of wind energy reserved for community or Aboriginal projects launched in 2009 by Hydro-Québec Distribution. This third call for tenders provides that, of the $ 60 million linked to the project, a minimum of $ 36 million will have to be invested in Quebec, including $ 12.6 million in Gaspésie and Matanie RCM. 
"Wind energy in Quebec can consolidate nearly 2,000 jobs in the manufacturing sector, especially in the Bas-Saint-Laurent region and Gaspésie," Martine Ouellet said.
