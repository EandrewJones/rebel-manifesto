Five members of the Saraguro people arrive before the National Court of Justice in the city of Quito to the cassation audience that will be held this August 7, 2018 at 2:00 p.m. 
This audience is part of the last judicial instance, according to Karina Monteros, one of the criminalized. 
Luisa Lozano, women's leader of the Confederation of Indigenous Nationalities of Ecuador, another of the criminalized, affirms that the only crime weighing on the 5 of the 29 of Saraguro is having demanded respect for collective rights.
