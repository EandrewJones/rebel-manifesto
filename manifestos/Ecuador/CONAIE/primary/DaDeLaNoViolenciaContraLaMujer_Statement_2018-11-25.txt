Quito, November 25, 2018  37 years ago at the first Latin American and Caribbean feminist encounter, held in Bogotá-Colombia, declared on November 25 as the day of nonviolence against women, which was ratified by the UN General Assembly in 1999. 
This date was chosen in tribute to the three Sisters Mirabal of the Dominican Republic, tortured and cruelly murdered by the Trujillo dictatorship in 1960, a fact that the wrath of the people and months later, the dictatorship was overthrown that for 30 years, this Central American country was overthrown. 
The social rights and demands have acquired them through the social struggle through history, remember Rosa Uquillas, Lidia Herrera, the "Black" Julia on the days of November 15, 1922, the Hiking of Dolores Cacuango to Carondelet to demand Claims for the Indigenous Workers Sector, Amaguaña Traffic and its clandestine schools for peasant indigenous children. 
History demonstrates the fundamental role of women in social struggles as well as the historical need to continue along the path of organization and mobilization for a better world for all. 
The Confederation of Indigenous Nationalities of Ecuador -Boneies joins all and all those who fight against the multiple forms of violence and oppression exercised against historical women and systematically, indigenous women, join the struggle of women Montubias, Afro Ecuadorianas , mestizas and peasants in this historic struggle. 
For all women violated by extractivism, by all women violated on August 17 in Saraguro, for all that are now unfairly judicialized to defend their territories, for administering indigenous justice, by all women victims of femicides and disappearances, by All women victims of sexual violence, by all mothers and wives victims of intrafamily violence, by all women victims of exclusion and invisibilization in labor, organizational, educational spaces, together we will continue in the fight. 
By the Governing Council   Jaime Vargas Luisa Lozano  President Conaie Female leader  .
